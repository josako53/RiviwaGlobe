### Scenario 20-1: Resident Reports Widening Structural Cracks and Pooling Water — Complaint Logged as Routine Maintenance (Pattern B+D)
**Real Basis:** Champlain Towers South (Surfside, Florida) collapse, June 24, 2021; 2018 Morabito structural engineering report; 98 deaths; $1.2 billion settlement approved June 2022. NPR, PBS NewsHour, CBS Miami, Insurance Journal.
**The Problem:** A resident of a 12-story beachfront condominium has a copy of an engineering report circulated to building management three years ago flagging major structural damage to the concrete slab below the pool deck. This morning she has observed new water infiltration pooling in the underground garage and can see the cracks documented in the report appear visibly wider. She has attended building meetings at which repairs were deferred twice due to cost. She is now calling the property management company's complaint line.
**What staff/org would do:** The call handler tells her this is a "known maintenance item already in the work queue" and that a contractor will be scheduled "in the coming months." The call is logged as a general maintenance request. No supervisor is alerted, no structural engineer is contacted, and she is told not to worry because the building passed its last inspection.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately recognises this as a potential life-safety emergency rather than a maintenance request. Flags the call for real-time escalation to building management and the local building authority. Confirms the resident's right to demand an emergency structural inspection.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true
- Opening reply: "What you are describing — widening cracks in structural concrete combined with new water infiltration in the garage — is a situation that must be treated as a potential structural emergency, not a maintenance queue item. I want to help you get an immediate response today."
- Who to direct to: Local municipal or county building official (emergency inspection request, same day); local Fire and Rescue Service (independent powers to condemn unsafe structures and order evacuation); state department of buildings or equivalent; if the building official is unresponsive, escalate to the county emergency management office. In Florida, also the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR) building codes enforcement.
- Evidence to collect: Photograph and date-stamp all visible cracks with a ruler for scale; make a video of the pooling water with timestamp; retrieve and preserve the engineering report previously shared by management; note the full name, date, and time of the call handler who dismissed the concern; obtain the maintenance ticket reference number.
- Feedback category tag: Structural Safety Emergency / Construction Defect / Complaint Dismissal / Urgent

### Scenario 20-2: Disabled Veteran Loses $221,000 Retirement Savings to ADU Contractor — State Hid 259 Complaints (Pattern A+E)
**Real Basis:** Anchored Tiny Homes, Sacramento, California. 450+ homeowners affected; $12.8 million in liabilities; Chapter 7 bankruptcy filed July 2024; CSLB license revoked December 2024; 10,000+ complaints hidden from public over five years per NBC Bay Area investigation. NBC Bay Area, ABC7 San Francisco, California CSLB enforcement records.
**The Problem:** A disabled Army veteran paid $221,000 — his entire retirement savings — to an ADU builder for a backyard unit that was never constructed. He signed the contract in January 2024 after checking the contractor's California Contractors State License Board (CSLB) license page and seeing no complaints. The contractor never pulled a permit, stopped returning calls, and filed Chapter 7 bankruptcy in July 2024. The veteran has now discovered that three complaints about this contractor were filed with the CSLB in December 2023 — the same month he signed — but those complaints were hidden from the public license lookup tool because the contractor settled them individually with the complainants. He is calling the CSLB consumer hotline to understand what recourse exists.
**What staff/org would do:** The CSLB call handler confirms the license was suspended in July 2024 and revoked in December 2024. When asked why December 2023 complaints were not visible when he checked before signing, the agent explains complaints are removed from public record when a contractor settles individually with consumers. The handler offers to mail a complaint form. No mention is made of the contractor's surety bond, any restitution fund, or legal referral services.
**What the Owner does:** Validates the veteran's experience as a documented systemic failure, explains the CSLB complaint-suppression mechanism clearly, and connects him to every available financial recovery pathway.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (financial harm already sustained; no ongoing physical danger)
- Opening reply: "I am very sorry this happened to you. Losing your entire retirement savings to a contractor who never broke ground — and then discovering that warnings about this company were hidden from you by the state's own licensing system — is a serious injustice. There are still meaningful steps you can take toward recovery, and I want to walk you through them."
- Who to direct to: California CSLB — file a claim against the contractor's mandatory surety bond (contractors are required to carry a $25,000 bond; file immediately as bond claims have deadlines); CSLB Arbitration Program; California Department of Consumer Affairs; California Attorney General consumer protection complaint line; State Bar of California Lawyer Referral Service (many consumer fraud attorneys take construction fraud cases on contingency); the Chapter 7 bankruptcy trustee (file a proof of claim as a creditor in the bankruptcy proceeding).
- Evidence to collect: Original signed contract; all payment receipts and bank wire/transfer records; all communications with the contractor (texts, emails, voicemails); screenshot of the CSLB license page as it appeared before and after suspension; results of any permit search showing no permits pulled; the bankruptcy case number and court filing; any marketing materials or advertisements.
- Feedback category tag: Financial Fraud / Abandoned Project / Regulatory Complaint Suppression / Vulnerability

### Scenario 20-3: Homeowner Calls "Local Emergency Plumber" — Reaches Overseas Call Center, No One Arrives, Card Charged (Pattern E+A)
**Real Basis:** Premium Home Service (B.E.S.T. GDR LLC), Chicago, Illinois. 15,000+ fake local business listings; $79 million+ in suspicious profit; calls routed to Philippines call center; fabricated five-star reviews including stolen profile photos. FTC/DOJ lawsuit filed May 11, 2026. FTC press release, DOJ press release, Chicago Sun-Times.
**The Problem:** A homeowner searched online for an emergency plumber and clicked the top local result — showing a Chicago address, hundreds of reviews, and a 4.8-star rating. She called, spoke to a friendly agent who confirmed a plumber would arrive within two hours, and gave her credit card number to "hold" the appointment. Four hours later, no one arrived. Three call-back attempts each result in a new agent placing her on hold for 20 minutes before disconnecting. On the third call she is told the technician is "running behind" and will arrive within the hour. She has now discovered the listing appears to be a fake and the "reviews" may have been fabricated.
**What staff/org would do:** Each call-back connects to a different overseas agent who gives a new false arrival window, asks her to confirm her card details for a "service confirmation," and then either places her in a hold queue or disconnects. When she disputes the charge with her bank, the merchant listed is a generic LLC with a Philippine address and no phone number she can trace.
**What the Owner does:** Recognises this as a documented national fraud scheme confirmed by FTC and DOJ enforcement action; helps the homeowner stop further financial harm immediately and file complaints in the right places.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (financial harm and deception; the original plumbing problem may still need a legitimate local plumber)
- Opening reply: "What you have described — a 'local' business showing a fake Chicago address that routes calls to an overseas call center with no actual local technicians — matches a major documented fraud scheme that the FTC and US Department of Justice filed suit against in May 2026. You are not alone. Here is what to do right now."
- Who to direct to: Contact your bank or card issuer immediately to dispute the charge and block the merchant (act within 24 hours to maximise dispute success); FTC ReportFraud.ftc.gov (file a consumer complaint — your report feeds into the active enforcement case); Illinois Attorney General consumer fraud hotline (this scheme includes state-level Illinois charges); BBB Scam Tracker (to warn other consumers); Google Maps and Yelp (both have fake-business reporting portals — flag the listing).
- Evidence to collect: Screenshots of the listing exactly as it appeared (name, address, star rating, review count, photos); recordings or notes of every call (date, time, agent name or ID, what was said); bank or card statement showing the charge; any email or text confirmation of the appointment; the merchant name and address as it appears on the card statement.
- Feedback category tag: Call Center Fraud / Fake Business Listing / Financial Overcharge / Abandoned Service

### Scenario 20-4: Earthquake Survivor Seeks Accountability for Hotel Built on River Sand and Illegal Floors — Public Officials Shielded (Pattern B+E+D)
**Real Basis:** Isias Grand Hotel, Adiyaman, Turkey. February 6, 2023 earthquake (7.8 magnitude); 25 hotel deaths; 150-page technical report confirming river-sand concrete, unauthorized 2016 floor addition, 2018 government amnesty bypass; owner and architect sentenced to 18 years each in 2024. Up to 75,000 buildings covered by amnesty schemes. Al Jazeera, CNN, The Dial investigative journalism, The National News.
**The Problem:** A teacher who survived the collapse of the Isias Grand Hotel — where she had been attending a chess tournament — is filing a formal complaint with the regional disaster authority. She has read the 150-page technical investigation confirming the hotel was built with river sand rather than proper aggregate concrete, had an unauthorized floor added in 2016 without a permit, and was covered under a 2018 government amnesty scheme that bypassed safety recertification requirements. Criminal convictions of the hotel owner and architect have occurred. She now wants to know why the officials who approved the amnesty scheme and renewed the building's certificates are not being prosecuted, and what compensation is available for her physical injuries and psychological trauma.
**What staff/org would do:** The regional authority agent refers her to the criminal court process already underway for the hotel owner and says compensation is "a civil litigation matter." When she asks specifically about the officials who approved the amnesty and the permits, the agent says those individuals are "the subject of a separate government review" with no further information. No victim support referral is offered. The call ends without any escalation.
**What the Owner does:** Explains the documented legal landscape — criminal convictions have occurred but the accountability gap for public officials is confirmed by investigators — and directs the survivor to victim compensation channels and independent legal advocacy.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (injuries already sustained; survivor's immediate physical safety not currently at risk)
- Opening reply: "I am deeply sorry for what you survived and for the loss of those who did not. The failures that killed people in that hotel — substandard concrete, an illegal extra floor, and a government amnesty scheme that allowed a dangerous building to remain certified — have been confirmed by a detailed technical investigation. You have every right to seek accountability and compensation, and there are pathways beyond the regional authority."
- Who to direct to: Turkish Disaster and Emergency Management Authority (AFAD) victim support office; Turkish Bar Association (Istanbul or Gaziantep branch) for legal representation on civil compensation claims; Hakikat Adalet Hafıza Merkezi (Truth Justice Memory Center, Turkey) for survivor documentation and advocacy support; European Court of Human Rights (longer-term route if domestic accountability for public officials is blocked, as documented by The New Arab and investigative journalism).
- Evidence to collect: Medical records of all injuries; documentation of attendance at the chess tournament (booking confirmation, registration records, any certificate or invitation); photographs or video taken at or near the collapse; any publicly available building license or amnesty certificate issued to the hotel; names of the approving officials as reported in published technical reports or media.
- Feedback category tag: Building Collapse / Construction Defect / Corruption / Staff Misconduct / Complaint Ignored by Authority

### Scenario 20-5: Resident Discovers Her Tower Has ACM Cladding — Landlord Has Given No EWS1 Answer in 14 Months (Pattern B+D+E)
**Real Basis:** Grenfell Tower fire, North Kensington, London, June 14, 2017. 72 deaths. Grenfell Tower Inquiry Phase 2 Report (September 2024): Arconic "deliberately concealed" fire test results showing ACM cladding "burned violently." Criminal referrals: 57 individuals and 20 organisations identified as suspects, May 2026. NBC News, CBS News, GOV.UK inquiry response, Wikipedia.
**The Problem:** A resident of a social housing tower in Manchester has been told by a fire safety surveyor that her building's external cladding is ACM (aluminium composite material) — the same type installed at Grenfell Tower. The housing association told her 14 months ago that "a review is ongoing." She has now read the Phase 2 Grenfell Inquiry finding that Arconic, the cladding manufacturer, deliberately hid fire test results showing the material "burned violently and performed poorly." She has asked the housing association whether the specific Arconic product data for her building's cladding was ever disclosed to them, and whether an EWS1 (External Wall System) assessment form has been completed. No substantive answer has been received.
**What staff/org would do:** The housing association's complaints handler says confirming which cladding product was installed and whether fire test data was disclosed is "not something we can confirm in this call" and offers to "pass the query to the building compliance team." No timeline is given. The agent cannot confirm whether an EWS1 form exists. No escalation or safety advisory is offered.
**What the Owner does:** Recognises this as a life-safety transparency failure consistent with the documented pattern of manufacturer information suppression confirmed by the Grenfell Inquiry. Directs the resident to statutory rights and the post-Grenfell regulatory framework.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true
- Opening reply: "Your concern is entirely well-founded. The Grenfell Tower Inquiry's Phase 2 Report, published in September 2024, confirmed that Arconic deliberately hid fire test results showing their ACM cladding burned violently. If your building has ACM cladding and no EWS1 assessment has been completed or disclosed after 14 months, you have a right to demand immediate answers — and to escalate formally to statutory bodies that have powers to compel action."
- Who to direct to: UK Building Safety Regulator (HSE) — the statutory body created post-Grenfell with enforcement powers over high-rise residential buildings; Housing Ombudsman (file directly at housing-ombudsman.org.uk — no MP referral required since 2022; applicable if 8-week internal complaint period has elapsed or complaint has been falsely handled); local Fire and Rescue Service (independent inspection powers and ability to serve enforcement notices); Leasehold Advisory Service (LEASE) for rights regarding cladding remediation and cost obligations; End Our Cladding Scandal campaign for peer support and advocacy.
- Evidence to collect: Written copy of the surveyor's report identifying the cladding type; all written communications with the housing association, especially the initial 14-month-old response and any subsequent acknowledgements; any EWS1 form issued or explicitly refused in writing; a copy of the building's fire risk assessment (residents have a statutory right to request this); any Arconic product specification or batch number found on building documents.
- Feedback category tag: Transparency Failure / Information Suppression / Construction Defect / Fire Safety / Urgent

### Scenario 20-6: Single Mother Reports Damp and Mould for 18 Months — Landlord Closes Complaint Without Inspection or Repair (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** UK Housing Ombudsman Annual Complaints Review 2024-25. 7,082 determinations (30% year-on-year increase); 71% upheld rate; 43% increase in property-condition complaints; 504 severe maladministration findings in property condition; 113 severe maladministration findings specifically in complaint-handling conduct; £5.4 million compensation ordered; 474% increase in repair complaints vs. pre-pandemic levels.
**The Problem:** A single mother with two young children has reported damp and extensive black mould covering the bedroom walls of her social housing flat to her housing association 18 months ago. She has called six times. Each call produces a new reference number and a promise that a repair team will be in touch. No inspection has ever occurred. Her youngest child, aged three, has been diagnosed with a respiratory condition that her GP has linked in writing to the mould exposure. Last week she received a letter from the housing association stating her complaint has been "resolved and closed" — she had never been contacted about any resolution, and nothing has been repaired.

The agent cannot say who conducted the review, when it occurred, or what it found. There is no record of any physical inspection in the system. The agent offers to schedule another repair team "within 28 working days."
**What the Owner does:** Recognises that falsely closing a complaint without inspection or repair is exactly the conduct the Housing Ombudsman's 2024-25 report found to constitute severe maladministration in 113 separate cases. Moves the tenant directly to the external escalation pathway.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (serious and ongoing health harm; no immediate life-threatening danger)
- Opening reply: "I want you to know that closing your complaint without any inspection or repair is exactly what the UK Housing Ombudsman described as 'severe maladministration' in 113 separate findings in their 2024-25 annual review — and your GP's written letter linking your child's respiratory diagnosis to the mould is one of the most important pieces of evidence you can have. You can escalate this externally right now."
- Who to direct to: UK Housing Ombudsman — file directly at housing-ombudsman.org.uk (no MP referral required; applicable because the internal complaint was falsely closed without resolution); Shelter England (free legal advice on disrepair claims and health impacts); local council environmental health team (councils have enforcement powers under the Housing Health and Safety Rating System — a category 1 hazard finding can compel the housing association to act); Regulator of Social Housing (if the landlord is a registered social housing provider, systemic complaint-handling failures can be referred directly).
