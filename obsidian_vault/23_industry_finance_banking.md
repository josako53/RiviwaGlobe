## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Finance / Banking & Financial Services

### Digital Banking & Mobile Money
- Please introduce a transaction reversal feature on mobile money — one wrong digit ruins everything
- The mobile banking app should support face recognition or fingerprint login — PIN entry is slow
- Add an option to set daily transfer limits on my mobile app so I can control my own security
- Please make your USSD banking service work without an internet connection — it times out frequently
- A spending analytics feature in the app showing monthly category breakdown would be very useful
- A feature to schedule recurring payments (rent, DSTV, utility bills) from within the mobile app would save time
- Please add support for multiple languages including Swahili in the mobile banking app
- The mobile money app should notify me when an agent near me has float available — it wastes time going to closed agents

### Loan Products
- Please design loan repayment schedules aligned to income cycles — weekly earners cannot make monthly repayments
- A credit score transparency tool showing me my BOT CRB score and how to improve it would empower customers
- Offer group guarantee loans for small traders who have no land title for collateral
- Reduce the loan processing time — six weeks for a small business loan is too slow for trading opportunities
- Please offer a grace period on loan repayments for borrowers affected by seasonal income drops

### Branch & Service Improvements
- Please extend branch operating hours to include Saturday afternoon — working people cannot visit during the week
- A virtual queue system (take a number, wait anywhere) would reduce the frustration of long queues at branches
- Please train tellers on system navigation — customers should not wait 20 minutes while a teller calls for help
- An online appointment booking system for relationship managers would save customers wasted journeys
- More ATMs in peri-urban areas would reduce the burden on agents who frequently run out of float

### Insurance
- Please send annual policy renewal reminders at least 60 days in advance — not one week before expiry
- Offer micro-insurance products for low-income earners with weekly premium payment options
- A digital claims submission portal with real-time status tracking would reduce unnecessary branch visits
- Provide a summary of policy exclusions in plain Swahili so customers truly understand what is not covered

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A customer reports unauthorized transactions on their account — suspected account takeover or card skimming fraud
2. A mobile money agent collected a customer's cash and has become uncontactable — suspected fraudulent agent
3. A customer's pension contributions (NSSF/PPF/GEPF) have been deducted by their employer but never remitted to the fund over multiple years — wage theft at scale
4. A customer reports that a bank or insurance broker disappeared with their premium payments without issuing a valid policy
5. A customer's account was frozen with life-saving funds (medical emergency, school fees deadline) and no emergency access was provided
6. A customer reports a confirmed identity theft — their KYC details were used to open accounts or take loans without their knowledge
7. A forex bureau is confirmed to be distributing counterfeit currency notes to customers
8. An insurance claim involving a terminal illness or death of a policyholder has been pending for more than six months without payment
9. A loan app is making unauthorized deductions from a customer's mobile money wallet beyond the agreed repayment amount — possible illegal over-collection
10. A bank teller or employee is suspected of internal fraud — unauthorized access to customer accounts, falsifying records
11. A customer reports that a loan recovery agent is using threats, physical intimidation, or harassment to collect a debt
12. A customer's SWIFT transfer carrying business-critical funds has been missing for more than 10 working days with no trace
13. Multiple customers in the same area report the same agent banking operator collected deposits that were never posted — pattern of agent fraud
14. A pension fund or SACCO is suspected of misappropriating member savings — systemic financial misconduct

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Finance/Banking vs. Mobile Telecom**: Mobile money (M-Pesa, Airtel Money, Tigo Pesa, HaloPesa) complaints about transaction failures, wrong amounts, or agent conduct belong to finance/banking KB — even though they operate on telecom networks. If the complaint is about voice call or data service on the same telecom network, route to telecoms KB. The discriminating signal is whether money movement is involved.
- **Finance vs. Government Services**: NSSF, GEPF, PPF, and NHIF are government-backed funds. Complaints about contribution records, benefit delays, or policy administration belong in finance/banking KB. Complaints about the government institution itself as an employer or regulator belong to government services KB.
- **Insurance vs. Healthcare**: NHIF complaints about premium deductions, coverage claims, or rejected treatment reimbursements stay in finance/banking KB. Complaints about the quality of medical treatment received at a hospital — even if NHIF-covered — belong to healthcare KB.
- **Banking vs. Real Estate**: Home mortgage products sit in finance/banking KB when the complaint is about loan terms, disbursement, or repayment. If the complaint shifts to the property itself (developer delays, title deed issues, construction defects), route to real estate KB.
- **Agent Banking vs. Agent Telecom**: An agent banking operator working under a bank's network who collects deposits belongs to finance KB. An agent working as a mobile money distributor (float dealer) also belongs to finance KB. An agent selling SIM cards or data bundles belongs to telecoms KB. The key signal is whether the transaction is financial (money movement, account creation) or communications-related.
