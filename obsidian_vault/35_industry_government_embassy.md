## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Embassy / Diplomatic Services / Immigration

### Digital Services & Self-Service
- Please introduce an online application status tracker so applicants can monitor progress without calling
- A clear checklist of all required documents for each visa type should be published and kept up to date
- Electronic appointment booking with confirmation SMS would eliminate the confusion around walk-in queues
- Consider offering biometric enrollment at multiple locations — not only the capital city consulate
- A live chat support feature on the embassy website would help applicants with common questions
- Introduce automated email updates at every stage of the application — submitted, under review, approved, dispatched
- Make document uploads possible via the portal to avoid physical submission for non-sensitive items
- An online fee payment verification system would prevent disputes at the counter about paid amounts

### Processing Efficiency
- Please publish and enforce clear processing time standards for each visa and permit category
- Assign a dedicated case worker to complex permit cases so applicants have a named point of contact
- Introduce a fast-track option for documented emergencies such as medical travel or bereavement
- Ensure the document checklist is complete at first submission to avoid back-and-forth rejections
- A pre-submission online check tool would help applicants verify their documents before wasting fees
- Saturday morning sessions for document submission would reduce weekday crowd pressure
- Consider delegating biometric enrollment to approved partner centers to reduce embassy congestion

### Communication & Transparency
- Please publish visa refusal reasons — even a general code — so applicants understand what to correct
- Regular public advisories about processing delays would help applicants plan their travel realistically
- Update the embassy website monthly to ensure fee tables and document requirements are current
- A frequently asked questions section in Swahili would greatly help Tanzanian applicants
- Communicate proactively when the system goes down so applicants do not travel unnecessarily

### Border Operations
- Please train border officers in customer service — applicants should not be treated as suspects by default
- Clear signage in Swahili and English at border crossing points would improve the flow significantly
- Digital queue management at major border crossings would reduce congestion and waiting times
- An anti-corruption reporting mechanism specifically for border crossings should be visible and accessible

### Refugee & Humanitarian Services
- A digital registration and case tracking system for refugees would improve accountability significantly
- Regular community information sessions in Swahili and other languages would reduce anxiety and misinformation
- Mobile registration units visiting remote refugee settlements would reduce the burden on central camps
- Partner with UNHCR to offer a dedicated legal aid desk within the immigration office

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. Person in immigration detention without legal basis, proper notification, or access to legal counsel
2. Asylum seeker or refugee facing immediate risk of deportation to a country where they face persecution
3. Refugee or stateless child without documentation or registration — risk of statelessness and rights violations
4. Credible allegation of sexual harassment or exploitation by an immigration officer or consular staff member
5. Person denied emergency travel documents despite documented life-threatening medical emergency abroad
6. Confirmed bribery or corrupt solicitation at a border crossing or immigration counter by an official
7. Forcible removal of a person from the country without proper deportation procedures or legal review
8. Family separated at a border crossing — particularly children separated from parents without documentation
9. Physical assault on an applicant by immigration or border control personnel
10. Serious data breach exposing passport, biometric, or personal identity data of visa applicants
11. Refugee camp security incident — violence, sexual assault, or death within a managed refugee facility
12. Diplomatic crisis situation requiring urgent consular protection for nationals detained abroad

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Embassy vs. Immigration (Tanzania government)**: Feedback addressed to a foreign embassy about visa services belongs in this KB. Feedback about Tanzania government immigration services (work permits, residence permits, EAC passport issuance) also belongs here as both operate under the diplomatic/immigration domain.
- **Embassy vs. Logistics**: Import documentation involving TRA customs and clearing agents is Logistics KB (33). Travel documents, passport authentication, and apostille certification handled by embassy or immigration belong here.
- **Embassy vs. Government Social Services**: Refugee camp food, health, and livelihoods services often run through NGOs or UNHCR — cross-reference NGO KB (34). Immigration status and documentation for refugees belong in this KB.
- **Visa on arrival vs. e-Visa**: Both are visa categories and belong in this KB regardless of whether the process is digital or physical. If the complaint is about the payment platform itself (e.g., the payment gateway crashing), cross-reference Finance/Banking KB (23) for the payment failure component.
- **Diplomatic mission vs. government ministry**: A foreign embassy serving its nationals abroad is Embassy context. Tanzania government ministries serving Tanzanian citizens are Government Services context — route to the relevant sector KB if no dedicated embassy KB applies.
