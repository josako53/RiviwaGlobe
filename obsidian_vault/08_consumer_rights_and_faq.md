# Consumer Rights and Frequently Asked Questions

## Consumer Rights

Every person submitting feedback on Riviwa has the right to:

1. **Free submission**: Submitting feedback is free of charge.
2. **Anonymity**: Choose to submit anonymously without providing personal details.
3. **Tracking**: Receive a unique reference number to track the status of their submission.
4. **Acknowledgement**: Receive confirmation that their feedback was received, within the SLA.
5. **Response**: Receive a substantive response explaining what action was taken.
6. **Escalation**: Request escalation to a higher authority if unsatisfied.
7. **Appeal**: Appeal against a resolution they find unsatisfactory.
8. **No Retaliation**: Submit feedback without fear of retaliation from project staff or contractors.
9. **Confidentiality**: Personal information protected and not shared without consent.
10. **Access**: Submit in their preferred language (Swahili, English, or local languages via voice).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I submit a grievance?

You can submit through:
- WhatsApp: Send a message to the Riviwa WhatsApp number
- Web portal: Visit riviwa.com and click "Submit Feedback"
- SMS: Send keyword to the Riviwa SMS number
- Mobile app: Download the Riviwa app
- In person: Visit your local GRM Officer or Focal Person

### What information do I need to provide?

At minimum:
- What happened (description of the problem)
- Where it happened (district/LGA, ward or area)
- Your contact details (unless submitting anonymously)

Optional but helpful:
- Date of incident
- GPS location
- Photos or documents
- Your name and phone number (for follow-up)

### What happens after I submit?

1. You receive a tracking number immediately
2. A GRM Officer is notified and reviews your submission
3. You receive an acknowledgement (SMS, WhatsApp, or email) within 24-48 hours
4. The officer investigates and takes action
5. You receive a response with the resolution
6. If unsatisfied, you can appeal or request escalation

### How do I track my feedback?

Use your tracking number (e.g. GRV-2026-0001) to check status:
- Web portal: Enter tracking number at riviwa.com/track
- WhatsApp: Send your tracking number to the Riviwa number
- SMS: Send TRACK [tracking number]

### What if I don't have a project ID?

You don't need to know the project ID. The AI will automatically identify which project your feedback relates to based on your location and description. Just describe what happened and where.

If you're submitting feedback about a company or organisation (not a specific construction project), you also don't need a project ID.

### How long will it take to resolve my complaint?

Timelines depend on priority:
- Critical issues (safety, health risk): 3 days
- High priority: 7 days
- Standard: 14 days
- Low priority: 30 days

Complex cases or those requiring escalation may take longer.

### Can I submit anonymously?

Yes. If you select anonymous submission, your name and contact details are not stored. However, anonymous submissions may be harder to follow up on, and you won't receive notifications.

### What if the project team retaliates against me?

Retaliation against anyone who submits feedback is strictly prohibited. If you experience retaliation:
- Submit a new grievance describing the retaliation
- The case will be flagged as high priority
- For World Bank projects, you can also contact the World Bank directly

### Can I submit feedback in Swahili?

Yes. The platform supports Swahili in all channels. The AI chatbot (WhatsApp, SMS) works in Swahili.

### What types of issues CAN'T be submitted?

- Personal disputes not related to the project/organisation
- Criminal matters (these should be reported to police)
- Issues outside the project area or scope
- Spam or false submissions

### What is a Focal Person?

A Focal Person is a community representative trained to help residents submit feedback. They can assist you if you have difficulty with the digital channels.

### How do I find out who my Focal Person is?

The project site notice board should have the Focal Person's contact information. You can also ask at the local government office or project site office.
