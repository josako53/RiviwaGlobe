### Scenario 27-1: Retirement Savings Lost to Pastor — No Recovery Path (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** DOJ press release (USAO-SDNY); NPR report — Lamor Whitehead, Leaders of Tomorrow International Ministries, Brooklyn, New York, USA; convicted March 2024, sentenced June 2024 to 9 years federal prison
**The Problem:** A nurse in her mid-50s transferred $90,000 from her retirement savings to her pastor after he promised to help her purchase a home. Months have passed, no property purchase occurred, and she has now read in the news that the pastor has been federally convicted of fraud. When she calls the church office she is told the pastor is "no longer officiating" and that financial matters should go to the church's legal counsel — a number that rings unanswered. She does not know whether she is eligible for restitution, whether she needs to register with the court, or what steps to take first.
**What staff/org would do:** Church administrative staff deflect to legal counsel, who is unreachable. The victim is told to "pray and wait." No mention is made of the federal victim-witness process or of restitution orders.
**What the Owner does:** Acknowledge the severity of the financial and emotional betrayal. Confirm that as a direct victim named in the federal prosecution she has the right to register with the victim-witness coordinator at the US Attorney's Office and to receive updates on restitution orders. Direct her to the USAO-SDNY victim-witness unit immediately. Advise her to consult a civil attorney about a parallel personal injury or fraud claim against the pastor's seized assets. Emphasize that all documentation of the money transfer must be preserved.

- Opening reply: "What happened to you is a documented federal crime and a profound betrayal of trust. You are not without options — the US Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York has already convicted this pastor, and you have rights as a crime victim including potential restitution. Here is exactly what to do."
- Direct to: USAO-SDNY Victim-Witness Assistance Unit (212-637-2200); FBI New York Field Office victim reporting line; IRS Form 13909 (Tax-Exempt Organization Complaint Referral) if church tax-exempt status was abused; a consumer protection or civil fraud attorney for private restitution claim
- Evidence to collect: All written communications with the pastor (texts, emails, letters); bank transfer records confirming the $90,000 payment; any documents signed or receipts given at the time of transfer; news articles confirming the federal conviction to understand the case timeline

### Scenario 27-2: Sexual Abuse Survivor — Church Routes Complaint Through Diocese, Provides No Independent Support (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** NPR report; US News & World Report; Sokolove Law litigation tracker — Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Los Angeles $880 million settlement with 1,353 survivors, October 2024; abuse documented from 1950s onward; total US Catholic Church payouts 2004–2024 exceeding $5 billion
**The Problem:** A survivor of childhood sexual abuse by a Catholic priest contacts the local parish office seeking referral to a counselor or victim support service. The administrative assistant says abuse allegations must be directed through the bishop's office, provides a diocesan voicemail number that is never returned, and suggests the survivor speak with the current parish priest. The survivor does not know a lawyer, is unaware of the settlement, and does not know whether they are within any applicable statute of limitations.
**What staff/org would do:** Route the complaint through diocesan hierarchy — bishop's office, unanswered voicemail, suggestion to speak to another clergy member. No independent support services are mentioned. No information on legal rights or settlement eligibility is provided.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately validate that this is a serious rights situation requiring independent support outside the Church's institutional structure. Make clear the survivor has no obligation to report through the diocese. Provide direct access to SNAP and RAINN. If abuse occurred in Los Angeles, explain the $880 million settlement and how to inquire about eligibility. Advise on current California lookback window legislation that may extend the statute of limitations.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (survivor safety, active trauma, statute of limitations may be at risk)
- Opening reply: "You do not need to go through the diocese or speak to any priest. You have independent legal rights and there are support services available to you right now — completely outside the Church's control. I am providing those contacts directly."
- Direct to: SNAP (Survivors Network of Those Abused by Priests) hotline 1-877-SNAP-HEALS; RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline 1-800-656-HOPE (4673); clergy abuse specialist law firms (Jeff Anderson & Associates; Pfau Cochran Vertetis Amala); California courts for AB 218 lookback window inquiry; Los Angeles Archdiocese settlement inquiry line if relevant
- Evidence to collect: Any communications with church officials already made; dates, locations, and names associated with the abuse; any prior settlement correspondence received; records of unanswered diocesan contacts (dates, times, numbers called)
- Feedback category tag: `sexual_abuse | institutional_concealment | rights_violation | failed_internal_complaint_mechanism | survivor_support | urgent`

### Scenario 27-3: Sham Veterans' Charity — Call Center Provides Scripted False Assurances to Donor Seeking Accountability (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** FTC press release March 2021 (telefunding enforcement action); FTC Operation Donate with Honor 2018–2021; Associated Community Services collected over $110 million via deceptive telephone solicitations; American Veterans Foundation spent 92% of donations on telemarketers and overhead
**The Problem:** A donor made repeated telephone donations totaling $400 over two years to an organization called after receiving unsolicited calls claiming funds go directly to veterans. A friend has now shown them a news article listing the organization in the FTC's Operation Donate with Honor enforcement sweep, confirming that 92 cents of every dollar went to professional telemarketers and overhead. When the donor calls the charity's number to request a financial breakdown, a trained telemarketer answers, places them on hold for 12 minutes, transfers them twice, and finally reads from a script: "90 cents of every dollar goes directly to veterans" — the exact claim the FTC has already documented as fraudulent. The call ends without any information provided.
**What staff/org would do:** Transfer to a "donor relations line" staffed by the same professional telefunding company. Twelve-minute hold, two transfers, scripted false reassurance, reference to a website with no audited accounts. Call terminated with no outcome.
**What the Owner does:** Confirm the call center failure pattern and the deceptive script are documented in the FTC enforcement record. Advise the donor to stop all future donations immediately. Provide the FTC complaint portal, the state Attorney General, and a reliable charity verification tool. Explain that FTC restitution orders may entitle some donors to partial recovery.

### Scenario 27-4: Young Church Member Pressured to Take Bank Loan and Transfer Funds to Pastor (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** UK Charity Commission GOV.UK inquiry report and press release; BBC Panorama investigation 2019 — SPAC Nation (Salvation Proclaimers Ministries Ltd), London, UK; insolvent 2022; three trustees disqualified 12 years each
**The Problem:** A 22-year-old from a disadvantaged background was pressured by pastors of a Christian youth church to take out a £3,800 personal loan from a high-street bank and transfer the funds to a pastor's personal account. They were told the church would set them up as a "crypto-trader" under pastoral mentorship. No trading ever occurred. The loan is now 90 days overdue. The bank's collections department is calling daily. The young person has no idea the church has been shut down by the Charity Commission, that trustees were disqualified for fraud, or that their loan may have been fraudulently induced — meaning the bank may be required to pause collection while a crime investigation is open.
**What staff/org would do:** Church pastors are unreachable — the charity is dissolved. The bank's collections team pursues standard debt recovery with no knowledge of the fraudulent third-party inducement and refuses to pause collections without a crime reference number.
**What the Owner does:** Urgently recognize the vulnerability of the complainant and the compounding financial and psychological distress. Explain that the loan was likely fraudulently induced and that a crime report to Action Fraud UK will generate a crime reference number the bank legally requires before pausing collections. Direct them to free debt advice and to the Charity Commission's public inquiry records confirming the church fraud — which can be presented to the bank as supporting evidence.

- Direct to: Action Fraud UK (0300 123 2040 / actionfraud.police.uk) to file crime report and obtain crime reference number; StepChange Debt Charity (0800 138 1111, free) for loan management; Citizens Advice Bureau (free, local branches); UK Charity Commission public inquiry records for SPAC Nation (available at gov.uk) to present to bank; Financial Ombudsman Service (0800 023 4567) if bank refuses to engage after crime reference number is provided

### Scenario 27-5: Donor Discovers Charity Trustee Transferred Funds to Themselves — No Communication from Organization (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** UK Charity Commission GOV.UK official inquiry report — Charr Yarr Welfare Foundation Ltd, statutory inquiry opened May 2020; trustees disqualified 2.5–5 years for unexplained fund transfers to a trustee personally, lack of due diligence on large donations, and inadequate governance
**The Problem:** A regular donor to a small UK religious welfare charity notices the charity has gone completely silent on social media and their most recent donation produced no receipt or acknowledgment. They call the charity contact number — no answer for three weeks. They email — no reply. A fellow donor informs them that a trustee was found to have transferred £1,400 of charity funds to themselves with no recorded justification. The donor wants to know whether their money was misused, whether the charity is still operating, and whether they should continue giving.
**What staff/org would do:** No response from the charity. With trustees disqualified and the charity effectively ceased, donors receive no communication, no financial accounting, and no explanation. The trustee named in the inquiry is unreachable.
**What the Owner does:** Confirm that the Charity Commission has completed a statutory inquiry and that the outcome, including the trustee disqualifications, is publicly available. Direct the donor to the Charity Commission public register to check the charity's current status and access the published inquiry report. Advise them to cease donations. If they believe additional funds were misused beyond what the inquiry found, they can file a supplementary public complaint with the Charity Commission.

### Scenario 27-6: Former Member Seeks Course Refund — Refused, Then Subjected to Psychological Pressure Session (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** France 24; NPR; CNN — Church of Scientology France criminal fraud conviction, Paris court October 2009; Cour de cassation (French Supreme Court) upheld conviction October 2013; fines totaling €600,000; one complainant spent approximately €21,000; court explicitly rejected religious freedom defense
**The Problem:** A former member in France paid approximately €18,000 over 18 months for personality tests, sauna "purification packs," vitamin supplements, and advanced courses. They now wish to leave and request a partial refund for courses not completed. The organization's registrar tells them refunds are impossible because all payments were "voluntary religious donations." When the former member persists, they are told to meet with a senior official who subjects them to a two-hour session of psychological pressure to continue their "spiritual journey." They are warned that seeking legal action is "religious persecution."
**What staff/org would do:** Classify all payments as voluntary religious donations to deny refund eligibility. Schedule a pressure meeting disguised as pastoral care. Warn against involving authorities by framing legal action as anti-religious discrimination.
**What the Owner does:** Inform the former member that the French Cour de cassation — the highest court in France — has already ruled in law that these types of payments can constitute fraud, not protected religious donations. Direct them to MIVILUDES (the French government body monitoring cult-like groups) and UNADFI (the national association assisting victims of sectarian abuse). Advise them to file a criminal complaint with local police, citing the confirmed court precedent, and to contact a consumer rights lawyer.

### Scenario 27-7: Church Leader Indicted for Embezzlement — Members Have No Internal Process for Complaint (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** UPI; Al Jazeera; South China Morning Post; Japan Times — Hak Ja Han, Unification Church (Family Federation for World Peace and Unification), South Korea; indicted October 2025 on embezzlement of approximately 500 million won from missionary funds (used for designer jewelry and handbags), illegal political donations of approximately 2.1 billion won, and obstruction of justice
**The Problem:** A longtime Unification Church member in South Korea reads in the news that their global leader has been indicted for embezzling approximately 500 million won from missionary funds to purchase designer luxury goods, with falsified expense reports used to conceal the transactions. The member has personally donated substantial amounts over many years to designated church funds. They want to know whether their donations may have been among the misused funds and how to formally raise this concern. When they raise questions through internal church channels, they are told the indictment is "politically motivated religious persecution" and are socially pressured to remain silent.
**What staff/org would do:** Church hierarchy dismisses the indictment as political in origin. Senior members discourage questioning. No financial accounting is provided to ordinary members. Questions directed upward result in social pressure and warnings about loyalty.
**What the Owner does:** Acknowledge the legitimacy of the concern and confirm the indictment is a matter of public record verifiable through official Korean court and prosecution sources, not political opinion. Clarify that prosecutors — not ordinary members — have the investigative mandate, but that members who have evidence of fund misuse can cooperate voluntarily with prosecutors. Advise preservation of all personal donation records and referral to independent legal counsel.

Direct the victim to the EFCC zonal office to file a formal complaint; doing so adds the victim to the prosecutorial case and increases the basis for any restitution order. Advise them to bring all evidence of payment. Clarify that the Ford Foundation does not operate any grant program requiring fees paid to a pastor or church official.

### Scenario 27-9: Prophet Claims Divine Revelation to Extract Relocation Fees — No Service Delivered, Victim Blamed for "Lack of Faith" (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** EFCC official press release — Prophet Adeniyi Abiodun James, CAC Freedom City Prophetic and Deliverance Ministry, Ilorin, Nigeria; arrested April 2, 2024; defrauded a congregation member of ₦3,980,000 (~$2,600 USD) through a fake Canada relocation scheme using a fabricated "divine revelation"
**The Problem:** A congregation member paid ₦3,980,000 to their prophet in multiple installments after the prophet delivered a public prophecy that the member was divinely destined to relocate to Canada. The prophet claimed to have contacts who would facilitate the visa and migration process. Each payment was described as unlocking the next "spiritual and administrative stage" of the process. After the final payment, the prophet becomes evasive, says delays are caused by "spiritual opposition," and eventually stops responding entirely. Fellow church members warn the victim against reporting, suggesting that doing so would "block the blessing." The victim is now in debt and has no travel documents, no visa application, and no evidence that any immigration process was ever engaged.
**What staff/org would do:** Senior pastors in the church suggest the victim's lack of faith is causing the delay. No refund is offered. The victim is spiritually pressured into silence. No external complaint pathway is mentioned or facilitated.
**What the Owner does:** Firmly and immediately validate that this is financial fraud — not a spiritual failure. Clarify that no licensed Nigerian immigration consultant routes fees through a pastor's personal account and that no legitimate Canadian immigration process requires payments to a religious intermediary. Direct the victim to the EFCC and the Nigeria Immigration Service. Emphasize that reporting is the correct and courageous step.

### Scenario 27-10: Cult Member Cannot Leave — Collateral Threat and Harassment Campaign on Exit Attempt (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** DOJ USAO-EDNY press release (Keith Raniere convicted 2019 on sex trafficking, forced labor conspiracy, racketeering); civil lawsuit filed January 2020 by 80 former members; Courthouse News Service — NXIVM $1.3 million legal bills judgment; organization operated in New York, Texas, and Canada
**The Problem:** A Canadian woman paid CAD $18,000 across multiple NXIVM personal development courses over two years. Upon informing her designated "stripe path" supervisor that she wishes to leave, she is told her departure will be "spiritually and practically costly." She begins receiving pressure calls from senior members. She recalls that she signed a document surrendering personal "collateral" — sensitive personal information — as a condition of joining a women's mentorship sub-group. She fears the organization will release this material to her employer and family if she leaves. She does not know what legal rights she has, whether the collateral mechanism is recognized as criminal coercion, or where to seek help without triggering retaliation.
**What staff/org would do:** NXIVM historically deployed its internal legal team to file civil lawsuits against former members who spoke out. Internal escalation results in increased psychological pressure. Trained coaches are sent to "address her concerns" in ways designed to reinstate compliance, not resolve her grievance.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately recognize this as documented criminal coercion. Validate unconditionally that she has the right to leave any organization. Explain that the collateral mechanism was central to the US Department of Justice's federal conviction of Raniere for sex trafficking and forced labor conspiracy — it was prosecuted as a crime, not a legal contractual tool. Direct her to Canadian law enforcement and to cult recovery support. Advise her that threats to release the collateral may themselves constitute extortion under Canadian law.
