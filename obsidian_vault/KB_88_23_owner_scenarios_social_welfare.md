### Scenario 23-1: Universal Credit Deductions Taken for Government's Own Administrative Error (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** Citizens Advice press release, September 2024 — "Universal Credit recipients unfairly paying £111m a year due to government"; ICE DWP Annual Report 2023–2024, GOV.UK
**The Problem:** A Universal Credit recipient discovers their monthly payment has dropped by £61 with no letter or prior explanation. After logging into their online journal they see a deduction for "overpayment recovery." When they call DWP, the agent confirms a debt exists but cannot explain how it arose. The claimant has not changed their circumstances — the debt is one of 686,756 new overpayments created by DWP official error in 2023–2024, collectively costing claimants £111 million per year. The claimant now cannot cover their rent.
**What staff/org would do:** The DWP phone agent confirms the deduction is being applied and tells the claimant they must repay the debt before it can be reviewed. No information is offered about the right to request a write-off where the error was the agency's own. The agent reads from a script and escalates nothing.
**What the Owner does:** Acknowledge the claimant's distress immediately. Confirm they have an explicit right to challenge overpayment deductions — and that DWP is required under its own guidance to consider waiving debts caused by official error, not claimant error. Advise them to submit a mandatory reconsideration request in writing, ask DWP to confirm in writing whether the overpayment was caused by claimant error or official error, and seek free specialist help at once.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (rent at immediate risk; money taken without explanation)
- Opening reply: "Having money taken from your payment without any explanation is wrong — and if this was DWP's own error, you have a clear right to have the debt cancelled. Let us help you challenge it right now."
- Direct consumer to: Citizens Advice (free — citizensadvice.org.uk or freephone 0800 144 8444); DWP mandatory reconsideration (submit in writing within one month of the decision); Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman (PHSO) if DWP fails to respond to a formal complaint
- Evidence to collect: Screenshot of UC online journal showing the deduction entry; any letters received from DWP; full log of all calls to DWP including date, time, agent name, and what was said; bank statements showing the reduced payment
- Feedback category tag: Benefits/Financial Harm/Official Error

### Scenario 23-2: PIP Disability Benefit Scored Zero Despite Medical Evidence Agency Already Held (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** Disability Rights UK — "7 in 10 PIP appeals won on same evidence DWP already held"; UK Parliament Work and Pensions Committee evidence session, September 2023
**The Problem:** A claimant with a progressive neurological condition submitted three specialist medical letters, a physiotherapy report, and a GP summary before their PIP telephone assessment. Following the assessment, DWP awarded zero points on both components — no PIP at all. The assessment report misquotes the claimant as stating they can walk 200 metres when they clearly stated 20 metres before severe pain. The claimant requested mandatory reconsideration and was refused again on the same evidence. Over 61% of claimants nationally report factual errors of this type in their assessment reports; 70% of PIP appeals at tribunal are overturned — the majority without any new evidence.
**What staff/org would do:** The mandatory reconsideration letter repeats the zero-point decision verbatim, without acknowledging the factual discrepancies the claimant raised. The DWP agent on the follow-up call tells the claimant that if they disagree, they should "go to tribunal."
**What the Owner does:** Validate the experience — this pattern is statistically documented at scale, not an isolated mistake. Advise the claimant to appeal to the First-tier Tribunal (Social Entitlement Chamber) immediately, noting that they do not need new evidence, and to prepare a point-by-point written rebuttal correcting every factual error in the assessment report.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (appeal window of one month from mandatory reconsideration decision — act promptly)
- Opening reply: "A report with factual errors and a zero score despite your evidence is a documented pattern — and 7 in 10 people who appeal PIP to tribunal win, most without any new evidence. You have strong grounds to appeal."
- Direct consumer to: HM Courts and Tribunals Service — appeal form SSCS1 (gov.uk/appeal-benefit-decision); Disability Rights UK; Citizens Advice welfare benefits specialist; Scope disability helpline (0808 800 3333)
- Evidence to collect: Full PIP assessment report (request under Subject Access Request if not automatically provided); all medical letters and reports submitted at assessment; contemporaneous notes from the telephone assessment including exact questions asked and answers given; any GP or specialist willing to confirm the factual error in writing
- Feedback category tag: Disability Benefits/Wrongful Denial/Assessment Factual Error

### Scenario 23-3: DWP Staff Give Wrong Entitlement Advice Over Phone — Then Demand £38,000 Repayment Three Years Later (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** The Big Issue investigative reporting, 2023–2024; GB News — state pensioner £38,000 Universal Credit debt caused by DWP advice
**The Problem:** A state pension-age man who cares for his disabled adult son was advised by a DWP official over the phone that he was entitled to claim Universal Credit. He noted the date of the call and followed the advice. Three years later, DWP stopped his UC claim, told him he had never been eligible, and sent a letter demanding repayment of £38,453. He has a record of the dates he called DWP for advice. No one informed him during any call that he could be making his own contemporaneous notes or that the advice might be wrong.
**What staff/org would do:** DWP's debt recovery team informs the claimant the overpayment is valid and must be repaid regardless of how the claim started. The agent cannot authorise a waiver and tells the claimant to submit a written complaint — while deductions to his other benefits begin immediately. The complaint process takes months with no interim suspension of recovery.
**What the Owner does:** Identify this immediately as an official-error-caused debt, arising directly from incorrect advice given by a DWP agent — one of the strongest grounds for a full debt write-off under DWP's own overpayment waiver policy and established case law. Advise seeking free legal representation immediately given the sum involved, and flag the case for MP parliamentary intervention.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (£38,000 demand; household is a carer with disabled dependent; financial ruin risk)
- Opening reply: "Being advised to claim, following that advice, and then being chased for £38,000 because of the advice you were given is a serious injustice — and there is a documented legal route to challenge this. Here is exactly what to do first."
- Direct consumer to: Citizens Advice Benefits specialist (free — can support waiver application); Child Poverty Action Group welfare benefits legal team; Welfare Rights Unit at local council; local MP to raise as a parliamentary case; Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman (PHSO) after exhausting DWP's internal complaint process
- Evidence to collect: Written chronology of every DWP phone contact — dates, times, what advice was given; any letters from DWP confirming the claim was accepted; bank statements showing UC payments received; any notes made at the time of original phone advice; names of agents where remembered
- Feedback category tag: Benefits/Wrong Information/Official Error/Financial Harm

### Scenario 23-4: SSA Disability Claimant on Hold 93 Minutes, Transferred to Wrong Field Office, Given Incorrect Deadline (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** Government Executive, February 2026 — SSA directing case-processing employees to answer phones after 3 hours of training; DREDF qualitative barriers study, 2025 — SSA call access "gotten a lot worse"
**The Problem:** A 54-year-old woman with Stage 3 breast cancer submitted an SSDI application six months ago and was denied at initial review — as 62% of all applicants are. She needs to file a reconsideration appeal but cannot get through. In four attempts, she experienced holds of 60–93 minutes. On two calls she was transferred to a field office in a different state that had no access to her case and could not help. On her fourth call she reached an agent (redirected to phones after 3 hours of training) who gave her an incorrect deadline for the reconsideration submission, creating a risk that she will miss the actual window and lose her appeal rights entirely.
**What staff/org would do:** The SSA field office she reaches confirms it cannot see her case and tells her to call the national number again. The SSA national number places her on hold again. No alternative is offered.
**What the Owner does:** Provide specific alternative contact routes that do not rely on the main phone line. Confirm the actual reconsideration deadline (60 days from denial notice plus 5 days for mailing). Connect her to advocacy resources that have priority access channels.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (terminal illness; SSDI deadlines are hard cutoffs; missed deadline means losing appeal rights)
- Opening reply: "Four calls and still no real help — that is a system failure, not yours. There is a route that does not depend on that phone line. Here is what to do today."
- Direct consumer to: SSA online portal — my Social Security account at ssa.gov to file reconsideration online without calling; National Organization of Social Security Claimants' Representatives (NOSSCR) — nosscr.org — for free or low-cost legal representation; Disability Rights Advocates; local Social Security field office in person (different from a misrouted phone transfer)
- Evidence to collect: Written log of every call attempt with date, time, hold duration, what was said, and the name of the agent who gave the incorrect deadline; the original denial letter with the actual reconsideration deadline printed on it; all medical documentation with treatment timeline and work-stoppage date
- Feedback category tag: Disability Benefits/Call Center Failure/Process Delay/Missed Deadline Risk

### Scenario 23-5: Child Welfare Caseworker Falsified Home Visit Records — Family With Medically Vulnerable Child Received No Actual Support (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** Colorado Sun investigative reporting, January 2024 — Larimer County caseworker Sandra Spraker charged with 99 criminal counts including 29 forgery counts for fabricating child welfare check records across at least 10 families
**The Problem:** A parent receives a letter from the county child welfare office confirming a home welfare check took place on a specific date. No social worker came to the home that day — or on any of the three other dates listed in official records spanning the past year. The family's child has a medical condition requiring monthly monitoring and support. The parent has now learned through a contact that the assigned caseworker has been arrested on 99 criminal counts for fabricating visit records across multiple families. Their child has had no actual welfare support for potentially over a year, and the parent does not know what services they should have been receiving.
**What staff/org would do:** The county agency front desk tells the parent the case is under criminal investigation and cannot be discussed. They are placed on hold and later told to expect a letter. No interim caseworker is assigned and no acknowledgement of missed services is made.
**What the Owner does:** Treat this as an immediate child safety and adult rights emergency. Advise the parent to demand in writing, today, emergency reassignment of their case to a new caseworker. Escalate directly to the state-level child welfare oversight body — bypassing the county office where the failure occurred. If the child has unmet medical needs, those should be separately flagged to the child's health provider as needing catch-up review.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (child's medical needs unmet for potentially a year; welfare records fabricated; active safety risk)
- Opening reply: "If your child has not actually been checked on despite records saying otherwise, this is an emergency — not something to wait for a letter about. Here is how to escalate right now, bypassing the county office."
- Direct consumer to: Colorado Department of Human Services — Child Welfare Division (state level, bypasses county); Colorado Child Welfare Ombudsman; local legal aid for emergency representation; report fabricated records to Larimer County District Attorney if not already submitted; child's own GP or medical team to flag the gap in welfare monitoring
- Evidence to collect: All county letters claiming visits occurred on specific dates; phone records or diary showing no visitor arrived on those dates; any neighbors or family present who can confirm no social worker came; the child's current medical records documenting unmet needs during the period
- Feedback category tag: Child Welfare/Staff Misconduct/Falsified Records/Emergency

### Scenario 23-6: SNAP Benefits Terminated Without Individual Notice — Single Mother's EBT Card Declined at Checkout (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** CBPP SNAP Tracker, 2025–2026 — at least 3.5 million people lost food assistance following Republican budget reconciliation changes; Arizona lost 51% of its SNAP beneficiary base; Florida documented 15% administrative error rate in SNAP eligibility determinations
**The Problem:** A single mother of three children in Arizona discovers her SNAP EBT card is declined at a grocery checkout. She received no individual letter telling her benefits were stopping. Her benefits were terminated because she did not submit new work documentation under requirements she was not clearly informed about — she has been working 22 hours per week but did not know the new form was required. Arizona has lost over half its SNAP recipients. When she calls the state agency, she waits 45 minutes and is told to reapply — a process taking 30 days — without being told she has a legal right to emergency expedited processing for households with immediate food need.
**What staff/org would do:** The state agency phone agent confirms her case is closed, tells her to submit a new application, and does not mention expedited processing, emergency food resources, or her right to appeal the termination. She is told to call back to check application status.
**What the Owner does:** Inform her immediately that federal SNAP law requires expedited processing (within 7 days) for households facing urgent food insecurity — this is not discretionary and she must request it by name. Direct her to in-person office attendance for fastest processing and to emergency food resources for the days until benefits are restored.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (three children with no food access; EBT card declined; immediate food insecurity)
- Opening reply: "Having your card declined with children to feed is a food emergency — and you have a federal legal right to have your case processed within 7 days under SNAP expedited rules. Here is exactly what to say when you go in."
- Direct consumer to: Arizona Department of Economic Security SNAP office — go in person and request expedited processing by name, citing 7 CFR 273.2(i); 2-1-1 Arizona (dial 211) for same-day emergency food pantry locations; Greater Phoenix Food Bank or local food bank for immediate food while appeal is processed; legal aid for SNAP appeal representation
- Evidence to collect: Receipt or photo of declined EBT transaction with date and amount; any letters or messages received about the work documentation requirement; proof of current work hours such as pay stubs or an employer letter; household income and essential expense documentation for expedited processing eligibility confirmation
- Feedback category tag: Food Assistance/Benefits Terminated/Refused Service/Emergency

### Scenario 23-7: Tigray Registered Beneficiary Arrives at Distribution Point Three Consecutive Months — No Food, No Explanation, No Grievance Channel (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** PBS NewsHour investigation, 2023; Addis Standard exclusive — coordinated food diversion scheme across seven Ethiopian regions involving 63 commercial flour mills, federal government, and regional entities; USAID/WFP suspension of aid following what US officials described as "potentially the largest-ever diversion of food aid in any country"
**The Problem:** A subsistence farmer in Tigray, Ethiopia, is registered as a WFP food aid beneficiary. He has walked four hours to reach the distribution point on the scheduled date. Staff tell him there is nothing available today — no food, no rescheduled date, no written receipt of his attendance. This is the third consecutive month this has happened. What he does not know is that the wheat designated for his community has been diverted to commercial flour mills by local officials as part of a scheme later uncovered spanning seven regions. No posted grievance process exists at the distribution point. There is no independent channel through which he can report that he — a registered beneficiary — received nothing.
**What staff/org would do:** Distribution point staff tell him to return next month. No complaint form is offered, no alternative contact provided, no written record of his visit or non-receipt is given. Staff are affiliated with the same local government structure later implicated in the diversion.
**What the Owner does:** Treat this as an acute food security emergency. Activate an anonymous, tamper-proof grievance recording channel so the non-delivery is formally documented outside local control. Verify his registration and entitlement. Escalate to WFP field monitors and independent humanitarian bodies operating in the region.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (acute food insecurity; three consecutive non-deliveries; family with no income alternative; no functioning recourse channel)
- Opening reply: "You are registered and you were entitled to food today. The fact that nothing was there three months in a row must be formally recorded — here is how to report it safely, outside the local office."
- Direct consumer to: WFP Inspector General — secure reporting at oig.wfp.org/report-fraud (independent of country office management); OCHA Ethiopia for humanitarian escalation; Ethiopian Human Rights Commission; international NGOs active in Tigray — IRC, MSF, NRC — who maintain independent beneficiary hotlines separate from government-affiliated structures
- Evidence to collect: WFP beneficiary registration card or number; written or recorded log of dates and times of each visit to the distribution point with no food received; names or descriptions of staff present; other beneficiaries present who also received nothing and are willing to corroborate; any communications from WFP or local authorities about the distributions
- Feedback category tag: Food Aid/Non-Delivery/Corruption/Emergency

### Scenario 23-8: Ethiopian NGO Field Coordinator Documents Food Diversion to Flour Mills — Blocked When Attempting to Report to Implicated Regional Body (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** Addis Standard exclusive, 2023 — coordinated diversion scheme confirmed involving 63 commercial flour mills across seven Ethiopian regions; WFP and USAID suspending operations after discovering what officials called the largest-ever diversion of food aid in any country
**The Problem:** A field coordinator at a local Ethiopian NGO has conducted site visits over six weeks and documented that wheat from three consecutive WFP distributions has been transported to a commercial flour mill in a nearby town rather than to beneficiary communities. She has vehicle registration numbers, photographs, dates, and the names of officials who signed off on the transfers. She attempted to file a formal report with the regional coordination body responsible for aid distribution oversight — but that regional body is named in the scheme. Her WFP country office contact has not responded to two follow-up emails over three weeks. A regional official has made indirect contact suggesting she should withdraw her documentation.
**What staff/org would do:** The regional coordination body acknowledges receipt of her report but takes no visible action. WFP's internal email contact remains unresponsive. No escalation pathway is communicated to her. The obstruction she is experiencing is itself undocumented and informal.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately direct the field coordinator to independent reporting channels that fully bypass the implicated regional body and the non-responsive country office. Confirm her documentation has legal and investigative value and must be securely preserved now — before submission. Connect her to international whistleblower protection mechanisms appropriate to the Ethiopia operational context.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (not immediate physical danger as described — but evidence is perishable and obstruction is active; treat as time-sensitive)
- Opening reply: "What you have documented is serious, and the pressure you are receiving to stay quiet makes going outside the region even more important. There are independent channels that do not route through the country office. Here is how to report safely."
- Direct consumer to: WFP Inspector General directly — oig.wfp.org/report-fraud (fully independent of WFP management and country office); USAID Office of Inspector General — oig.usaid.gov (reports from Ethiopia accepted); UN Ethics Office for formal whistleblower protection registration; Transparency International Ethiopia; Committee to Protect Journalists if personal safety escalates
- Evidence to collect: All photographs, vehicle registration records, and date-stamped documentation secured with a trusted external party before submission; copies of all emails to WFP with sent timestamps; written record of the obstruction contact including date, medium, and exact words used; copies shared with a trusted colleague or legal adviser outside the organization as backup
- Feedback category tag: Food Aid/Staff Misconduct/Whistleblower/Corruption Reporting

### Scenario 23-9: Somali Displaced Family Never Receives WFP Food Aid — Delivery Recorded as Complete by Contractor That Diverted It (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** AEI analysis of UN Monitoring Group on Somalia findings — three Somali businessmen holding approximately $160 million in WFP food transport contracts simultaneously alleged to be diverting food aid while engaged in arms trading
**The Problem:** A family displaced by drought and living in an IDP camp in Somalia has been registered as WFP food aid beneficiaries for four months. They have not received any of the three scheduled monthly food deliveries. Other families in the camp report the same pattern. Community members observed the contracted transport vehicle arrive once, unload a small fraction of what should have been delivered, and leave quickly. WFP's official delivery records show all deliveries as completed and confirmed. There is no grievance box at the camp, no posted hotline, and no process by which beneficiaries can formally report a discrepancy between what records say and what they actually received.
**What staff/org would do:** Local distribution monitors employed through the same contractor network confirm deliveries as complete in the system. There is no independent verification mechanism at this location. The family is told to speak to the local distribution coordinator — who is affiliated with the same contractor.
**What the Owner does:** Provide a trusted, contractor-independent grievance channel. Record the non-delivery incidents systematically across the camp to establish a pattern across multiple families. Escalate to WFP Inspector General and independent humanitarian monitors operating in Somalia.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (displaced family with children; four months of acute food insecurity; records falsely show deliveries completed; no existing recourse)
- Opening reply: "Records that show delivery completed when your family received nothing is exactly the kind of discrepancy that must be reported independently of the local system. You have a right to raise this — here is how to do it outside the contractor network."
- Direct consumer to: WFP Inspector General — oig.wfp.org/report-fraud (accepts reports in Arabic and Somali); UNHCR Somalia protection desk for displaced person advocacy; OCHA Somalia; independent NGOs operating in the camp — IRC, Norwegian Refugee Council (NRC) — who maintain separate beneficiary feedback mechanisms independent of WFP contractors
- Evidence to collect: WFP beneficiary card or registration number; written log of dates and expected delivery amounts for each month with record of what was or was not received; names of other affected families in the camp willing to corroborate; any observations of contractor vehicle activity with approximate dates, times, and quantities unloaded
- Feedback category tag: Food Aid/Non-Delivery/Contractor Fraud/Displaced Population

### Scenario 23-10: WFP Somalia Field Monitor Witnesses Contractor Diverting Food Consignment — Supervisor Linked to Contractor Suppresses Report (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** UN Monitoring Group on Somalia annual report; AEI analysis — WFP Somalia transport contractors collectively holding approximately $160 million in WFP contracts while simultaneously alleged to be diverting food aid and engaged in arms trading
**The Problem:** A WFP-contracted local field monitor in Somalia has directly observed a transport contractor diverting approximately 40% of a food consignment to a commercial depot instead of the designated beneficiary distribution site. She attempted to log the incident in the standard reporting system. Her supervisor — whose position is operationally linked to the contracting company — told her the report "would create problems" and instructed her not to file it. She is aware the contractor holds contracts worth approximately $160 million and has significant local influence and connections. She is considering whether and how to escalate, and whether doing so puts her at personal risk.
**What staff/org would do:** The supervisor dismisses the report verbally and does not allow it into the formal system. Standard internal reporting pathways within the field structure route through the same contractor-adjacent management layer. There is no independently accessible internal channel visible to the field monitor.
**What the Owner does:** Provide a secure, direct reporting channel that is fully independent of the local management structure and the contractor. Affirm her legal and professional obligation to report. Connect her with whistleblower protection resources and, if her personal safety is a concern, with NGO security desks that can advise on safe escalation in the Somali operational context.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (personal safety concern may escalate this; evidence is time-sensitive; obstruction is active)
- Opening reply: "What you witnessed needs to be reported outside your local chain — and there are protected, independent channels designed exactly for situations where the normal route has been closed. Here is how to report safely."
- Direct consumer to: WFP Inspector General directly — oig.wfp.org/report-fraud (fully independent of WFP field management; accepts reports from contracted staff); USAID OIG Somalia oversight team — oig.usaid.gov; UN Ethics Office for formal whistleblower protection; IRC or Mercy Corps INGO safety desks for personal security advice if facing pressure or threats
- Evidence to collect: Detailed written record of the diversion incident — date, location, quantities observed, vehicle details, individuals present; secure copy stored with a trusted person entirely outside the organization before submission; written record of the supervisor's instruction not to file the report, with date and exact words used; copies of anything shared with a legal adviser as independent backup
- Feedback category tag: Food Aid/Staff Misconduct/Contractor Fraud/Whistleblower
