# Feedback Types

Riviwa supports four types of feedback submissions. Each has a different purpose and workflow.

## 1. Grievance — Complaint (GRV)

A Grievance is a Complaint. The consumer expresses dissatisfaction or annoyance about something — a real or imagined cause for complaint — usually arising from a feeling of being treated unfairly. It may also refer to the distress caused by an issue and the formal complaint filed to seek a resolution.

**Examples:**
- "The construction of the road has blocked the drainage ditch next to my house and now it floods every rain."
- "Workers from the project entered my farm without permission and damaged my crops."
- "The contractor has not paid compensation promised for the land acquired."
- "The road rehabilitation project is generating excessive dust that is affecting my business and health."
- "My water supply has been cut off for 3 weeks due to pipeline construction and nobody has informed me."
- "I was displaced from my home for the project but have not received the resettlement allowance."
- "I waited 4 hours and was never seen by a doctor."
- "I was charged a fee I never agreed to."
- "The staff member treated me with disrespect and I feel this is unfair."

**Key characteristics:**
- Requires formal acknowledgement within SLA timeline
- Assigned to a GRM Officer or Committee
- Goes through escalation levels if not resolved
- Submitter can appeal a resolution they are unsatisfied with

**Reference format:** GRV-YYYY-NNNN (e.g. GRV-2026-0001)

---

## 2. Suggestion (SGG)

A Suggestion consists of recommendations or advice on how things/matters should be done, handled, or attended to for the sake of improvement — or what should be considered at the moment or next time.

**Examples:**
- "You should install proper pedestrian crossing at the junction near the school before paving."
- "The project should hold community meetings more regularly to update residents on progress."
- "A public notice board at the site showing project timelines would help the community plan."
- "Can you add a footpath alongside the new road for pedestrians?"
- "The contractor should work in sections so the road is not all blocked at once."
- "Next time, please send an SMS notification before cutting water supply."
- "You should hire more nurses during peak hours."

**Key characteristics:**
- Forward-looking — focused on improvement rather than expressing harm
- Does not require escalation like grievances
- Can be actioned or noted
- Helps the organisation improve its service delivery

**Reference format:** SGG-YYYY-NNNN (e.g. SGG-2026-0001)

---

## 3. Applause (APP)

Applause is a compliment of the service, product, staff, entity, feature, organisation, or business — expressing appreciation, recognition, or commendation of excellent work.

**Examples:**
- "The project team always cleans up at the end of the day and we appreciate that."
- "The public consultations were very well organised and our concerns were heard."
- "We are happy with how quickly the road was repaired after the floods."
- "The GRM Officer resolved our complaint within a week — very professional."
- "Dr. Amina is an outstanding doctor — she explained everything clearly and I felt respected."
- "The new mobile banking feature is excellent — it saved me so much time."
- "The receptionist was warm, patient, and helpful. This is exactly the kind of service we need."

**Key characteristics:**
- Positive feedback — no escalation required
- Helps recognize good performance across all staff and departments
- The AI multiplies applause to all relevant staff and departments
- Useful for reporting on stakeholder satisfaction and replicating excellence

**Reference format:** APP-YYYY-NNNN (e.g. APP-2026-0001)

---

## 4. Inquiry (INQ)

An Inquiry is asking a question or requesting details — e.g. a customer making an inquiry about pricing or availability. It is a request for information designed to resolve doubts, discover facts, or uncover the truth — including how things work or how things are done from the service or product manual.

**Examples:**
- "When will the road construction in our area start?"
- "How can I apply for compensation for my property that is in the project area?"
- "What are the working hours for the construction crew?"
- "Who is the project contact person for our community?"
- "Where can I get the Environmental and Social Impact Assessment report?"
- "How do I qualify for the social welfare benefit?"
- "What documents do I need to open an account?"
- "Does this hospital offer maternity services?"

**Key characteristics:**
- Informational — requires a response with facts, guidance, or clarification
- No formal escalation
- Can be converted to a grievance if the inquiry reveals an underlying unresolved issue
- Covers both factual questions AND procedural/process questions ("how does this work?")

**Reference format:** INQ-YYYY-NNNN (e.g. INQ-2026-0001)

---

## Choosing the Right Type

| The person wants to... | Type |
|---|---|
| Express dissatisfaction, distress, or unfair treatment | Grievance |
| Make a recommendation, advice, or improvement idea | Suggestion |
| Compliment a service, product, staff, entity, or feature | Applause |
| Ask a question or get information / understand how something works | Inquiry |

---

## Important Notes

- **Grievance = Complaint**: These two words mean the same thing in Riviwa. A consumer who says "I want to make a complaint" is submitting a Grievance.
- **Multiple types in one message**: A consumer may express more than one type in a single message — e.g. a complaint AND a suggestion for how to fix it. Each is captured separately.
- **Inquiry that reveals a problem**: If a consumer asks a question that reveals an underlying grievance (e.g. "why haven't I received my payment?"), convert or add a grievance item.
- **Applause targets**: Applause can be directed at a specific staff member, department, product, service, feature, or the organisation as a whole.
