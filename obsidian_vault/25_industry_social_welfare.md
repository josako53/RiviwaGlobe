## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Social Welfare

### Program Design & Access Improvements
- The TASAF eligibility criteria should be reviewed — many genuine poor are excluded
- Cash transfer amounts should be indexed to inflation — they have not changed in years
- Community targeting committees should include independent observers to prevent bias
- You should create a mobile registration unit to reach elderly people who cannot travel
- Digital application forms would make it easier for educated applicants to submit
- Beneficiary lists should be publicly posted at village offices for transparency
- A grievance mechanism specific to cash transfer programs should be established
- Regular beneficiary re-verification should happen annually, not every three years

### Disability Services Improvements
- Disability registration offices should be in every district, not just regional centers
- Partner with vocational training centers to offer skills training for PWDs
- Assistive device distribution should happen at community level, not only in Dar es Salaam
- Provide sign language training to all front-line social welfare officers
- Create an accessible complaints hotline for people with hearing or speech impairments
- Include disability-inclusive design requirements in all government building renovations

### Child Welfare Suggestions
- More foster families should be recruited and supported with better stipends
- Social workers should be assigned a maximum caseload to improve quality of visits
- Child welfare case management should be digitized so case files are never lost
- Partner with schools to identify at-risk children earlier
- Regular unannounced inspections of orphanages and residential centers should occur

### Community Development
- Savings groups for women in poor communities should get seed capital support
- Community development officers should hold monthly open forums for beneficiaries
- Vocational training centers for youth should be established in every ward
- Link TASAF beneficiaries to agricultural extension services for income diversification
- Establish community resource centers where people can access welfare services digitally

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

- Child abuse reported and no action taken within 48 hours — child remains in danger
- Child found living in hazardous conditions — immediate removal and safe shelter required
- Resident in elderly care home reporting physical abuse or neglect by staff
- Suspected embezzlement or diversion of cash transfer funds by local officials
- Fraudulent beneficiary registration — non-poor households replacing genuine poor families
- Missing child from a government-run orphanage or residential facility
- Death of a beneficiary at a care facility due to suspected negligence
- Medical emergency at a residential care center with no trained medical personnel present
- Report of sexual abuse or exploitation within a rehabilitation center or approved school
- Whistleblower allegation of systematic bribery by social welfare officers during application processing
- Refugee family facing immediate deportation despite pending protection claim
- Entire community excluded from disaster relief distribution through corruption or error

## Disambiguation Notes

- Social welfare feedback about **cash transfer delays** may superficially resemble **banking complaints** — classify as Social Welfare if the payment mechanism is a government program (TASAF/PSSN) and the complaint targets the program, not the bank or mobile money operator directly.
- **Disability services** overlap with **Healthcare** when the complaint is about medical treatment at a rehabilitation center — classify by primary subject: if the concern is about service eligibility and social support, it is Social Welfare; if it is about clinical treatment, it is Healthcare.
- Feedback about **school bursaries and HESLB** may overlap with **Education** — classify as Social Welfare if the primary complaint is about poverty-based eligibility and welfare targeting, and as Education if the concern is about academic policy or institutional procedures.
- **NGO service delivery** complaints may fit either Social Welfare or a specific sector (health, education) — use the program context (poverty alleviation, child protection, disability) to anchor to Social Welfare.
- Feedback mentioning **refugee services, UNHCR, or asylum** is in Social Welfare / Social Services even if it touches immigration — classify by the welfare dimension, not the immigration dimension.
