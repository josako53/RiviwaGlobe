## Complaint / Grievance Signals — Mining / Extractive Industries

### Community Impact & Land Rights
- Your company took our farmland for mining operations and the compensation was nowhere near fair value
- The resettlement house you built is smaller and worse than the home you demolished — this is not acceptable
- We were promised agricultural land equivalent to what was taken but received barren plots with no water
- Our community was never properly consulted before mining operations began on our ancestral land
- The dust from blasting covers our crops and livestock every single day — our yields have collapsed
- Our water source is now contaminated and we have confirmed this is from your mine's tailings
- The vibrations from blasting operations have cracked the walls of houses in our village
- You promised a tarmac road to our community as part of the community benefit agreement — it has never been built
- The community development fund that your CSR department promised has never been disbursed
- Our fishing grounds near the river have been destroyed by discharge from your processing plant
- The noise from your mining operations at night is unbearable and is affecting children's schooling
- Your trucks pass through our village day and night destroying the road and creating dangerous dust
- We were relocated to make way for the mine and the promised amenities — school, clinic, water — have not been provided
- The fence your company erected cut off our access to the market route we have used for generations
- Our community was told blasting would happen twice a week but it is happening six days a week

### Environmental Violations
- There is a visible oil spill from your processing plant into the seasonal river that flows through our farms
- The tailings dam overflowed during last season's rains and contaminated farmland downstream
- Mercury is being detected in our river — artisanal miners upstream are using mercury without controls
- The cyanide storage facility at your plant has had a visible leak that your team has not addressed
- Trees have been cleared far beyond the mine boundary into our protected forest reserve
- You have not completed land reclamation on the areas you finished mining three years ago
- The open pits you have abandoned are now drowning hazards — two children nearly died last month
- Your dust suppression system is switched off most of the time — the community lives in a permanent dust cloud
- The EIA mitigation measures agreed with NEMC are clearly not being implemented on the ground
- We have reported acid mine drainage to your environment department four times without response
- Waste rock from the mine is being dumped in a dry riverbed that floods during rains and carries waste to farms
- You are operating at night beyond the hours permitted in your environmental certificate
- The reclaimed area was planted with non-native species that are now invasive — you did not consult us
- Your exploration team left drill sites open and unrehabilitated after completing exploration

### Worker Safety & Conditions
- A rock fall in shaft 3 injured two miners and your safety team said it was "minor" — this is unacceptable
- Workers are not provided with proper PPE — many are working underground with no helmets or boots
- The ventilation in level 5 of the underground workings is inadequate and workers are reporting breathing difficulties
- Our shift ended but we were not given transport out and had to walk five kilometers in the dark
- There was an explosion in the processing area and your company has not communicated what happened
- The safety harness we were issued is visibly damaged and no replacement has been provided
- Workers are being pressured to enter areas that have not been declared safe by the shift supervisor
- The blasting team did not give the proper all-clear signal before detonation — this nearly caused casualties
- Medical personnel at the mine clinic turned away an injured worker and told him to seek care elsewhere
- There have been three near-miss incidents this month alone and none were documented or investigated
- Workers are required to operate equipment they have not been trained or certified to use
- The rescue equipment at the mine is expired and has not been replaced
- We reported a methane gas pocket in section B to the mine captain three weeks ago and no action was taken
- Workers descend without gas monitors — we have no way of knowing if the air is safe

### Employment, Wages & Local Content
- Your company promised to hire local community members but is bringing in workers from outside the region
- We were employed as contract workers for two years but denied permanent employment without explanation
- My wages were deducted without explanation and I cannot get anyone in HR to clarify
- The local content requirement says 30% of goods must be sourced locally but you are importing everything
- We were dismissed without notice or severance pay when the contract ended
- Your company brought in expatriates for roles that local Tanzanian professionals are fully capable of filling
- We are paid less than workers from outside our region for identical roles — this is discriminatory
- The promised skills training for community members has never materialized despite being in the benefit agreement
- Workers are not given pay slips and cannot verify deductions made from their wages
- Our union representatives were denied access to the mine to meet with workers — this violates labor law
- We have been working 14-hour shifts without overtime pay and with no rest day
- The housing provided to local employees is inferior to that provided to expatriate staff doing comparable work

### Artisanal & Small-Scale Miner (ASM) Issues
- Your company's security guards assaulted artisanal miners who were operating outside your lease boundary
- The large-scale mine is encroaching into areas designated for ASM operations without notice
- We have been using this area for artisanal mining for 20 years and suddenly your company has a license that covers it
- Your company is purchasing our gold at below-market prices through your buying center and we have no alternative buyer
- The government liaison officer from MEM that we reported to has not responded for three months
- ASM miners are being criminalized by your company's security team even when operating in permitted areas
- We applied for a primary mining license through TMAA 18 months ago and have received no response or update
- Mercury-free mining training was promised to our ASM cooperative by your CSR team but never delivered

### Regulatory & License Violations
- Your company exceeded the mineral extraction tonnage allowed under your mining license
- Blasting is occurring outside the hours permitted by TMAA and NEMC
- The environmental bond that should be held by your company appears to not have been paid based on TMAA records
- Your company's mineral royalty payments to TRA appear to be understated based on publicly reported production volumes
- You are extracting minerals from grid squares not covered by your prospecting license
- The local content plan submitted to MEM does not reflect actual practice on the ground
- Your company failed to submit the required quarterly environmental compliance report to NEMC
- Community development fund contributions are not matching the percentages required under your license agreement

## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Mining / Extractive Industries

### Community Relations & Social Responsibility
- Your company should establish a community liaison committee that meets monthly and includes village leaders
- The community development fund should be transparent — publish the budget and expenditure every quarter
- Consider establishing a grievance desk staffed by a local community member, not just company HR
- Your CSR program should prioritize education and healthcare because these are the needs the community actually has
- Engage traditional elders and local government alongside formal community meetings — do not bypass them
- Provide agricultural support to displaced farmers including seeds, tools, and irrigation access
- Your company should hire a local community liaison officer who speaks the language of the host community
- Consider a scholarship program for children from the host community as part of your benefit agreement
- Establish a community environmental monitoring committee with training and equipment to measure dust and water quality
- The CDF project selection process should be community-led, not decided by your headquarters

### Environmental Management
- You should install real-time dust monitoring equipment at the mine boundary and share readings with the community
- Revegetation of the disturbed areas should use indigenous species appropriate to the local ecosystem
- Installing water quality monitoring stations downstream of the mine would build community trust
- Your tailings dam inspection schedule should be increased during the rainy season
- Mercury substitution equipment should be made available to ASM miners who want to adopt mercury-free processing
- Acid mine drainage treatment should begin immediately rather than after the problem becomes visible
- Establish a clear emergency response protocol for tailings dam failure and communicate it to communities
- The open pits from expired operations must be fenced and signed immediately as they are a drowning hazard

### Worker Safety Improvements
- All underground workers should carry personal gas monitors regardless of the cost
- Introduce a mandatory stop-work authority so any worker can halt operations if they identify a safety risk
- Your mine rescue team should practice emergency drills monthly, not only annually
- Safety incident near-misses should be reported and investigated with the same seriousness as actual incidents
- Provide independent health screening for workers exposed to silica dust and cyanide
- The mine should have a 24/7 medical officer, not just a nurse available only during day shift
- Safety induction should be conducted in Swahili and local languages, not only in English

### Local Content & Employment
- You should publish your local content performance data annually so communities and government can verify compliance
- Partner with vocational training institutions to upskill community members for mine roles rather than importing workers
- Establish a preference policy for locally owned suppliers for goods and services that do not require specialized expertise
- Create an ASM formalization program that helps artisanal miners obtain legal operating licenses
- Offer fair offtake agreements to ASM miners rather than exploitative buying center pricing

## Inquiry / Question Signals — Mining / Extractive Industries

### Community Rights & Compensation
- What is the basis for the land compensation valuation your company used for our village?
- Who do we contact at your company to raise a community grievance about our relocated homes?
- How much is your company contributing to the community development fund this year?
- What is the process for appealing the resettlement allocation if we believe it is unfair?
- Are we entitled to compensation for crops destroyed during your exploration phase?
- What is the boundary of the mining lease and how do we verify it matches what was approved?
- Who is the government authority we can contact if your company does not respond to our concerns?
- Is there a legal obligation for your company to consult us before expanding operations into new areas?
- How do we find out if our drinking water has been contaminated by mining activities?
- What is the community's share of royalties paid by your mine and how is it distributed?

### Environmental Questions
- What chemicals are being used in your mineral processing plant and are they safe?
- How do you prevent tailings from spilling into the seasonal river during heavy rains?
- What is the reclamation plan for the open pit areas after mining is complete?
- Has your company completed the full environmental impact assessment required by NEMC?
- Who monitors compliance with the EIA mitigation measures — is it NEMC or your own team?
- What is your dust management protocol and how is it enforced during blasting operations?
- Is mercury being used anywhere in the processing chain and what controls are in place?
- How do I report an environmental violation to TMAA or NEMC?

### Employment & Labor Questions
- What are the criteria for local community members to be considered for employment at the mine?
- What is the minimum wage paid to unskilled workers at your mine?
- Are workers entitled to overtime pay for shifts beyond eight hours?
- What is the process for joining the mine workers union?
- Can workers be dismissed without notice if the contract is a short-term contract?
- What local content percentage is required under your mining license for employment?
- Are there opportunities for women in non-traditional mining roles?
- What training and certification does your company provide before assigning workers to underground roles?

### Regulatory & Licensing Questions
- Is your company's mining license current and what area does it cover?
- How do we verify that your company's royalty payments to TRA are correct?
- What permit does your company hold for blasting operations and what are the permitted hours?
- How do we file a complaint with TMAA about a mining company's non-compliance?
- What is the role of the Mining Commission in resolving disputes between communities and mining companies?
- Are artisanal miners permitted to operate within 500 meters of a large-scale mine boundary?
- How do we apply for a primary mining license for an area that is not under any existing lease?

### Safety Questions
- What is the mine's emergency response plan in the event of an underground collapse?
- How do you monitor and manage air quality in underground workings?
- What should a worker do if they are asked to enter an area they believe is unsafe?
- Is the mine clinic equipped to handle serious trauma injuries from mining accidents?
- What protection do workers have if they report a safety violation to management?
- How many days of safety training are provided to a new underground worker before their first shift?

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. Tailings dam structural failure or overflow threatening downstream communities with toxic discharge — environmental catastrophe requiring immediate emergency response
2. Fatal or serious injury incident underground (rock fall, explosion, entrapment) — immediate safety investigation, regulatory notification to OSHA and TMAA mandatory
3. Contamination of community drinking water supply with cyanide, mercury, or acid mine drainage — public health emergency
4. Security personnel fatally shot or seriously harmed artisanal miners operating outside company lease — criminal and human rights violation
5. Blasting operations caused structural damage to community homes — urgent engineering assessment and compensation process required
6. Mine site environmental certificate has lapsed and company is continuing full production — regulatory violation requiring immediate NEMC notification
7. Community members have been forcibly evicted from land without court order or proper resettlement process — human rights violation
8. A worker disclosed that gas monitoring in underground sections has been disabled or ignored — imminent life-threatening safety risk
9. Mercury contamination detected in a river used by communities for drinking water — public health emergency requiring Tanzania Food and Drug Authority (TFDA) and MEM notification
10. Child labor detected in artisanal or informal mining operations within or adjacent to the company's operational area
11. Cover-up of a safety incident by mine management to avoid regulatory reporting obligations — criminal liability
12. Collapse or crack detected in tailings dam wall — critical infrastructure emergency requiring immediate engineering inspection and possible evacuation
13. Community leader was threatened or intimidated by company representatives to prevent them from raising grievances — freedom of expression and protection violation

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Mining vs. Construction**: Both involve heavy machinery, blasting, and community noise complaints. Mining feedback will reference ores, minerals, shafts, tailings, royalties, and TMAA. Construction feedback references buildings, contractors, building materials, and TANROADS or NHC. Look for mineralogy terms to confirm mining context.
- **Mining vs. Agriculture / Land**: Land rights complaints can originate from mining impact on farming communities. If the complaint references compensation for farmland, crop loss, or water contamination linked to an extraction operation, classify as Mining even if the speaker is a farmer.
- **Large-Scale Mining vs. Artisanal Small-Scale Mining (ASM)**: Large-scale mining feedback will reference companies, corporate processes, and SML/ML licenses. ASM feedback will reference individual miners, cooperatives, mercury use, buying centers, and PML licenses. The urgency and regulatory pathways differ significantly.
- **Mining vs. Oil and Gas**: Tanzania has offshore gas and some oil exploration. Gas sector feedback references TPDC, LNG, offshore platforms, gas pipelines, and natural gas. Mining feedback references solid minerals, TMAA, and on-land extraction. Both can involve community displacement — use regulatory body references to distinguish.
- **Environmental Complaint vs. Community Development Complaint**: Environmental complaints focus on pollution, contamination, land degradation, and EIA compliance. Community development complaints focus on promised benefits, CDF disbursement, employment commitments, and infrastructure obligations. Both are mining grievances but route to different departments (Environment vs. Community Affairs).
