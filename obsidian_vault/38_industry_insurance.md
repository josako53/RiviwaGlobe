## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Insurance Services

### Claims Process Improvement
- The insurer should allow customers to upload claim documents via a mobile app to speed up processing
- Insurance companies should be required to give a final decision on claims within 30 days of receiving all documents
- A dedicated claims tracking system via USSD or WhatsApp would help policyholders follow up without calling
- Claims assessors should visit within 48 hours of a report being filed — not weeks later
- The insurer should provide a written explanation with every claim rejection, citing the exact policy clause
- Partial claim payments should be made for the undisputed portion while the disputed amount is being assessed
- A claims fast-track for amounts below TZS 500,000 would reduce the backlog for small claims
- The insurer should partner with more repair garages outside Dar es Salaam to serve upcountry policyholders
- NHIF should implement real-time verification so hospitals can check member status instantly via mobile

### Product & Policy Design
- Insurance companies should offer smaller monthly premium options via mobile money to reach low-income customers
- Microinsurance products should have a simple one-page policy summary in Swahili with clear pictures
- Agricultural insurance should be index-based and linked to satellite rainfall data to remove the need for farm visits
- Insurance companies should offer bundled household cover that includes building, contents, and liability in one product
- Mobile insurance products should send the policyholder a Swahili SMS summary of their cover on activation
- NHIF should expand its network of accredited facilities in rural areas and smaller towns
- CHF premiums should be collectable via mobile money to simplify payment in rural communities
- Travel insurance should include coverage for safari activities as standard given Tanzania's tourism sector
- The insurer should offer a parametric livestock insurance product that pays out automatically when disease is declared
- Life insurance products should allow naming of child beneficiaries with a guardian trustee arrangement

### Communication & Transparency
- Insurers should be required to send annual benefit statements to all policyholders showing their current coverage and premiums paid
- All policy exclusions should be presented to the customer before purchase and require an explicit acknowledgement signature
- The insurer should send SMS reminders 30 days and 7 days before premium due dates
- Insurance certificates should include a QR code that can be scanned to verify authenticity
- All motor insurance certificates should be verifiable online by traffic police using the vehicle registration number
- TIRA should maintain a public register of licensed brokers and agents on its website
- Insurance companies should publish their claims rejection rates and average settlement times on TIRA's portal
- Insurers should be required to provide a dedicated complaints officer contact who responds within 72 hours

### Distribution & Access
- Insurance companies should work with mobile money providers to allow direct premium payment via M-Pesa and Airtel
- Bank branches should be used to cross-sell insurance products with transparent fee disclosure
- Village agents (wakala wa vijiji) should be trained and licensed to sell microinsurance in rural areas
- The insurer should open a claims processing desk in Mwanza, Arusha, and Mbeya for regional customers

**Policy Types**: Third-party motor (TPM), comprehensive motor, marine cargo, fire & allied perils, burglary, money insurance, fidelity guarantee, professional indemnity (PI), public liability, employer's liability, group life, group medical, term life, whole life, endowment, education policy, annuity, travel insurance, critical illness, personal accident, agricultural/crop, livestock, funeral insurance (bima ya mazishi), microinsurance, mobile insurance

**Key Terms**: Premium, excess/deductible, sum insured, indemnity, subrogation, utmost good faith (ubora wa imani), material fact, pre-existing condition, waiting period, free-look period, grace period, loading, exclusion clause, warranty, condition precedent, pro-rata premium, short-rate cancellation, total loss, constructive total loss (CTL), third-party liability, own damage, no-claims bonus (NCB), reinsurance treaty, facultative reinsurance

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. An insurance company denying a life insurance death benefit claim leaving a family without financial support — immediate regulatory escalation to TIRA
2. An agent confirmed to have collected premium payments and absconded with the money without remitting to the insurer — fraud, requires police and TIRA involvement
3. A patient denied medical treatment at an NHIF-accredited hospital due to disputed card status when genuine contributions are on record — health emergency
4. A policyholder involved in a serious accident (injuries, fatalities) where third-party claims are being denied or significantly delayed — victim welfare risk
5. Evidence of a fraudulent motor insurance certificate issued by an unlicensed agent — road safety risk, TIRA and police escalation required
6. A claim rejection based on a non-disclosed exclusion clause that a customer had no way of knowing about — potential unfair trade practice requiring TIRA complaint
7. An insurer that has been confirmed insolvent or is delaying claims across a large number of policyholders — systemic regulatory issue requiring TIRA escalation
8. An agricultural insurance company refusing to dispatch a loss assessor after a declared crop failure affecting smallholder farmers' livelihoods
9. A beneficiary of a life policy who is a minor or widow being deliberately delayed on death claim payment — vulnerable person protection escalation
10. A serious undisclosed pre-existing condition loading applied to a policyholder without disclosure — potential discrimination issue
11. Mobile money premium deductions continuing after a customer has cancelled their microinsurance policy — consumer protection breach
12. A hospital denying emergency treatment pending insurance pre-authorisation when the patient's life is at risk — clinical emergency overrides insurance process

## Disambiguation Notes

- Insurance feedback is distinguished from Healthcare feedback by the presence of insurance-specific terms (policy, premium, claim, excess, NHIF, cover note, beneficiary). A complaint about hospital billing rejection can be Healthcare or Insurance depending on whether the issue is clinical (Healthcare) or administrative insurance processing (Insurance).
- Motor insurance complaints may overlap with Transport/Automotive feedback. Distinguishing signals: terms like "claim," "loss adjuster," "comprehensive," "third party," and "excess" confirm Insurance classification. A complaint about a bad repair at a garage without insurance reference is Automotive.
- NHIF and CHF complaints are classified as Insurance when the issue is about coverage, card activation, contribution records, or claim reimbursement. If the issue is about quality of care delivered at the hospital itself, classify as Healthcare.
- Agricultural insurance complaints should be distinguished from general Agriculture/Farming feedback by the presence of policy, premium, claim, loss assessor, and sum insured keywords. Complaints about crop yields, soil, or inputs without mention of insurance belong to Agriculture.
- Life insurance endowment maturity complaints may overlap with Financial Services/Investment if the policyholder is treating the product as an investment vehicle. The presence of "policy," "premium," "insurer," and "beneficiary" confirms Insurance classification.
