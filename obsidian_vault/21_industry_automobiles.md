## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Automobiles / Motor Vehicles

### Dealership & Sales Improvements
- You should provide a pre-purchase inspection report from an independent mechanic for all used vehicles
- It would be useful if you offered a certified used vehicle program with a short warranty period
- Please create a clear breakdown of all charges before the sale — import duty, TRA fees, delivery charges
- A test drive on a proper road, not just the parking lot, would help buyers assess the vehicle properly
- Display the Japan/Singapore auction grade sheet for all imported vehicles so customers can verify condition
- It would help buyers if you provided the full service history of the vehicle before purchase
- Offer a buy-back guarantee within 30 days if major undisclosed defects are found post-purchase
- A comparison tool on your website showing similar models at different price points would help customers decide

### Garage & Spare Parts Improvements
- Please use only manufacturer-recommended oil grades and display which oil you use for each service
- A digital service history log accessible by the customer after each service visit would be very useful
- Offer a pick-up and drop-off service for vehicles that cannot be driven to the garage safely
- Please give customers a written estimate and get approval before starting any repair work
- Train your mechanics on diagnostic software for newer vehicle models — not just mechanical inspection
- Create a spare parts authenticity guarantee — if you are selling original parts, provide a certificate
- An online spare parts catalog with availability and prices would reduce unnecessary trips to the shop

### Petrol Station Improvements
- Install clearly visible digital pump displays so customers can see the reading from inside their vehicle
- Label all pump nozzles prominently — petrol in green, diesel in yellow — to prevent misfueling
- A loyalty card or points program for regular customers would encourage repeat business
- Provide receipts automatically without customers having to request them
- Calibrate pumps monthly and display the calibration certificate at each pump

### Transport & Bodaboda Improvements
- Please train bodaboda riders on traffic laws and proper road behavior as part of your onboarding
- Helmets for passengers should be mandatory and enforced by your platform — not optional
- A pricing transparency feature showing the estimated fare before the ride is confirmed would reduce disputes
- Implement a rating system that actually removes poorly rated riders from the platform
- Provide a clear escalation channel for safety complaints — currently there is no clear way to report incidents

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A vehicle sold by the dealership experienced brake failure causing an accident with injury or death
2. A customer reports that brake fluid, steering fluid, or coolant was not properly secured after garage service, leading to an accident
3. A bodaboda rider operated without a valid license, caused an accident, or assaulted a passenger
4. A fuel station dispensed contaminated fuel (water, wrong fuel type) resulting in engine damage or vehicle stalling on a highway
5. A vehicle was sold with a cloned or fraudulent chassis number — potential involvement in vehicle theft ring
6. A customer reports the garage kept their vehicle for over 90 days with no repair progress and is now uncontactable
7. A driving school instructor engaged in sexual harassment or physical misconduct toward a student
8. A vehicle inspection officer (SUMATRA) demanded a bribe to pass a vehicle that should have failed
9. A TRA registration officer altered vehicle import documents, leading to incorrect duty payment or fraudulent registration
10. An insurance claim involving personal injury or fatality remains unresolved for more than 6 months
11. A vehicle imported and sold was found to be on a stolen vehicle register (Interpol or origin country database)
12. A petrol station is confirmed to be adulterating fuel (diluting with water or kerosene) — public health and safety risk
13. A transport operator's truck was involved in a road accident due to known mechanical failure that was reported but not acted on

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Automobiles vs. Transport/Logistics**: If the complaint is about cargo not being delivered, freight charges, or goods damaged in transit, it belongs to transport/logistics KB. If it is about the vehicle itself (truck breakdown, driver conduct, fuel issues), it stays in the automobiles KB.
- **Automobiles vs. Insurance (General)**: Vehicle insurance complaints sit firmly in automobiles KB. Non-vehicle insurance (health, life, property) belongs to the finance/insurance KB. The distinguishing signal is the subject: vehicle damage, accident, theft of a car.
- **Automobiles vs. Government Services**: TRA and SUMATRA processes are regulatory touchpoints in the auto purchase journey. If the complaint is specifically about corruption, systemic delay, or policy at TRA/SUMATRA as government bodies, it may need to be escalated to a government services category. If the complaint is about a vehicle dealer's failure to complete TRA paperwork on the customer's behalf, it stays in automobiles.
- **Bodaboda vs. Ride-Hailing Apps**: Bodaboda complaints about physical safety, vehicle condition, or rider behavior stay in automobiles KB. If the complaint is about the app itself (payment, navigation, account issues), consider routing to tech/platform KB.
- **Fuel Stations vs. Utilities**: Fuel quality and quantity disputes belong to automobiles KB. A complaint about TANESCO power to a fuel station is a utilities issue. Fuel price complaints may have dual routing — automobiles KB for the experience, but may require escalation to government if price cap violations are suspected.
