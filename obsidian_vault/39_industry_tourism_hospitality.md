## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Tourism / Hospitality

### Guest Experience & Service
- The hotel should implement a mobile check-in option so guests do not have to queue at the front desk
- Lodges should provide a printed daily activity schedule so guests can plan their time without asking staff
- The hotel restaurant should offer a broader menu variety so guests staying for more than three nights have options
- Tour operators should provide guests with a detailed written itinerary at least seven days before travel
- Hotels should have a dedicated complaints handling officer available 24 hours a day during peak season
- Guides should pass a certified wildlife knowledge test before being permitted to lead international tourists
- The lodge should provide a cultural orientation session for international visitors on first arrival
- Hotels should create a multilingual welcome pack covering local transport, safety, and cultural norms
- A real-time feedback button in the room would allow guests to report issues immediately before they escalate
- The hotel should proactively tell guests about included services (laundry, transfers, park fees) to avoid confusion

### Safety & Compliance
- All boats used for tourist excursions should be required to carry and display their maximum passenger capacity
- Diving operators should be required to provide proof of equipment certification before any tourist dive
- Kilimanjaro trekking operators should mandate minimum crew training in altitude sickness first aid
- Hotels should install and maintain proper emergency lighting along all pathways to rooms and amenities
- Zanzibar beach hotels should post signage about currents and ocean conditions at the beach entry points
- All tourist vehicles should be inspected annually and display a valid mechanical certificate
- The park authority should require game drive vehicles to carry first aid kits on every excursion
- Tour guides operating in marine reserves should hold a certified marine safety and first aid qualification
- Hotels should provide a property map at check-in highlighting emergency exits and assembly points

### Sustainability & Responsible Tourism
- The lodge should eliminate single-use plastic bottles and provide refillable water stations instead
- Safari vehicles should be electric or hybrid to reduce emissions in national parks
- The hotel should source its produce from local farmers and put this on the menu to support the community
- Cultural tourism operators should ensure that village visits are conducted respectfully and benefit the host community
- The lodge should implement a lights-out policy after 10pm near wildlife corridors to reduce disturbance
- Tour operators should educate guests on responsible wildlife viewing — no flash photography, no feeding animals
- Hotels near coral reefs should conduct mandatory reef briefings before allowing guests to snorkel or dive
- The resort should have a visible sustainability policy and publish its water and energy usage annually

### Technology & Booking
- Tour operators should use an online booking management system that sends automatic confirmation emails
- Hotels should allow guests to view and settle their final bill digitally on the day of checkout
- TANAPA should introduce an online park booking and entry system to reduce congestion at main gates
- Tanzania Tourist Board should maintain a verified and publicly searchable register of licensed tour operators
- A dedicated tourist app for Zanzibar showing maps, verified operators, tide times, and emergency contacts would be very useful
- Hotels should integrate with international booking platforms but honour those rates without adding surcharges

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A tourist reporting physical assault, robbery, or sexual harassment by a tour guide, hotel staff, or person within the tourism facility — immediate tourist police and operator escalation
2. A wildlife safety incident — tourist in direct danger from wildlife due to operator negligence (inadequate vehicle, forced walking near dangerous animals, tent failure in wildlife area)
3. A boat or vessel incident involving tourists with no life jackets or risk of capsizing — immediate maritime safety escalation
4. Altitude sickness emergency on Kilimanjaro or other mountain where the operator is refusing to evacuate or does not have a plan — medical emergency
5. A tourist reporting their passport or travel documents have been taken by a hotel, tour operator, or person claiming to be a government official — document seizure, requires police and embassy notification
6. Evidence of a fraudulent tour operator collecting payments without a TTB licence — consumer fraud with potential to affect multiple tourists
7. A food poisoning incident affecting multiple guests at the same hotel or restaurant — public health emergency requiring health authority notification
8. A tourist reporting they are stranded with no means of return transport, no money, and no reachable operator contact — immediate welfare emergency
9. A report of child exploitation or suspected trafficking linked to tourism facilities — requires police and social protection escalation
10. A diving incident involving equipment failure or decompression sickness with no dive operator emergency plan — medical emergency
11. A solo traveller who booked through an operator and has not made contact with their family for an unexpectedly long period — welfare check required
12. A tourist being extorted by persons falsely claiming to be tourist police or government officials — requires genuine police notification and tourist police unit involvement
13. A report of fire or structural collapse at a tourism facility with guests present — emergency services escalation required

## Disambiguation Notes

- Tourism and Hospitality feedback is distinguished from general Retail/Service feedback by the presence of sector-specific vocabulary: safari, lodge, game drive, tour guide, national park, beach resort, Zanzibar, Kilimanjaro, park fees, visa, itinerary. A complaint about a restaurant is hospitality only if it is attached to a hotel or tourism experience context.
- Hotel feedback may overlap with Real Estate if the guest is a long-stay tenant or corporate resident rather than a tourist. Short-stay, nightly rates, room service, and tour activities confirm Tourism/Hospitality classification.
- Transport complaints within a safari or tour package belong to Tourism/Hospitality classification even though transport is a separate industry. The key is whether the transport is part of a tourism service (transfer, game drive vehicle, charter flight) versus standalone transport.
- Payment and pricing disputes involving foreign currency (USD/EUR) are common in Tourism and should not be classified as Banking/Finance unless the complaint involves a financial institution (credit card dispute, forex fraud). USD billing disputes in a hotel context remain Tourism feedback.
- Wildlife-related feedback (conservation, poaching, ecology) may look like Environment or Agriculture feedback. If the context involves a tourist's experience in a national park or game reserve, it is Tourism. If it is from a farmer reporting crop damage by wildlife, it belongs to Agriculture.
