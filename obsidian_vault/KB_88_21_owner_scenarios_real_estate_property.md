### Scenario 21-1: Unknown Property Sale Executed Using Stolen Identity (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** DOJ USAO-CDCA federal press release, January 2024. A licensed real estate broker and two co-defendants were arrested for fraudulently selling a $1.5 million Burbank, California home they did not own, using stolen identities to obtain a near-$1 million loan against the property. The legitimate owner had no knowledge the property was in a transaction.
**The Problem:** A homeowner in California receives an alert from her title insurer that a transfer deed has been recorded against her property. She authorized no sale. A loan of nearly $1 million has been taken against the property in her name by parties she does not recognize. She did not contact any real estate agent or lender. Her identity has been used to execute the entire transaction without her presence.
**What staff/org would do:** The county recorder's office says they can only accept title correction requests in writing with notarized documentation sent by mail. The lender's fraud line puts her on hold for 47 minutes before disconnecting. The FBI tip line instructs her to file an IC3 report online with no follow-up timeline given. The title insurer says they will open a claim but cannot freeze the transaction until legal review, which takes 6 to 8 weeks.
**What the Owner does:** Treats this as an active financial emergency. Confirms the homeowner's situation, validates the severity of what has happened, and immediately outlines the parallel actions required across law enforcement, county recorder, lender fraud, and title insurer in a prioritized order.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true
- Opening reply: "This is a serious property fraud emergency. Your identity has been used without your consent to sell or borrow against your home. You need to act on several fronts simultaneously — I will walk you through each step right now."
- Direct to: FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3.gov) for federal fraud report; DOJ USAO-CDCA if in California; county recorder's office for immediate title freeze request via certified written demand; lender fraud and identity theft department for emergency loan suspension; title insurer for emergency claim opening; CFPB complaint portal (consumerfinance.gov/complaint) if the lender is unresponsive; Federal Trade Commission identity theft report (identitytheft.gov) to establish an official record
- Evidence to collect: Deed of transfer (pull as public record from county recorder); any loan documents or correspondence sent to your address; proof of continuous residence and ownership such as utility bills, tax notices, and prior mortgage statements; government-issued ID confirming your identity; a timeline of when you first learned of the transaction and through which channel
- Feedback category tag: Financial Fraud / Identity-Based Property Crime

### Scenario 21-2: Property Manager Stealing Rent from Elderly and Disabled Tenants (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** Manhattan DA press release, April 2024. Shatina Howell, property manager at Ennis Francis Apartments (Section 8, Central Harlem), stole 91 money orders from approximately 41 veterans, elderly, and disabled low-income tenants between May 2021 and August 2022, then continued the scheme at other Harlem properties. Total theft: $56,412. Tenants discovered the losses only when eviction notices arrived for non-payment of rent they believed they had paid. Howell specifically targeted tenants with health issues, offering to fill out and deliver their money orders on their behalf.
**The Problem:** An elderly veteran living in a Section 8 housing complex in Central Harlem has just received an eviction notice for four months of non-payment. He is certain he paid — every month he physically handed his rent money orders to the property manager, who took them and recorded them in a ledger. He has no receipts. The property manager is not answering his calls. He does not understand why he is facing eviction for rent he is sure he paid, and his mobility limits make it difficult to attend in-person hearings.
**What staff/org would do:** The housing office instructs him to appear in person during office hours with his original money orders as proof of payment — the very documents that were taken from him. The eviction notice processing clerk says proceedings cannot be paused without a court order. No one in the agency recognizes the pattern as potential theft.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately recognizes the vulnerability and urgency. Validates his payment history, flags this as a theft pattern consistent with documented cases, and prioritizes stopping the eviction while simultaneously initiating a criminal fraud report.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true
- Opening reply: "You should not be facing eviction for rent you paid. Handing money orders directly to a property manager and receiving no receipts is consistent with a documented fraud pattern in which managers alter or redirect those money orders. This is an emergency on two tracks: stopping the eviction and reporting the theft."
- Direct to: Manhattan District Attorney's office or local DA fraud hotline; NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) tenant helpline (212-863-7900); Legal Aid Society housing unit for emergency eviction defense; the Section 8 housing authority administering the subsidy contract — they can verify payment records independently of the on-site manager; NYPD for a criminal report; Adult Protective Services if the tenant has cognitive or mobility vulnerability that was exploited
- Evidence to collect: Any photos taken of money orders before handing them over; bank withdrawal records or post office receipts showing when and where each money order was purchased; any text messages or written communications with the property manager; testimony from neighbors who experienced the same issue; original lease and housing subsidy documentation; dates and amounts of every month you paid
- Feedback category tag: Staff Misconduct / Financial Theft from Vulnerable Tenants

### Scenario 21-3: Real Estate Agent Refuses to Discuss Competing Loan Options on Instructions from Employer (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** CFPB federal complaint filed December 23, 2024, Eastern District of Michigan, against Rocket Homes Real Estate LLC and The Jason Mitchell Group. Between 2019 and 2024, agents were institutionally instructed to steer buyers exclusively to Rocket Mortgage and affiliated settlement company Amrock, and were prohibited from informing consumers about USDA rural loans, down-payment assistance programs, and manufactured home loans — an alleged violation of RESPA (Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act).
**The Problem:** A first-time homebuyer in rural Michigan is working with an agent from a nationally branded real estate network. She has asked twice about USDA rural loans, which would allow her to buy with zero down payment, and about first-time buyer assistance programs. Both times the agent changed the subject and redirected her to the affiliated lender's pre-approval form. She senses she is not receiving complete information but cannot identify why, and she is worried that signing with the affiliated lender will cost her significantly more than alternatives she has not been shown.
**What staff/org would do:** The agent reassures her that the affiliated lender is "the easiest and best option" and that down-payment assistance programs "slow down closings." The company's main customer service number routes to a general sales line that confirms only affiliated products.
**What the Owner does:** Recognizes this as a documented institutional information-withholding pattern. Validates her instinct, explains that federal law requires agents to act in her interest and not filter options for commercial reasons, and provides the independent information she has been denied.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- Opening reply: "Your instinct is right. Federal law under RESPA requires that agents act in your interest and not withhold mortgage options from you in exchange for referral incentives. USDA rural loans and down-payment assistance programs are legitimate products you are entitled to know about, and you are being directed away from them."
- Direct to: CFPB consumer complaint portal (consumerfinance.gov/complaint) — the CFPB is the exact enforcement body that investigated and filed suit over this practice in December 2024; USDA Rural Development loan locator (rd.usda.gov) for independent eligibility check; HUD-approved housing counselor for free independent mortgage advice (800-569-4287); your state's housing finance agency for down-payment assistance program listings; your state real estate licensing board for an agent conduct complaint; a second independent agent or mortgage broker for a comparison quote
- Evidence to collect: Written record of the questions you asked and what the agent told you; any email or text exchanges where specific loan products were raised; pre-approval offers and documents received from the affiliated lender; any referral fee disclosures or absence of disclosures from the agent; the agent's brokerage and license number from their business card or the listing agreement
- Feedback category tag: Information Withholding / Agent Misconduct / Consumer Financial Rights

### Scenario 21-4: Landlord Changes All Locks While Tenant Is at Work (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** UK Crown Court and Landlord Licensing and Defence legal case record. Mirza Tariq Mahmud Jaral, a Peterborough landlord, changed all locks to his rental property while the tenant was at work in July 2020, removing the tenant's possessions and rendering him immediately homeless. The tenant held a valid tenancy with no rent arrears. Jaral was sentenced in 2022 to 12 months imprisonment after proceedings spanning nearly two years.
**The Problem:** A tenant in Peterborough has returned from a nine-hour work shift to find his key does not open his front door. His clothes, documents, phone charger, and passport are inside. A handwritten note on the door says the landlord has "taken back" the property. It is 9:30 PM. He has no immediate family in the city, no alternative accommodation, and a valid tenancy agreement showing he is not in arrears. The landlord is not answering calls.
**What staff/org would do:** The police non-emergency line (101) says this is a civil matter and advises him to contact a solicitor. The council housing emergency line puts him on hold for 22 minutes before a recorded message instructs him to call back during office hours. The landlord does not respond to any calls.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately confirms this is a criminal offence and treats the call as a housing emergency requiring same-night action, not an administrative matter to be deferred.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true
- Opening reply: "What your landlord has done is a criminal offence under Section 1 of the Protection from Eviction Act 1977. Changing your locks without a court order while you have a valid tenancy is illegal eviction. Tonight's first priority is getting you shelter and beginning the legal process — this cannot wait until office hours."
- Direct to: Police (999 if you feel unsafe, 101 to report illegal eviction — officers can attend and in some cases require re-entry to be granted that night); local council housing emergency line — under the Homelessness Reduction Act 2017 the council has a legal duty to assist you tonight; Shelter emergency helpline (0808 800 4444, free, available 24 hours); Safer Renting charity, which specializes in illegal eviction support; Citizens Advice housing emergency line; a housing solicitor or Legal Aid provider for an emergency injunction application to the county court requiring immediate re-entry — this remedy is available under civil law alongside the criminal prosecution
- Evidence to collect: Photograph the locked door and any note left by the landlord; photograph your tenancy agreement showing valid lease dates and rent payment history; bank statements showing rent paid and received; any prior threatening messages from the landlord by text, email, or voicemail; names and contact details of neighbours who can confirm you were living there and were at work tonight; a full log of every call you make to police, council, and Shelter tonight with times and outcomes
- Feedback category tag: Illegal Eviction / Housing Emergency

### Scenario 21-5: Sale-and-Rent-Back Scheme with Concealed Fees and Subsequent Eviction (Pattern A+B)
**Real Basis:** FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) press release and court record. Rajinder Gill, operating as Secure Property Consultants Ltd, targeted financially distressed homeowners across England between 2014 and 2018. Victims sold at below market value on the promise of remaining as tenants; undisclosed and excessive fees totaling £925,233 were extracted. Multiple victims later faced eviction from their own former homes. The judge described "a pattern of systematic exploitation." Gill was sentenced in March 2026 to 2.5 years imprisonment plus an 8-year director disqualification.
**The Problem:** A homeowner in the English Midlands in financial difficulty was approached six months ago by a company offering to purchase her home rapidly for cash and allow her to remain as a tenant. She signed the agreement and the sale proceeded. She has now received a tenancy termination notice and cannot reconcile the fees deducted from her sale proceeds — they total more than £40,000 that she was never told about. The company's representative is reading a clause from the contract she was not directed to at signing and telling her she has 28 days to vacate her former home.
**What staff/org would do:** The company's representative reads the buried clause and terminates the call. The head office routes her to a general voicemail that is never returned. The solicitor who handled the original transaction — recommended by the purchasing company — says there was nothing improper about the process.
**What the Owner does:** Recognizes this as the documented sale-and-rent-back exploitation pattern the FCA has prosecuted and identifies the specific regulatory and legal remedies available to challenge both the undisclosed fees and the eviction.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- Opening reply: "Being charged fees you were never told about and then receiving an eviction notice from your own former home matches a fraud pattern the FCA has investigated and sent people to prison for. You have legal options to challenge the fees and to contest the eviction notice, and the FCA is the right starting point."
- Direct to: FCA consumer helpline (0800 111 6768) — the FCA regulates sale-and-rent-back schemes under specific rules and has prosecuted exactly this type of undisclosed-fee case; Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) if any financial product or regulated activity was involved; Shelter housing advice line (0808 800 4444) for immediate tenancy rights review and eviction notice challenge; Citizens Advice for contract review and legal aid eligibility assessment; Trading Standards via your local council for consumer contract fraud relating to hidden charges; a housing solicitor for an injunction against the eviction notice pending resolution of the fee dispute; check whether the solicitor who acted for you has a conflict of interest that should be reported to the Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA)
- Evidence to collect: The full original sale-and-rent-back agreement including all schedules; the itemized fee breakdown as provided by the company; any independent property valuation obtained at the time or since; all correspondence with the company from first contact to today; bank records showing actual proceeds received versus the amount expected; the solicitor's name, firm, and any documentation showing they were introduced by the purchasing company
- Feedback category tag: Financial Fraud / Predatory Contract / Housing Rights

### Scenario 21-6: Social Landlord Refuses to Acknowledge or Escalate Tenant Repair Complaint (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** UK Housing Ombudsman Annual Complaints Review 2024-25. 7,082 formal determinations, 71% overall uphold rate, £5.4 million in compensation ordered. 175 Complaint Handling Failure Orders (CHFOs) issued for delayed acknowledgment, failure to communicate outcomes, and refusal to escalate complaints to the Ombudsman stage. Local authorities had an 87% uphold rate on complaint-handling disputes — meaning in nearly 9 out of 10 cases where a tenant said their complaint was mishandled, the Ombudsman agreed.
**The Problem:** A social housing tenant in a London local authority flat has been reporting black mould on the bedroom ceiling and a broken boiler since October. It is now January. She has called the repairs line seven times. She has been given reference numbers that lead nowhere. When she asked for her complaint to be escalated to management, she was told "we do not escalate repair requests, it is in the queue." Her most recent call ended with an agent telling her the case is "under review" with no timeline given. A letter has now arrived stating her complaint has been formally closed as "resolved" — but no repair has been made. Her young child has developed a persistent cough.
**What staff/org would do:** The next call is answered by a different agent with no record of her case who asks her to start again from the beginning. The supervisor is unavailable. When she asks about the closed complaint, the agent says she will need to submit a new complaint form.
**What the Owner does:** Identifies this as a documented complaint-handling failure pattern that the Housing Ombudsman specifically sanctions and explains the formal escalation route that bypasses the landlord entirely.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- Opening reply: "Seven calls, a closed complaint with no repair made, and an agent with no record of your case — this is exactly the pattern the UK Housing Ombudsman is set up to address, and landlords who handle complaints this way are being formally sanctioned. You no longer need to keep calling the same line. There is an escalation route that bypasses your landlord entirely."
- Direct to: UK Housing Ombudsman (housing-ombudsman.org.uk, 0300 111 3000) — if your landlord has not resolved the complaint within 8 weeks, or has formally closed it without resolution, you can submit directly to the Ombudsman without further referral; the Ombudsman's own 2024-25 data shows 87% of complaint-handling disputes against local authorities are upheld; your local councillor's surgery — councillors can contact housing departments directly and often achieve rapid action that the tenant cannot; Environmental Health at the same local council (a separate department from housing management) for a damp and mould inspection under the Housing Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS); if your child's respiratory symptoms are documented by a GP as linked to housing conditions, that medical evidence significantly strengthens an Ombudsman case
- Evidence to collect: All reference numbers issued from each of the seven calls, with the date and name of the agent spoken to where known; the letter formally closing the complaint as resolved; photographs of the mould with timestamps on each image; any written communications from the landlord by letter, email, or app message; GP or hospital records linking your child's respiratory condition to mould exposure; a written timeline of every interaction with the landlord from October to today
- Feedback category tag: Call Center Failure / Complaint Handling Refusal / Housing Disrepair

### Scenario 21-7: Indigenous Community Loses Access to Ancestral Grazing Land Amid Security Force Violence (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** Amnesty International report "We Have Lost Everything: Forced Evictions of the Maasai in Loliondo, Tanzania," June 2023, independently confirmed by UN OHCHR press release June 2022 and Al Jazeera 2024 follow-up. On June 10, 2022, security forces descended on Loliondo division and began demarcating 1,500 square kilometers of Maasai ancestral territory as Protected Area. When community members gathered to protest, security forces shot and teargassed them, injuring at least 40 people. An 84-year-old community member was shot in both legs and subsequently disappeared. 27 Maasai were arbitrarily arrested. Approximately 70,000 Maasai lost access to grazing lands; the territory was licensed to a private trophy hunting company.
**The Problem:** A Maasai community leader in Loliondo has received a government notice declaring that the grazing corridors his community has used for generations are now a Protected Area. Security personnel have begun demarcating the boundary. Community members who approached to observe were shot at with live rounds. One elder is missing. Livestock cannot access the dry-season grazing lands. The community cannot identify any legal process that preceded the demarcation or any domestic avenue to file a formal objection — community leaders who tried to report through government channels were among those arrested.
**What staff/org would do:** The district government office says the decision originated in Dar es Salaam and cannot be reversed at local level. The regional commissioner's office does not respond. Media and NGO access to the area was explicitly blocked during and after the evictions.
**What the Owner does:** Recognizes this as a documented state-sanctioned land rights violation with international legal dimensions, identifies the bodies already engaged with this specific situation, and prioritizes both the missing person and the land rights complaint simultaneously.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true
- Opening reply: "What your community is experiencing — forced removal from ancestral land to benefit a commercial operator, with live fire used against those who protest and an elder missing — has been documented by Amnesty International and reviewed by the United Nations. International bodies are already engaged with this specific case. Here is how to reach them and what to document immediately."
- Direct to: Amnesty International Tanzania contact (amnesty.org) — they have documented this case in detail and have ongoing engagement with the community; UN Special Rapporteur on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples via the OHCHR communications procedure (ohchr.org) — this is the UN body that issued warnings specifically about Loliondo; African Commission on Human and Peoples' Rights (ACHPR) in Banjul — land rights cases are admissible under the African Charter on Human and Peoples' Rights; African Court on Human and Peoples' Rights — Tanzania has accepted the court's jurisdiction over individual and NGO communications; Oakland Institute and Survival International for ongoing international advocacy and media documentation; for the missing elder — document full details immediately and report to OHCHR's enforced disappearances mechanism in parallel
- Evidence to collect: Any government demarcation notices or written communications; photographs and video of security forces and boundary marking activity where it is safe to document; medical records for injured community members with dates and descriptions of injuries; names, ages, and last known locations of any missing or arrested individuals with witness contacts; historical land use documentation including maps, livestock movement records, community oral testimony on record, and any prior government correspondence acknowledging the community's use of the land
- Feedback category tag: Indigenous Land Rights / State-Sanctioned Forced Eviction / Human Rights Violation

### Scenario 21-8: Government Demolition of Long-Term Residents' Homes with 48-Hour Notice (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** Human Rights Watch report "I Escaped with Only My Life," May 2024, based on 25 interviews conducted September 2023 to April 2024. Karachi Metropolitan Corporation demolished approximately 12,000 houses in the Gujjar Nullah waterway area in November 2021 under a Supreme Court directive, displacing approximately 96,000 people with minimal notice and no adequate resettlement. Resident Zakia Bibi died of a heart attack four days after her home was demolished. Residents who contacted the city authority referencing the Court directive found their complaints closed without action.
**The Problem:** A 52-year-old diabetic woman in an informal settlement in Karachi has just been told by a city official that her home will be demolished in 48 hours as part of a waterway clearance order. She has lived there for 23 years and has paid municipal electricity and gas bills throughout that time. She has no formal land title. She has two adult children with no income. She asks about resettlement assistance; the official says it will be arranged "through the scheme." When she asks for details of the scheme, no one at the office can provide them. The telephone number on the notice rings unanswered.
**What staff/org would do:** The city office hands her a printed notice referencing the Supreme Court order and tells her the process is "in accordance with the law." There is no independent complaint mechanism available at local level and no resettlement office she can visit.
**What the Owner does:** Addresses the human emergency first, then identifies the legal and humanitarian channels available even under the constraint of a court order, and clarifies that a court order does not extinguish the right to adequate notice, documentation, and fair compensation.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- Opening reply: "A 48-hour demolition notice after 23 years of residence — including utility bills that demonstrate your longstanding tenure — does not mean you have no rights. Even under court-ordered demolitions, you are entitled to documentation, to challenge the adequacy of resettlement, and to have your case heard. Here is what to do right now."
- Direct to: Human Rights Commission of Pakistan (HRCP, hrcp.org.pk) — an independent body that has documented this exact demolition pattern in Karachi and can provide rapid legal referrals; Pakistan Legal Aid Foundation or the local bar association for an emergency legal opinion on whether due process requirements were met before demolition; Edhi Foundation or Chhipa Welfare for immediate emergency shelter if demolition proceeds; National Commission for Human Rights Pakistan (NCHR) for a formal complaint; UN Special Rapporteur on the Right to Adequate Housing (ohchr.org) — this mandate specifically covers displacement without adequate resettlement; keep all utility bills as evidence of established occupancy for any future compensation claim
- Evidence to collect: All utility bills for the past several years showing your address and continuous payment history; the demolition notice itself (photograph it if you cannot keep the original); any previous written interactions with local government or the city about your property; medical documentation of diabetes and any dependents' health vulnerabilities to support a compensation claim on grounds of protected status; photographs of the property documenting its condition, age, and established character; names and contact details of neighbours who can provide corroborating testimony
- Feedback category tag: Forced Displacement / Government Demolition / Inadequate Resettlement

### Scenario 21-9: Home Buyer's Deposit Stolen Through Email Payment Redirection Scam (Pattern A+E)
**Real Basis:** ACCC media release and ABC Australia news report, July 2024. A home buyer's $26,000 property deposit was intercepted by scammers who compromised email communications between buyer and real estate agent, substituted their own bank account details in payment instructions, and the deposit was transferred to a fraudulent account. The ACCC reported Australian businesses lost over AUD $14 million to payment redirection scams, with real estate identified as a top targeted sector. ACCC's own guidance specifically identifies a verbal phone call to confirm bank details before transfer as the countermeasure — directly acknowledging that the communication breakdown between buyer, agent, and bank was the core failure.
**The Problem:** A homebuyer in Sydney followed emailed payment instructions and transferred her $26,000 deposit to what she believed was the real estate agent's trust account. She is now at settlement and the agent has no record of receiving the deposit. Re-examining the email, she can see the sender address was spoofed — a single character different from the agent's real address. When she called her bank immediately after discovering the fraud, she was put on hold for 34 minutes and told the transfer was "authorized" and therefore not the bank's liability. The funds cleared three days ago and have moved internationally.
**What staff/org would do:** The bank's fraud line says she authorized the transfer and declines liability. The real estate agency's principal says verifying bank details is her responsibility. The police non-emergency line says to file online and she will be contacted within 10 business days.
**What the Owner does:** Moves immediately to pursue the fastest available recovery channels while helping her understand the regulatory complaints routes that can compel bank action even after they have declined.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- Opening reply: "Your deposit has been stolen through a payment interception scam — you received a legitimate-looking email from a spoofed address and followed what appeared to be real instructions. This is not your fault, and there are steps to take right now that may still allow partial recovery. The first 72 hours matter."
- Direct to: Your bank's fraud department — request an urgent international payment recall and reference the Scam Safe Accord, under which Australian banks have recovery obligations for scam losses; AFCA (Australian Financial Complaints Authority, afca.org.au) if the bank declines to assist — AFCA can compel banks to demonstrate they met their Scam Safe Accord obligations, and the Ombudsman has the power to award compensation; ACCC Scamwatch (scamwatch.gov.au) — report immediately as it contributes to national fraud intelligence and strengthens AFCA complaints; Australian Federal Police cybercrime portal (cyber.gov.au) for a formal criminal report; your state fair trading authority for a complaint against the agent if the email compromise occurred via their systems, which may constitute a duty of care failure; the real estate agent's professional indemnity insurer
- Evidence to collect: The original spoofed email in its original format — do not delete it, and preserve the full email headers which show technical routing details critical for law enforcement; a screenshot of the spoofed sender address next to the agent's real address to document the character substitution; your bank's transfer confirmation and receipt with timestamp; the agent's real email address from a prior confirmed communication before the scam; your property contract showing the correct trust account details; any phone communications with the agent in the days before and after the transfer
- Feedback category tag: Payment Fraud / Cyber-Enabled Property Scam / Financial Loss

### Scenario 21-10: Farmers Coerced into Signing Land Terminations Under Police Supervision (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** Uzbek Forum for Human Rights and Ulster University joint report "A False Sense of Legality," December 2024. The report documents forced evictions across 11 urban development projects in Tashkent and Samarkand displacing over 10,000 people, and agricultural land seizures in 15 districts across six regions where farmers were coerced into signing "voluntary" land lease terminations at police-supervised meetings. Some farmers were escorted by officers late at night to sign documents. Farmers who raised questions at meetings were removed by officers.
**The Problem:** A farmer in a rural district outside Tashkent has been summoned, along with his neighbours, to a meeting at the local district administration office. Police officers are present in the room. An official places a document in front of him described as a "voluntary termination" of his agricultural land lease and tells him to sign. He is not told what the land will be used for or what compensation he will receive. His neighbours who asked questions were escorted out of the room by officers. He was visited at his home by officials the night before. He does not know whether he has any legal right to refuse or what will happen if he does.
**What staff/org would do:** The district office says the development project is a government priority and the process is "in accordance with the law." There is no independent complaint mechanism accessible at local level. Raising objections at the meeting itself results in removal from the room.
**What the Owner does:** Affirms his rights without endangering him further, identifies the organizations that are already documenting this exact pattern in his region, and provides concrete steps he can take to preserve his legal position whether or not he signs under duress.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- Opening reply: "Signing a document described as 'voluntary' while police officers are in the room and your neighbours are being removed for asking questions is not voluntary under any legal standard — it is coercion. Organizations have already documented this exact pattern in your region and are collecting testimonies. Here is what you can do to protect yourself, including if you are forced to sign."
- Direct to: Uzbek Forum for Human Rights (uzbekforum.org) — they specifically documented midnight visits and police-supervised signings in rural Uzbekistan and are actively collecting testimonies from affected farmers; UN Special Rapporteur on the Right to Food and UN Special Rapporteur on Adequate Housing (ohchr.org) — both mandates cover agricultural land seizure; Human Rights Watch Central Asia team; if signing is unavoidable, write the words "signed under duress" in your own handwriting anywhere on the page before handing it back, and photograph the document if it is safe to do so; do not sign a blank or incomplete document under any circumstances; seek any independent legal counsel — the Tashkent Bar Association or the Academic Research and Information Centre (ARIC) may offer referrals
- Evidence to collect: Photograph the document before and after signing if your phone is with you and it is safe to do so; note the full names, titles, and any badge numbers of every official and officer present at the meeting; record any verbal promises made about compensation (amount, form, timeline); names and contact details of neighbouring farmers who attended the same meeting and can provide corroborating testimony; documentation of any overnight visit — date, time, how many officials came, and precisely what was said; any written summons or notification you received about the meeting

- Feedback category tag: Land Rights Coercion / Government Misconduct / Rural Property Rights
