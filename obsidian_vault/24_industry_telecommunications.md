## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

- SIM swap fraud reported — customer's number hijacked to steal mobile money funds
- Unauthorized porting of a customer's number to a competing network
- Customer reports mobile money fraud — airtime or cash stolen via SIM cloning
- Complete tower outage affecting emergency services (police, ambulance, fire) communication
- Customer unable to call 112 emergency number due to network failure
- TCRA-mandated SIM re-registration causing mass account deactivations affecting thousands
- Deliberate network throttling suspected during national election or political event
- Data breach reported — customer personal information exposed from telecom systems
- Signal jamming incident reported near sensitive government installations
- Repeated billing fraud: unauthorized VAS charges confirmed across multiple accounts affecting large number of subscribers
- Customer's M-Pesa account drained after SIM cloning — financial loss exceeding TZS 500,000
- Network failure in hospital zone preventing medical communications
- Roaming fraud charges exceeding TZS 200,000 without customer consent

## Disambiguation Notes

- Telecom complaints about **mobile money** (M-Pesa, Airtel Money) may overlap with **Banking / Finance** industry — classify as Telecom if the payment failure is on the telecom operator side (SIM-based), and Finance if the issue involves the linked bank account or regulated banking product.
- **TV and decoder** complaints (DSTV, Startimes, Azam TV) are part of Telecom/Media — distinguish from general **Electronics** complaints by the presence of broadcast/subscription channel references rather than hardware-only device malfunction.
- Internet complaints referencing **office networks, VPNs, and server connectivity** may overlap with **IT / Software** industry — classify as Telecom if the ISP or last-mile connection (fiber/4G) is the primary subject.
- **USSD complaints** are firmly Telecom-domain even if they relate to financial transactions — the delivery channel is the classifier.
- Complaints about **electricity affecting internet/router** should be classified as Telecom if the primary frustration is about the internet service, and Utilities/Power if about the electricity infrastructure itself.
