## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Construction / Real Estate Development

### Process & Project Management
- "You should use project management software so clients can track progress in real time"
- "Weekly site meetings with the client should be mandatory in the contract"
- "A detailed BOQ should be shared before any work begins — not after disputes arise"
- "Milestone-based payment schedules should replace lump-sum advances"
- "A formal snag list inspection before handover should be standard practice"
- "Consider using certified materials suppliers to prevent use of substandard inputs"
- "A third-party structural inspection at the foundation stage would catch problems early"
- "Provide clients with a WhatsApp group for real-time site photo updates"
- "Site diaries recording daily progress, labor, and materials used should be maintained"
- "A clear variation order process should be defined in every contract"

### Quality & Standards
- "All contractors should be required to show NSSF registration and BRELA certificate"
- "Material testing certificates (cement, rebar) should be provided before use on site"
- "Consider using locally available natural stone for foundations — more durable than hollow blocks"
- "Buildings above G+2 should require a registered structural engineer's stamp"
- "Reinforcement bar spacing should be verified by an independent engineer before the slab pour"
- "Using a concrete pump for slabs would reduce water-cement ratio inconsistencies"

### Safety & Environment
- "Mandatory PPE (hard hats, gloves, boots, vests) should be enforced for all site workers"
- "Designated construction waste disposal areas should be identified before the project starts"
- "Dust suppression measures like water spraying should be used near residential areas"
- "Site security lighting should be installed to prevent theft and trespassing at night"
- "Safety induction for all new workers before they start on site is good practice"

### Client Experience
- "A dedicated client relationship manager assigned per project would greatly improve communication"
- "After-sales service with a 12-month defect liability period should be offered by all developers"
- "A client portal showing project photos, materials used, and progress percentage would build trust"
- "Consider offering shell-and-core options for clients with smaller budgets"
- "Provide translated versions of contracts in Swahili for clients who are not fluent in English"
- "A housewarming inspection checklist given to clients at handover would reduce disputes"

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. "Scaffolding collapsed and workers were injured on site" — workplace safety incident requiring OSHA reporting
2. "Worker died on site due to fall or structural collapse" — fatal accident requiring police, OSHA, and insurance notification
3. "Building collapsed during or after construction — residents inside" — immediate emergency and regulatory alert
4. "Contractor found using counterfeit or below-spec structural materials (substandard rebar, weak cement)" — public safety risk requiring Engineering board notification
5. "Excavation trench collapse trapping workers" — emergency rescue and site shutdown
6. "Developer sold the same property to multiple buyers" — fraud requiring police and BRELA report
7. "Building constructed without any approved permit on public or protected land" — municipal enforcement trigger
8. "Asbestos materials used in roofing or insulation without disclosure" — health and environmental hazard
9. "Untreated construction waste (chemicals, paints) dumped into drainage or water source" — NEMC violation
10. "Child fell into unguarded open excavation on or near the site" — immediate safety emergency
11. "Fire broke out on construction site due to unsafe welding or electrical work" — emergency services required
12. "Contractor defrauded client of large advance payment and disappeared" — criminal fraud requiring police report
13. "Building built beyond approved floors (illegal extra story)" — structural and regulatory emergency
14. "Landslide or slope failure caused by excavation affecting neighboring properties" — emergency with potential casualties
15. "Worker suspected of sexual harassment on site — female worker targeted" — disciplinary and criminal matter

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Construction vs. Real Estate Sales:** Complaints about building quality during or after construction belong here. Complaints about property listings, misrepresented property prices, or rental agreements without construction involvement belong under Real Estate / Property Management.
- **Construction vs. Government Infrastructure:** Road construction or bridge projects funded and managed by government entities (TANROADS, municipal councils) may overlap with Government Services. Classify under Construction if the complaint is about contractor work quality, and under Government if it concerns tendering, procurement transparency, or public authority decisions.
- **Contractor vs. Developer:** A developer complaint is about the entity selling/delivering the finished property. A contractor complaint is about the entity performing the physical work. If both are the same entity, default to Construction classification.
- **Material Supplier vs. Contractor:** Complaints about materials purchased from a hardware shop or supplier (wrong grade of cement, defective rebar) should be classified under Retail/Building Materials, not Construction, unless the materials were supplied by the contractor as part of their contract scope.
- **Safety incident vs. General complaint:** Any mention of physical injury, structural collapse, or life risk must trigger Escalation classification regardless of the broader feedback type — do not downgrade these to routine complaints.
