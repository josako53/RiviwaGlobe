## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Logistics / Supply Chain & Delivery

### Technology & Tracking
- You should implement real-time GPS tracking for all vehicles so customers can monitor their cargo
- A dedicated customer portal with live tracking and document uploads would greatly improve transparency
- Send automated SMS updates at every milestone — arrival at port, clearance, dispatch, delivery
- Introduce an electronic proof of delivery system to avoid disputes about what was received
- A WhatsApp bot for tracking consignments would be very accessible for Tanzanian clients
- Consider integrating with TRA's TANCIS system for faster electronic customs submissions
- Digital document management would reduce the errors caused by manual paperwork

### Operations & Efficiency
- You should have a dedicated cold chain team with temperature logging equipment for every reefer movement
- Consider partnering with TAZARA for upcountry routes — it is cheaper and avoids highway congestion
- A 24-hour operations center would serve importers who have urgent customs deadlines
- Pre-clearance documentation review before goods arrive would reduce port delays significantly
- Invest in more trained clearing agents to handle peak periods like end-of-year or festive seasons
- Build a network of local agents in Mwanza, Arusha, and Mbeya for faster last-mile distribution
- A self-service scheduling tool for cargo pickups would reduce phone call volumes

### Pricing Transparency
- Please provide all-inclusive quotes upfront — hidden charges after delivery are very damaging to trust
- A clear rate sheet for standard routes on your website would help customers compare accurately
- Itemize every charge on the invoice with the regulatory reference where applicable
- Provide customers with a written quote confirmed before booking — not verbal estimates only

### Customer Service & Communication
- Assign a single account manager to each client so there is one point of contact for all queries
- Proactively notify clients of any delays or route changes as soon as they are known
- A callback system rather than a hold line would be much more customer-friendly
- Monthly shipment reports for regular clients would help with their supply chain planning

### Compliance & Standards
- Ensure all drivers have valid TANROADS licenses before assigning them to customer routes
- Provide mandatory anti-bribery training for all clearing agents at border crossings
- Implement cargo insurance as a default offering rather than an optional add-on

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. Perishable goods (fresh produce, pharmaceuticals, food) spoiled due to cold chain failure — financial and health implications
2. Cargo confirmed missing or stolen in transit with no trace of the driver or vehicle
3. Driver involved in a road accident causing fatalities or serious injury to third parties
4. Dangerous goods (hazardous materials, chemicals, flammables) transported without proper IATA/ADR certification
5. Evidence of bribery at TRA customs border crossing by a clearing agent acting on behalf of the company
6. Container seal number discrepancy indicating tampering or potential cargo theft
7. Port agent facilitated customs fraud — undervalued declarations confirmed by TRA investigation
8. Cargo of sensitive or controlled nature (medicines, chemicals, arms) missing from documented consignment
9. Cold chain vehicle carrying medical or vaccine cargo reported non-functional refrigeration unit during transit
10. Customer medical supplies or life-saving equipment stuck in customs causing a health emergency downstream
11. Major route accident blocking TANZAM highway with company cargo at risk of theft during the delay
12. Worker injury or fatality in warehouse due to unsafe forklift operations or loading equipment failure

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Logistics vs. Retail Delivery**: Retail KB (32) covers delivery by a retail brand's own riders. Logistics KB covers specialist freight, courier companies, clearing agents, and third-party logistics (3PL) providers.
- **Logistics vs. Government/Embassy**: Import documentation complaints involving TRA, permits, and customs duty are Logistics context. Visa or travel document complaints belong in Embassy KB (35).
- **Logistics vs. Finance/Banking**: Payment disputes with logistics companies (freight invoice payment) belong in Logistics. Payment disputes with mobile money or banks belong in Finance/Banking KB (23).
- **Logistics vs. Construction**: Equipment transportation for construction projects is a Logistics complaint. On-site equipment failures and construction quality issues belong in Construction KB (17).
- **Bodaboda delivery context**: When feedback mentions bodaboda riders for parcel delivery (not personal transportation), classify as Logistics. Personal ride-hailing via bodaboda is a different service category.
