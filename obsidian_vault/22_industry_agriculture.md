## Complaint / Grievance Signals — Agriculture / Agribusiness

### Seeds & Planting Materials

- The certified seeds you sold me were not genuine — I took them to the extension officer and she confirmed it
- I paid for hybrid maize seeds but the crop came out as an open-pollinated variety — I was cheated
- The seeds I received were not the variety I ordered — you sent me sunflower when I needed sesame
- The packaging was torn and the seeds had moisture damage inside — they were stored incorrectly
- Counterfeit seed bags are being sold openly at the market with your logo — your brand is being abused
- The seed germination rate on the bag says 95% but my trial plots showed barely 60% germination
- I bought certified cashew seedlings from your nursery but half of them died within two weeks of planting
- The vegetable seeds I planted did not produce the yield described on the packaging at all
- Your seeds expired last season but were still on the shelves of the agro-dealer — no one checked the dates

### Fertilizers & Agrochemicals
- The fertilizer I applied to my maize caused leaf burn — I followed the recommended dosage exactly
- I suspect the NPK fertilizer I bought is adulterated — the granules dissolve incorrectly and the crop shows no improvement

- The herbicide applicator showed me the wrong product for my crop type — he is not properly trained
- The insecticide I used following the label instructions did not control the fall armyworm at all
- Fertilizer bags from your distributor were found to be underweight — I weighed them and got 48kg instead of 50kg
- The agro-dealer sold me fertilizer with no label in Swahili — I could not read the application instructions
- The pesticide I bought has no expiry date printed on the label — is this a fake product?
- I used the recommended rate of your herbicide but it damaged the root system of my crop
- The storage conditions at your agro-dealer are poor — fertilizer is stored next to food products

### Crop Buying & Aggregation
- The crop buyer weighed my maize using a faulty scale and I lost over 100 kilograms in a single transaction

## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Agriculture / Agribusiness

### Input Supply & Quality
- You should introduce a seed batch tracking system so farmers can report germination failures against a specific lot number
- Please ensure agro-dealers in rural areas have updated price lists — the prices vary widely and farmers are being exploited
- It would help if fertilizer bags were labeled in Swahili with clear application instructions for each major crop
- Consider establishing quality testing kiosks at major rural markets so farmers can verify fertilizer quality before buying
- A counterfeit reporting hotline for seeds and agro-chemicals would protect farmers significantly
- Offer smaller pack sizes (2.5kg, 5kg) for pesticides so small-scale farmers can afford the right chemical

### Extension & Advisory Services
- Please use SMS-based advisory services to reach farmers who do not have smartphones or internet access
- Extension officers should be assigned to specific wards and be accountable — farmers should know who to call
- A seasonal advisory calendar specific to each region and crop would help farmers plan better
- Video training content in Swahili, downloadable for offline use, would reach more farmers in remote areas
- Please organize demonstrations at the farm level, not just at training centers — many farmers cannot travel far
- Partner with AMCOS and cooperatives to deliver extension services — they already have the community trust

### Market Access & Crop Buying
- Set up a transparent price discovery mechanism — farmers should know the floor price before harvest
- Please introduce digital weighing with a farmer-visible display so both parties see the same reading
- A mobile collection point schedule announced in advance would help farmers prepare their produce
- A contract farming model with clear written agreements and dispute resolution procedures would reduce exploitation
- Publish the grading standards used at collection points so farmers can self-assess before bringing produce

### Agri-Finance
- Offer agri-loans with repayment tied to the harvest cycle — not monthly installments that don't match farm income
- Create a group guarantee loan product for smallholder farmers who have no individual collateral
- A crop insurance product that covers all major risks (drought, flood, pest, disease) in one policy would simplify coverage
- Please make the TADB loan application process available at district level — traveling to Dar es Salaam is a barrier

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A farmer or family member suffered pesticide poisoning after using an agrochemical purchased from a licensed agro-dealer
2. A banned or restricted pesticide (by TPRI or TFDA) is being actively sold in the market
3. A disease outbreak (livestock or crop) was reported to an extension officer and no response was received within 48 hours, with risk of spreading to neighboring farms
4. Counterfeit or adulterated fertilizer or seeds are confirmed to have caused widespread crop failure affecting multiple farmers in a community
5. A cooperative official is suspected of financial fraud — misappropriating member funds, falsifying records, or unauthorized sale of collective produce
6. An irrigation infrastructure failure has resulted in flooding of farms or destruction of standing crops affecting multiple smallholder farmers
7. A crop buyer or aggregator has disappeared after taking possession of farmers' produce without payment — potential theft at scale
8. A farmer's land was seized or plowing was blocked by a third party citing a contract farming agreement the farmer did not consent to
9. A chemical spill or misapplication at a farm has contaminated a water source used by a community
10. A government subsidy program official is demanding bribes in exchange for voucher allocation
11. Aflatoxin contamination is reported in stored grain or animal feed distributed at scale — food safety emergency
12. A veterinary medicine suspected of being counterfeit caused mass livestock deaths in a community
13. A child or family member accidentally ingested pesticide stored improperly — requires immediate medical and regulatory response

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Agriculture vs. Food Processing / FMCG**: If a complaint is about a packaged food product (flour, packaged rice, cooking oil), route to FMCG/food retail KB. Agriculture KB covers raw/unprocessed farm produce, inputs (seeds, fertilizers), and farm-level processes. The signal is whether the issue occurred at the farm level or the consumer product level.
- **Agriculture vs. Utilities/Water**: Complaints about irrigation rely on water access, but if the core issue is government water supply infrastructure (canal management, government irrigation scheme), it may overlap with utilities KB. If the issue is about equipment sold or installed by an agribusiness company, it stays in agriculture KB.
- **Agriculture vs. Finance/Banking**: Agri-loans and crop insurance sit in agriculture KB when the complaint is about the agricultural use-case (harvest cycle mismatch, crop failure claims). Generic loan complaints (interest rates, hidden fees) with no agricultural specificity may route to finance/banking KB.
- **Livestock vs. Veterinary Services**: Animal health issues involving private veterinary clinics or government vets belong in agriculture KB. If the issue is a standalone private veterinary hospital charging for services, this may overlap with healthcare KB — distinguish by checking if the patient is a human or an animal.
- **Cooperatives vs. Financial Institutions**: AMCOS cooperative financial disputes (dividend sharing, savings) stay in agriculture KB. SACCOs providing loans to farmers overlap with finance KB — route by the primary subject of the complaint (agricultural input/output or financial service).
