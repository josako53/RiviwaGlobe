## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Real Estate / Property Management

### Rental & Leasing Improvements
- The property management company should provide residents with a digital app for logging maintenance requests
- All rental properties should be required to have a written inventory signed by both parties at move-in
- The company should establish a 24-hour emergency maintenance hotline for genuine property emergencies
- Lease agreements should be in both English and Swahili for tenants who are not English literate
- Management should send a monthly newsletter with updates on maintenance, accounts, and upcoming works
- Service charges should come with an itemised invoice so residents know exactly what they are paying for
- A resident committee should be formally established at our complex to hold management accountable
- The property company should use an escrow account for deposits so tenants are protected if the landlord defaults
- Property visits by management should be arranged at least 48 hours in advance by formal notification
- The landlord should be required to deposit a maintenance bond equal to one month's rent before a tenancy begins

### Development & Quality
- The developer should build a show unit that accurately represents the finished product before selling off-plan
- Construction materials used should be certified and the developer should provide a quality certificate upon handover
- The developer should maintain a snag list process for buyers to report defects within the first six months
- All gated communities should have sufficient visitor parking that is properly managed
- The complex should install solar water heaters to reduce electricity costs for all residents
- The developer should plant trees and maintain landscaping as promised in the marketing material
- Backup water storage tanks should be mandatory for any building above four storeys
- The building should have a functioning borehole as backup during DAWASCO water shortages

### Transaction & Agency Reform
- Estate agents should be required to display their registration licence number on all marketing materials
- A government-backed property escrow system would protect buyers paying deposits to developers
- The real estate industry association should publish a blacklist of unregistered brokers
- Property listing portals should verify ownership before allowing listings to be published
- Agents should be required by law to disclose dual agency in writing before accepting a commission on both sides
- A standardised tenancy agreement template should be published by MLHHSD for landlords to use

### Technology & Transparency
- All property transactions above TZS 50 million should be lodged electronically with the land registry
- The land registry should have an online portal where buyers can verify title status before purchase
- Property management companies should publish annual audited accounts for all managed properties on a resident portal
- A national property price index for Tanzania should be published quarterly to help buyers assess fair value
- Mobile valuation tools should be made available so that buyers can cross-check agent-quoted prices

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A tenant reporting a structural collapse or imminent building failure posing immediate danger to occupants — emergency evacuation required
2. A buyer who has made a full or substantial payment to a developer who has since ceased construction and cannot be contacted — potential fraud
3. A fire safety violation such as a blocked or locked fire escape in a residential building — life-safety emergency
4. A landlord forcibly evicting a tenant by changing locks, removing doors, or cutting utilities without a court order — unlawful eviction requiring legal escalation
5. A report of physical violence or threats by a landlord, property manager, or security guard against a tenant
6. Evidence of a fraudulent land sale where the same plot has been sold to multiple buyers — title fraud requiring police and land registry escalation
7. A tenant reporting the discovery of illegal electrical work or gas installation that poses an explosion or electrocution risk
8. A property developer who has collected payments from multiple buyers and shown signs of absconding — property fraud
9. A report that a property is being used for illegal activities (drug sale, criminal operation) and management refuses to act
10. Discovery that a property was sold without the consent of all legally registered co-owners — title fraud
11. A child or family being rendered homeless due to an illegal landlord eviction without court process
12. Evidence that a property agent is practising without registration — consumer protection breach requiring regulatory escalation

## Disambiguation Notes

- Real Estate feedback is distinguished from Government/Public Services feedback primarily by the presence of private-sector actors (developer, estate agent, property manager) versus government actors (land registry, MLHHSD, municipal council). However, title and land registry issues may span both — tag with both when the issue involves a private transaction AND a government processing delay.
- Real Estate maintenance complaints may look like general Facility Management or Construction feedback. Key distinguishing signals are tenancy relationship terms (landlord, tenant, deposit, lease, service charge) rather than project management terms.
- Property developer fraud complaints may overlap with Financial Services if the money is channelled through bank accounts or mobile wallets — look for developer names, construction sites, off-plan, and reservation payment keywords to classify as Real Estate.
- Rental pricing and deposit disputes should not be confused with general consumer pricing complaints. The presence of tenancy agreement, security deposit, rent review, or rent tribunal keywords confirms Real Estate classification.
- Mortgage-related complaints involving property as collateral may touch both Real Estate and Financial Services — if the primary issue is the property (valuation, title, foreclosure) classify as Real Estate; if the issue is the loan terms or bank conduct, classify as Financial Services.
