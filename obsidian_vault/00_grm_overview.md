# Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) — Platform Overview

## What is Riviwa?

Riviwa is a digital Grievance and Feedback Management (GRM) platform used by government agencies, development projects, NGOs, companies, and public institutions to receive, track, and resolve feedback from affected communities, customers, and stakeholders.

The platform supports World Bank-aligned GRM requirements for projects with social safeguards obligations (Environmental and Social Framework, ESF), as well as corporate customer feedback management for businesses and service providers.

## Purpose of a GRM

A Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) is a formal, structured process through which individuals and communities can:
- Report problems, harms, or concerns caused by a project or organisation
- Make suggestions to improve services or project delivery
- Express appreciation for good work
- Ask questions or seek information about services (Inquiry)

GRMs are required by the World Bank for all investment projects to ensure affected people have accessible, transparent, and fair channels to seek remedy.

## Who Uses Riviwa?

**Consumers / End Users (formerly PAP — Project Affected Persons):**
Individuals who submit feedback about projects or services. They may be residents near a construction site, customers of a business, beneficiaries of a government programme, or anyone affected by an organisation's activities.

**GRM Staff / Officers:**
Government or project staff who receive, review, assign, escalate, and resolve feedback. They have full access to the case management system.

**Organisations:**
Companies, government agencies, NGOs, and project implementing units (PIUs) that register on Riviwa to manage their feedback processes.

**Stakeholders:**
NGOs, community leaders, PAP representatives, and other parties who are formally engaged as part of a Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP).

## Key Concepts

- **Project**: A time-bound initiative (e.g. a road construction project, a water supply programme). Feedback is often linked to a specific project.
- **Organisation (Org)**: The institution owning or managing the project. Feedback can be at project level OR org level (about the organisation as a whole, a branch, service, or department).
- **Branch**: A geographic or functional sub-office of an organisation (e.g. TARURA Dar es Salaam Regional Office).
- **Department**: A functional unit within an organisation (e.g. Finance, HR, Customer Care, Engineering).
- **Service**: A specific service type offered by an organisation (e.g. road maintenance, water supply, health clinic services).
- **Feedback Reference**: Every submission gets a unique reference number (e.g. GRV-2026-0001, SGG-2026-0002, APP-2026-0003) for tracking.

## Tanzania GRM Context

In Tanzania, GRMs are particularly important for World Bank-funded infrastructure projects managed by:
- **TARURA** (Tanzania Rural and Urban Roads Agency)
- **TANROADS** (Tanzania National Roads Agency)
- **DAWASA/DAWASCO** (Water utilities)
- **TANESCO** (Electricity)
- **Local Government Authorities (LGAs)**

Projects often follow the **World Bank Environmental and Social Framework (ESF)** which requires a functioning GRM as a condition of project implementation.
