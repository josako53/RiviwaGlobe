### Scenario 32-1: Deceased Person Billed for Ankle Tag by Security Contractor (Pattern C+E)
**Real Basis:** UK Serious Fraud Office enforcement action July 2020; IBTimes UK investigative reporting; G4S Care and Justice Services billed Ministry of Justice for monitoring deceased, imprisoned, and released individuals 2005–2013; £38.5M fine + £108.9M repaid; individuals wrongly held for curfew violations had no direct dispute channel
**The Problem:** A woman's late husband died eight months ago. She is now receiving official-looking compliance notices from a government contractor referencing curfew conditions attached to her husband's electronic monitoring order. The notices carry government letterhead. She has called the contractor's helpline three times, each time reaching a general queue that cannot locate the case file, and has been told to "ignore it" and the call closed.
**What staff/org would do:** Advise her verbally that it is "a system error" and close the call without escalating to a contract manager, without flagging the record for cancellation, and without issuing written confirmation that no legal obligation exists — leaving live billing and compliance notices running on a deceased person's record indefinitely.
**What the Owner does:** Acknowledges how distressing it is to receive official enforcement notices while grieving. Confirms this is a documented systemic billing failure by the contractor — not a legal threat to her — and explains exactly what steps will close the record and stop the notices. Escalates the case to the Ministry of Justice directly rather than leaving her in the contractor's queue.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (legal risk to her is low, but distress is high and notices must be stopped in writing)
- Opening reply: "I'm so sorry you are dealing with this after losing your husband. These notices are the result of a serious administrative failure by the contractor — not a legal obligation on you. Let me explain what has happened and how to stop it permanently."
- Direct to: UK Ministry of Justice Electronic Monitoring Service directly (not the contractor); if notices continue, file a complaint with the Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman who handles government contract failures; the SFO consumer information line for context on the documented fraud pattern
- Evidence to preserve: All paper notices received with dates; a record of each call to the contractor's helpline including dates and what was said; certified copy of husband's death certificate; any confirmation reference numbers given during calls
- Feedback category tag: Financial / Administrative Fraud — Contractor Overreach

### Scenario 32-2: Airport Security Staff Files of Peruvian and Colombian Citizens Exposed Online (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** Securitas Security Services USA December 2024 data breach; SafetyDetectives and ClassActionU.org documentation confirming Securitas data leak exposed sensitive files belonging to citizens of Peru and Colombia including airport and security-related documents; SSNs and personal records also exposed in parallel domestic breach
**The Problem:** A Peruvian airport security official reads in a local news article that Securitas — which held a contract with his regional airport — exposed files in a data breach that includes staff background check data, access control records, and personal identification documents. He has received no notification. He calls the airport's security desk and is told "we are aware but have no information to share." He calls Securitas's international line and reaches a US-based operator who cannot locate international client files.
**What staff/org would do:** Refer him back to Securitas's main line, which has no dedicated breach response channel for international clients; the airport deflects to the contractor; the contractor cannot locate his records from a US routing queue; he is left with no confirmation of whether his data was exposed.
**What the Owner does:** Validates his concern — the Securitas breach is documented and real, and he has enforceable legal rights under Peruvian and Colombian data protection law to demand disclosure of what was accessed. Provides specific national regulatory bodies and the formal request he must send in writing to Securitas.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (breach has already occurred; urgency is asserting rights and monitoring for misuse)
- Opening reply: "Your concern is well-founded. This was a real, documented security breach affecting Securitas's international files. You have legal rights under your country's data protection law to know whether your personal information was exposed. Here is exactly what to do."
- Direct to: Peru — Autoridad Nacional de Protección de Datos Personales (ANPDP) to file a formal complaint and request enforcement; Colombia — Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC) data protection division; send a formal written data subject access request to Securitas's privacy team citing Peru Law No. 29733 or Colombia Law 1581 of 2012 as applicable
- Evidence to preserve: Screenshot or printed copy of news article; employment records confirming role and airport contract period during the breach window; any communications received (or documented non-receipt) from Securitas or the airport; dates of calls and names of anyone spoken to
- Feedback category tag: Data Breach — Cross-Border / International Notification Failure

### Scenario 32-3: Security Guard Fatally Shot Customer Inside Store — Family Cannot Get Any Information (Pattern A)
**Real Basis:** Cornerstone Security Group guard fatally shot a customer at a Lowe's store in Oregon USA (2021); jury awarded $21.25 million including $1.25M punitive damages (2024); case established both the store and contractor carried liability for failure to screen, train, and supervise (Negligent Security Attorney verdict database)
**The Problem:** A man's brother was shot and killed by a private security guard at a hardware store three weeks ago. The store's corporate customer service line told him "security matters are handled by the contractor, not us." The contractor's number goes to a voicemail box that has not returned his calls. He has received no incident report, no account of what happened, and no compassionate contact from either organisation.
**What staff/org would do:** Both the store and the security contractor deflect liability to each other, treat all inquiries as legal matters to be handled by their respective attorneys, and provide no information or direct contact to the bereaved family.
**What the Owner does:** Immediately acknowledges the gravity of the loss. Explains that both the store and the security contractor can carry joint liability for negligent hiring and supervision, and that the family has the right to demand an incident report, the guard's training and licensing records, and to pursue civil action against both parties simultaneously. Confirms the evidence preservation window is urgent.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (bereaved family with no information; evidence and witness memory degrades rapidly; potential statute of limitations considerations)
- Opening reply: "I am deeply sorry for the loss of your brother. You deserve answers, not to be passed between voicemail boxes. Both the store and the security company may share legal responsibility, and you have rights to obtain information and hold them accountable. Here is where to start."
- Direct to: Oregon Department of Public Safety Standards and Training (DPSST) — which licenses and disciplines security guards — to request the guard's licence status and any prior disciplinary record; Oregon State Police for the public incident report; Oregon State Bar Lawyer Referral Service for a negligent security attorney; if federal firearms violation is alleged, FBI tip line
- Evidence to preserve: Written log of all calls to both companies including dates and responses; names of any corporate representatives spoken to; the guard's name and badge number if known from any public source; store security contractor signage photographed; names of any witnesses present; security camera footage (send written preservation demand to store immediately)
- Feedback category tag: Excessive Force — Fatal Incident / Negligent Supervision

### Scenario 32-4: Security Officer Dismissed Hidden Camera Report as Soap Dispenser — Voyeurism Continued Five More Weeks (Pattern C)
**Real Basis:** Securitas security officer at Expedia's Seattle headquarters, December 4, 2023 — employee reported suspicious device under bathroom sink; officer dismissed it as "probably a component of the automatic soap dispenser"; no investigation launched; cameras operated five additional weeks until January 11, 2024; 60+ victims identified; perpetrator sentenced to four years; civil lawsuits filed against Securitas (Seattle Times; PCVA Law civil lawsuit filing; KOMO News; Milberg class action)
**The Problem:** An employee reported to an on-duty security officer that she found a small device attached underneath a bathroom sink. The officer dismissed her concern within minutes and sent her back to work. She has now learned from news coverage that hidden cameras were operating in that bathroom for weeks after she made her report. She contacts the security company to find out why her report was not acted on and whether she is among the victims.
**What staff/org would do:** Route her complaint to a general HR or facilities line; tell her the matter is "under investigation by law enforcement" and they "cannot comment on active legal matters"; provide no personal notification of whether her report was acted on or whether she is identified as a victim.
**What the Owner does:** Validates her distress and her rights. Confirms her report was documented and dismissed — that dismissal is at the centre of the civil lawsuit. Explains that she has the right to know whether she is identified as a victim in the criminal case and how to access victim services. Confirms she may have civil claims against the security contractor specifically for the failure to act on her report.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (victim identification window; civil claims have statutes of limitations; ongoing psychological harm if unaddressed)
- Opening reply: "What happened to you should never have happened. Your report was real, it was dismissed, and that dismissal had direct consequences for you and others. You have rights here, and I want to help you understand them and act on them right now."
- Direct to: King County Prosecuting Attorney's Office victim advocates — to confirm whether she is listed as a victim in the criminal case and to access victim services; PCVA Law or Milberg (class action counsel already representing victims in this case) for a civil claim assessment; Washington State Crime Victim Compensation Program for counselling and support costs
- Evidence to preserve: The exact date, time, and location of her December 4 report; the name or badge number of the security officer she spoke to; any written record she made immediately after (email to a colleague, text message, notes); news articles confirming the timeline of the device's operation
- Feedback category tag: Negligence — Complaint Dismissal / Privacy Violation

### Scenario 32-5: Black Woman Handcuffed and Strip-Searched in Store Back Room (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** Ashanae Davis, 20-year-old Black woman, accused of wearing stolen merchandise at Target in Southfield Michigan (2018); handcuffed and dragged through the store in front of customers; taken to a back room and ordered to lift her shirt and pull down her pants in front of a manager and officers; Target fired the security guard; NBC News / NBCBlk original reporting
**The Problem:** A young Black woman was accused by a store security guard of wearing stolen merchandise. The guard handcuffed her in front of other shoppers, dragged her to a back room, and told her to partially undress in front of a store manager. She was humiliated, traumatised, and released without any charge or evidence of wrongdoing. When she calls the store's corporate complaints line she is told to "send a letter to the store manager" — the same person who was present during the strip search.
**What staff/org would do:** Direct her back to the store where the incident occurred; treat it as a store-level customer service matter; offer no information on the guard's employment status, no corporate complaint pathway, and no acknowledgment that what occurred was a rights violation.
**What the Owner does:** Takes the complaint seriously at a corporate level. Explains that she cannot and should not be directed back to the store where this happened. Confirms that what she experienced constitutes a potential civil rights violation and an unlawful search, and directs her to the appropriate corporate, regulatory, and legal channels.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (immediate physical harm is past; rights protection and formal complaint are the priority)
- Opening reply: "What you experienced was wrong, and directing you back to the same store where it happened is also wrong. This is a corporate accountability matter and a potential civil rights violation. Here is how to formally escalate and what you are entitled to."
- Direct to: Michigan Department of Civil Rights (MDCR) for a state-level civil rights complaint; NAACP Legal Defense Fund or Michigan ACLU for civil rights legal representation; the company's corporate headquarters written complaints division directly (not the store); if she was physically restrained without cause, consider a police report for unlawful detention — this creates a formal record
- Evidence to preserve: Date, time, and full store address of the incident; names or badge numbers of the guard and any managers present; names of any witnesses; receipts or other proof that the item was legitimately hers; medical or psychological treatment records if sought; written record of the call to corporate including the advice to write to the store manager
- Feedback category tag: Racial Profiling — Unlawful Search / Humiliation

### Scenario 32-6: Security Company Refused to Provide Written Complaint Contact — Caller Trapped in Queue Indefinitely (Pattern A+C)
**Real Basis:** Better Business Bureau complaint database — Allied Universal Security Services, 2021–2023; documented BBB complaint narrative: representative refused to provide email address for written complaints and denied caller the ability to reach a supervisor; PissedConsumer.com 234 verified reviews; BBB profiles across Colorado, Pennsylvania, New York, Missouri showing same pattern
**The Problem:** A resident of an apartment complex managed by Allied Universal security is trying to file a formal complaint after a guard made a threatening remark and followed her through the building corridors. She has called Allied Universal's corporate number five times over two weeks. Each time she is promised a supervisor callback that never comes. On the fifth call the representative told her the company "does not provide email addresses for written complaints" and that she "cannot be transferred to a supervisor on this call." She has no case number, no written record, and no confirmation the complaint has been logged.
**What staff/org would do:** Continue the callback cycle indefinitely; treat her complaint as a branch-level matter even though the branch is the source of the problem; provide no escalation path, no reference number, and no timeline — effectively suppressing the complaint without formally rejecting it.
**What the Owner does:** Validates her frustration. Explains that refusing to provide a written complaint channel after this kind of incident is itself a failure of duty of care and, in several US states, a violation of consumer protection requirements. Provides concrete bypass routes that create a paper trail the company must respond to.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (verbal threat is ongoing concern — upgrade to urgent if threats escalate or become physical)
- Opening reply: "You are not wrong — a company refusing to give you a written complaint channel after an incident like this is a failure of their basic obligation to you. You have other routes that will create a record they cannot ignore. Let me walk you through them."
- Direct to: State security guard licensing body (Colorado: Division of Professions and Occupations; Pennsylvania: State Police Bureau of Records; New York: Secretary of State Division of Licensing Services; Missouri: Secretary of State); BBB formal complaint portal at bbb.org — this creates a mandatory written response obligation; if the guard's conduct met the threshold of threatening behaviour, file a police report to create an independent written record
- Evidence to preserve: Dates and times of all five calls and names of any representatives; a written description of exactly what the guard said and did, prepared as soon as possible; any witnesses in the building; a written request to the property manager for security camera footage before it is overwritten (typically 30–72 hours)
- Feedback category tag: Call Center Failure — Complaint Suppression / Guard Misconduct

### Scenario 32-7: Incarcerated Person Denied Insulin for Six Days — Family Cannot Get Information (Pattern E)
**Real Basis:** Jonathan Salada, Trousdale Turner Correctional Center (CoreCivic Tennessee); denied diabetes medication; lay in pain for three days; found unconscious and died at hospital; settled for $50,000; AP investigative report October 2024 (Fortune, Washington Times); DOJ opened formal civil rights investigation into Trousdale Turner August 2024; Tennessee fined CoreCivic $11.1M at that facility alone
**The Problem:** A woman's son is incarcerated at a private prison. For six days he has been telling medical staff he is in pain and cannot stand, and has not been given his prescribed insulin. She has called the facility three times: the first call ended with "medical matters are confidential," the second disconnected after a 40-minute hold, and the third call gave her "he was seen by a nurse" with no further detail. She believes he is in medical danger.
**What staff/org would do:** Continue citing blanket medical confidentiality; refuse to confirm medication status or escalate within the facility's medical contractor chain; treat family calls as administrative inquiries rather than welfare alerts.
**What the Owner does:** Treats this as a potential life-threatening medical emergency. Explains that withholding prescribed life-critical medication from an incarcerated person is a documented civil rights violation and that the family has immediate escalation routes that bypass the facility's own complaint chain.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=true (potential medical emergency; insulin deprivation for a diabetic person is life-threatening; documented pattern of medical neglect at this facility type)
- Opening reply: "This is a serious situation and your instinct to escalate urgently is right. Withholding insulin from a diabetic person is a medical emergency. You have legal rights to demand his welfare be addressed right now, and I can tell you exactly how."
- Direct to: Tennessee Department of Correction (TDOC) Offender Complaints hotline — the state oversight body, not the private contractor; DOJ Civil Rights Division (currently conducting a formal investigation into CoreCivic's Trousdale facility as of August 2024 — complaints can be submitted to doj.gov/civilrights); Tennessee ACLU or the national ACLU National Prison Project for emergency legal intervention; if immediate danger, the family attorney should seek an emergency court injunction for medical access
- Evidence to preserve: Dates and times of all calls to the facility; any reference numbers given; his prescribed medication name and dosage from prior medical records; the last confirmed date medication was dispensed (from any prior visit or communication); names of any facility staff mentioned during calls; written record of everything said on each call
- Feedback category tag: Institutional Abuse — Medical Neglect / Life-Critical Denial

### Scenario 32-8: Home Security Company Did Not Notify Customers After Their Home Addresses Were Leaked (Pattern D)
**Real Basis:** ADT SEC Form 8-K filing August 2024; SC Media and Malwarebytes reporting; threat actor "netnsher" posted 30,800 ADT customer records on a hacking forum including full home addresses and purchased security system details; ADT disclosed reactively via SEC filing after the hacking forum post, not through direct customer notification; class action filed alleging data stored unencrypted on internet-accessible networks
**The Problem:** A homeowner who subscribes to a home security monitoring service reads online that her provider suffered a data breach exposing customer home addresses and the specific security systems they use. She has received no notification from the company. When she calls customer service, the agent tells her "we cannot confirm whether your account was affected" and suggests she "monitor her account online." She is alarmed because knowing her home address and the exact security system she has installed gives a burglar a specific blueprint for defeating it.
**What staff/org would do:** Decline to confirm or deny individual account compromise; offer no specific remediation; redirect her to a self-service portal with no breach-specific lookup; close the call without escalating to a breach response team or offering any physical security advice.
**What the Owner does:** Confirms the breach is real and documented. Explains that the combination of home address and security system details is uniquely dangerous for a home security customer — this is not just a privacy breach but a direct physical safety risk. Provides specific immediate protective steps and the regulatory complaint routes available to her.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (breach has already occurred; physical risk is contingent but elevated — treat as high priority)
- Opening reply: "Your concern is completely valid, and this breach is unusual precisely because it affects your physical safety, not just your data. Let me explain what the company is legally required to do, what you can do right now to protect yourself, and where to file a complaint."
- Direct to: FTC complaint portal at ftc.gov/complaint for failure to provide proactive individual notification; applicable state Attorney General data breach complaint mechanism (California AG, New York AG, or relevant state office); request in writing from the company — citing applicable state breach notification law — confirmation of whether her specific account was in the exposed dataset; monitor class action investigations filed post-breach for eligibility
- Evidence to preserve: Account number and service address; date of sign-up and all purchased products on the account; screenshot of online coverage with dates; written log of the call including agent name or ID; documentation of any security system changes made in response; copy of any subsequent written correspondence from the company
- Feedback category tag: Data Breach — Physical Safety Risk / Notification Failure

### Scenario 32-9: Detained Immigrant Compelled to Work for One Dollar per Day (Pattern E)
**Real Basis:** GEO Group Northwest ICE Processing Center, Tacoma Washington; over 10,000 detained individuals paid $1 per day or given extra food for facility maintenance labor from 2005–2021; Washington State Attorney General filed suit; federal jury verdict $17.3M October 2021; Ninth Circuit upheld and increased to $23M August 2025; ICE oversight gaps in accountability for detained persons documented in parallel reviews (Washington State AG press release; Prison Legal News; Kingsley & Kingsley)
**The Problem:** A Guatemalan national is detained at a private ICE processing facility. He has been assigned to clean common areas and bathrooms and is paid one dollar per day. When he asked an officer why he was not receiving minimum wage he was shown a form he signed at intake — in English, which he does not read — and told participation is "voluntary." If he refuses he is told he may lose commissary privileges. He wants to know whether this is legal and what he can do about it.
**What staff/org would do:** Insist the program is voluntary, show him the intake form, provide no information about his legal rights, and offer no external complaint mechanism; imply that refusal has informal consequences for his daily conditions.
**What the Owner does:** Confirms that courts have already found this exact practice to violate state minimum wage law. Explains that the form he signed at intake does not override his legal wage rights, and that legal support is available to detained individuals even without US immigration status. Provides specific organisations that can represent him while he remains detained.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (ongoing labor rights violation; not acute physical danger, but rights are actively being denied each day)
- Opening reply: "What you are describing has already been ruled illegal by a federal court against this same type of facility. You are entitled to minimum wage for this work regardless of the form you signed at intake. Here is what you can do even while you are detained."
- Direct to: Northwest Immigrant Rights Project (NWIRP) — which provides direct legal representation to detained immigrants in Washington State and can be contacted by detained individuals; Washington State Attorney General's Office which won the $23M judgment and may accept new complaints; ACLU Immigrants' Rights Project; CIVIC (Community Initiatives for Visiting Immigrants in Confinement) hotline which accepts calls from detained persons
- Evidence to preserve: The exact name of the "voluntary work" program shown to him; copies of any documents signed at intake; a written log of work assignments, hours, and payments received; dates labor was performed; names of staff who confirmed the wage rate; any written communications from facility management about the program
- Feedback category tag: Labor Rights — Detained Persons / Institutional Wage Theft

### Scenario 32-10: Black Applicant Rejected for Security Role — No Internal Grievance Route Available (Pattern B)
**Real Basis:** U.S. Department of Labor OFCCP enforcement action October 20, 2023; G4S Secure Solutions (acquired by Allied Universal 2021) found to have systematically discriminated against 1,459 Black applicants for security officer positions in Houston Texas, January 2016–December 2017; 2,081 applicants additionally misclassified to exclude from analysis; $411,000 back wages; 28 applicants offered job opportunities; resolution came only through federal enforcement 6–7 years after discrimination occurred; no effective internal complaint channel existed for rejected applicants (DOL/OFCCP official press release)
**The Problem:** A Black man applied for a security officer position at a major security firm's Houston facility. He passed initial screening, met all stated qualifications, and had no criminal record. He received a form rejection letter. Two white colleagues with comparable backgrounds who applied at the same time were hired. He suspects racial discrimination. The company's website lists only a national recruiting line that confirms the rejection and offers no appeal or complaint mechanism.
**What staff/org would do:** Confirm the application was "not selected at this time," provide no reason, confirm no internal appeal process exists, and close the interaction — leaving him with no recourse other than multi-year federal regulatory enforcement.
**What the Owner does:** Validates his suspicion and explains that federal enforcement has already found this exact company committed systematic racial discrimination in this hiring process at this location. Explains that even if the formal conciliation period has closed, he retains individual rights and has specific time-sensitive channels to assert them.
**Riviwa AI instruction:**
- is_urgent=false (employment rights matter; no immediate physical danger; time limits on EEOC charges are the key urgency factor)
- Opening reply: "Your concern is well-founded. You should know that the federal government has already found this company discriminated against Black applicants in exactly this hiring process at this facility. You have individual rights, and some of them are time-sensitive. Here is what to do."
- Direct to: U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) — file a charge of discrimination within 300 days of the rejection letter (Texas is a deferral state; act quickly); Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP) — Allied Universal holds federal contracts and OFCCP has direct jurisdiction; Texas Workforce Commission Civil Rights Division for a parallel state complaint; employment discrimination attorney for an individual claim assessment independent of the federal process
- Evidence to preserve: A saved copy of the original job posting; the date of application and the date of the rejection letter; the rejection letter itself; any emails or messages with recruiters; documented information about the comparable white colleagues who were hired including their qualifications and application dates; the names of any hiring managers or HR contacts he interacted with
- Feedback category tag: Racial Discrimination — Hiring / Employment Rights
