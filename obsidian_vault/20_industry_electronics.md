## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Electronics & Technology Products

### Product Range & Sourcing
- You should stock more affordable tablets for students — there's a gap in that segment
- It would be helpful if you carried phones with dual SIM and long battery life — that's what most customers here need
- Please consider stocking more UPS units suited for Tanzania's frequent power cuts
- Your TV range stops at 55 inches — many customers are looking for 65-inch and above
- You should add solar-compatible home appliances to your range — demand is growing
- It would be great if you offered phones pre-loaded with Swahili interface — most customers prefer it
- Consider partnering with local artists for phone cover designs — customers love local themes
- Please include more energy-efficient appliances — electricity costs are high and customers want savings
- Your laptop selection should include more options with 4G LTE support — wifi is not reliable everywhere
- Add more locally compatible printers that work with power fluctuations common in Tanzania

### After-Sales & Repair Services
- You should offer same-day repair services for common issues like screen cracks and charging ports
- Please provide repair status updates via SMS or WhatsApp instead of making customers call daily
- Open a repair center in Dodoma or Mwanza — customers outside Dar es Salaam have no support options
- You should give customers a replacement device while their phone is being repaired
- Offer a home visit service for large appliance repairs like washing machines and fridges
- Please train your technicians on newer models — they seem unfamiliar with recent Samsung and iPhone software
- A pick-up and drop-off repair service would help working customers who cannot take time off
- Publish a clear price list for common repairs so customers are not surprised at collection

### Pricing & Payment Options
- Please offer installment payment plans — many customers cannot afford to pay for laptops or TVs upfront
- Consider accepting M-Pesa and Airtel Money at point of sale — not everyone carries cash
- A loyalty program where repeat customers earn discounts would keep people coming back
- Offer student discounts for laptops and tablets — there is a large student market near the university
- Bundle deals — phone plus genuine charger plus screen protector at a package price — would attract buyers
- Flash sales during public holidays would increase foot traffic

### Customer Experience
- Your shop should have a dedicated demo area where customers can try phones before buying
- Add clear price tags on all display items — asking staff every time is inconvenient
- Please organize your products by brand and price range — it's hard to compare when items are mixed
- A queue system with numbered tickets would reduce the chaos at the counter during busy hours
- Provide a charging station near the waiting area for customers whose phones die while waiting for repairs

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A charger, battery, or electrical component sold to a customer caused a fire or burn injury
2. A fridge freon/refrigerant leak has exposed household members to toxic gas
3. An air conditioner was installed with exposed wiring creating an electrocution risk
4. A power surge protector or UPS failed to protect devices and customer suffered total loss of equipment
5. A technician wiped customer data without consent and no backup was offered
6. A customer received a phone that was already registered under another person's identity (possible stolen device)
7. An electrical appliance purchased less than 30 days ago caused structural damage (e.g., fire, flooding from leaking AC)
8. A customer was sold a counterfeit product (fake charger, non-genuine battery) that caused device damage
9. A sold device is suspected of being stolen merchandise (missing IMEI, no documentation)
10. A refund of a significant sum (above TZS 500,000) has been pending for more than 30 days without resolution
11. A customer is threatening legal action or reporting to TCRA/TBS due to non-compliant product
12. A repair shop retained a customer's device for over 60 days without resolution or communication
13. A customer's personal data (photos, contacts, banking apps) was accessed or copied during repair without consent

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Electronics vs. Telecom/ISP**: A complaint about mobile network signal or internet speed belongs to the telecom industry KB, not electronics. Electronics complaints are about physical devices — hardware, software on a device, warranties. If a router is mentioned and the issue is about ISP speed, route to telecom. If the issue is the router device itself (not connecting, firmware failure), it stays in electronics.
- **Electronics vs. Electrical/Utilities**: Complaints about TANESCO power supply, meter tampering, or electricity billing belong to utilities. Electronics complaints involve devices affected BY power issues (surge damage, voltage incompatibility) but not the power supply itself.
- **Electronics vs. E-Commerce/Retail**: If the complaint is about delivery logistics, wrong item shipped from an online order, or payment gateway failure — and the electronics item is secondary — consider routing to retail/e-commerce KB. If the product itself (quality, defect, warranty) is the issue, electronics KB takes priority.
- **Repair Shops vs. Manufacturers**: Warranty complaints can involve either the brand/manufacturer or the authorized repair center. Entity extraction should identify which party is responsible. Phrases like "your shop" or "the technician you sent" point to the retailer; "the manufacturer's warranty" or "Samsung support" point to the brand.
- **Overlapping signals with appliances**: Home appliances (fridges, ACs, washing machines) overlap with a "home goods" or "appliances" industry. Default to Electronics KB when the product is electrical/electronic and the issue involves technical failure, software, or electronic components.
