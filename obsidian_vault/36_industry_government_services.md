## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Government / Public Services

### Digital Transformation & Efficiency
- The government should make all applications fully online to reduce queues at offices
- EGOV portal should be made mobile-friendly so citizens can apply via phone
- TRA should send automatic notifications when tax assessment is ready for review
- NIDA should allow ID status tracking via SMS so people do not have to travel to offices
- BRELA should issue digital certificates immediately after company registration is approved
- Huduma Centre should introduce an appointment booking system to manage queues
- The land registry should be fully digitised so property searches can be done online
- The ward office should have a public feedback screen showing complaint status
- Government services should accept mobile money payments (M-Pesa, Airtel Money, TigoPesa)
- RITA should provide online verification of birth and death certificates

### Service Improvement
- Government offices should publish clear service delivery timelines at the front desk
- Each ministry should have a public complaints register visible to citizens
- TRA staff should be trained to explain tax calculations to ordinary taxpayers
- The passport office should add evening hours to reduce daytime congestion
- District offices should have dedicated counters for senior citizens and people with disabilities
- TANESCO should send advance notice at least 24 hours before planned outages
- DAWASCO should publish a schedule for maintenance shutdowns in affected areas
- The municipal council should have a public app for reporting infrastructure faults
- Government tender results should be published on the PPRA website within five business days
- Civil servants should be required to respond to written complaints within 14 days

### Policy & Governance
- The government should establish an independent anti-corruption hotline for public servants
- Penalties for civil servants who demand bribes should be made public to deter others
- Local government budgets should be published and accessible to ward residents
- The government should decentralise more services to the ward level to reduce travel
- Citizens should have access to an ombudsman for disputes with government agencies
- Performance contracts for government officers should be linked to citizen satisfaction scores
- The government should simplify the business registration process for small traders
- Building permit requirements should be standardised across all municipal councils

### Infrastructure
- The ward should prioritise road maintenance contracts to local contractors for speed
- Install public Wi-Fi at Huduma Centres so citizens can complete online applications on-site
- The government hospital should have a working generator for power backup
- Public schools in rural areas need upgraded toilets and handwashing facilities
- Introduce a public suggestion box at every district office that is reviewed monthly

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. A civil servant explicitly demanding a bribe or cash payment in exchange for processing a government service — immediate anti-corruption escalation required
2. A citizen reporting physical assault or detention by a government officer during a service delivery interaction
3. A public infrastructure failure (bridge, building, water system) creating immediate risk to human life — emergency escalation
4. Evidence of fraudulent land title issuance or double allocation of the same plot — legal and regulatory breach
5. A government official threatening a citizen for filing a complaint — serious rights violation
6. TANESCO or DAWASCO disconnection affecting a health facility or critical public service institution
7. Corruption allegation in a government procurement process involving public funds above a material threshold
8. A minor or vulnerable person being denied legally mandated registration services (birth certificate, ID)
9. An applicant reporting that a government officer has confiscated their original documents without issuing a receipt
10. A report that public funds collected at a government office are not being remitted — possible embezzlement
11. A citizen detained at an immigration or police checkpoint who is claiming unlawful detention
12. A health safety violation at a public facility (hospital, school, market) posing immediate risk to life
13. Evidence of selective tender award that circumvents PPRA regulations — potential public funds misappropriation

## Disambiguation Notes

- Government/Public Services feedback is distinguished from Healthcare feedback by the presence of administrative keywords (NIDA, TRA, BRELA, land registry, municipal council) rather than clinical terms (diagnosis, treatment, medication). A complaint about a public hospital may be healthcare or government depending on whether the issue is clinical care or administrative service.
- Government feedback differs from Utilities feedback in that TANESCO and DAWASCO complaints about billing and connection are government-adjacent but the operational context (meter reading, outage, pipe burst) may lean toward utilities. Riviwa should tag these with both industry signals when ambiguous.
- Procurement and tendering complaints may overlap with Financial Services if the grievance involves payment fraud or bank guarantees — look for PPRA, LPO, and government contract keywords to disambiguate.
- Land and property complaints may belong to Real Estate or Government depending on whether the issue involves a private developer/agent (Real Estate) or the MLHHSD/land registry/council (Government). "Right of occupancy" and "land office" strongly signal Government.
- Civil service employment complaints (salary, promotion, disciplinary action) belong to Government Services even if they sound like general HR/labour feedback. Keywords: "civil servant," "public service," "ministry," "deployment letter" are distinguishing signals.
