## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Energy / Utilities & Water Services

### Infrastructure & Technology Improvements
- TANESCO should invest in underground cabling to reduce weather-related outages
- Smart meters should be rolled out so customers can monitor real-time consumption
- You should install backup generators or battery storage at critical substations
- The LUKU token system should be accessible via all mobile money platforms, not just M-Pesa
- Introduce a USSD code that allows instant load reporting and fault notifications
- Install water pressure gauges at key distribution nodes so leaks are detected faster
- Solar mini-grids should be expanded to cover all villages more than 5 km from the national grid
- TANESCO should publish a real-time outage map on their website and app
- Biogas digesters should be promoted for rural households alongside the national gas network
- Water storage tanks should be installed in each ward so supply disruptions are buffered

### Customer Service & Communication
- Send SMS alerts before scheduled outages so customers can prepare
- Create an online self-service portal where customers can track their billing history
- TANESCO should have a 24-hour customer support line that is actually answered
- Provide digital receipts for all payments made via mobile money
- Set up water kiosks in every 1 km radius in peri-urban areas
- Issue itemized bills that clearly explain all charges including taxes and levies
- Customers should be able to dispute bills online without visiting the office

### Policy & Regulatory Suggestions
- EWURA should enforce stricter SLA requirements on TANESCO for outage resolution times
- Renewable energy subsidies should be passed directly to low-income households
- Introduce a lifeline tariff for the poorest households who consume less than 50 kWh per month
- PAYGO solar contracts should be registered with EWURA so consumer rights are protected
- Water utilities should be required to test water quality and publish monthly reports
- Government should fast-track rural electrification under REA for all villages

### Environmental & Sustainability
- TANESCO should invest in hydropower maintenance to reduce dependence on expensive emergency power
- Waste management trucks should run on a published schedule so households can plan
- Encourage greywater recycling by offering reduced water tariffs to eco-friendly households
- Biogas should be promoted as a cooking alternative to reduce charcoal use in rural areas
- Solar water heaters should be made mandatory for new buildings to save grid electricity

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. Live electrical wire has fallen on a public road or in a residential compound — electrocution risk
2. Sparking or smoking transformer in a populated area — explosion/fire risk
3. Confirmed gas leak near residential buildings — fire and asphyxiation risk
4. Water supply confirmed contaminated with sewage or pathogens — public health emergency
5. Customer was electrocuted or severely injured due to utility infrastructure failure
6. Meter box caught fire inside a customer's home or premises
7. Solar or biogas installation causing structural damage or fire at customer property
8. Child or person drowned in an uncovered utility manhole or open drainage canal
9. Power outage affecting a hospital, school, or water pumping station for more than 48 hours
10. Utility company representative demanding illegal payment (bribe) for reconnection
11. Unexplained disappearance of large prepaid units from meter (possible meter fraud)
12. EWURA regulatory violation — utility disconnecting supply without the mandated notice period
13. Burst main pipe flooding a road, building, or causing structural damage
14. Mass contamination event — multiple households reporting illness from the water supply

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Energy vs. Telecommunications:** Both involve billing complaints and network outages, but energy complaints reference TANESCO, power cuts, voltage issues, and tokens; telecoms complaints reference data bundles, call drops, and SIM cards. "Mtandao hauna nguvu" is ambiguous — check for "umeme" vs. "signal/network" context.
- **Energy vs. Construction:** Electrical installation complaints during a building project belong to construction industry if the contractor is in scope; they belong to energy/utilities if TANESCO or a licensed utility is the service provider at the meter or distribution level.
- **Water Utilities vs. Public Health:** Water contamination complaints span both industries — classify as utilities when the primary actor is DAWASCO/DAWASA and the complaint is about supply quality; escalate to public health if illness or mass contamination is reported.
- **Solar (Retail) vs. Energy Utilities:** Complaints about solar panel products bought from a shop belong to electronics/retail; complaints about PAYGO solar contracts, mini-grids, or REA subsidy programs belong to energy/utilities.
- **LPG Gas vs. Mining/Petroleum:** Complaints about cooking gas cylinders and delivery belong to energy/utilities; complaints about upstream petroleum exploration or refinery operations belong to mining/petroleum sector.
