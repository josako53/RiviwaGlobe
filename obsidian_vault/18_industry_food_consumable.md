## Key Entities & Roles

**Staff Titles (Food Manufacturing):** Food Technologist, Quality Control Officer, Production Supervisor, Packaging Line Manager, Warehouse Manager, Cold Chain Supervisor, Sales Representative, Brand Manager, Nutritionist, Regulatory Affairs Officer

**Staff Titles (Restaurant/Food Service):** Head Chef, Sous Chef, Kitchen Staff, Waiter/Waitress, Restaurant Manager, Cashier, Delivery Rider, Catering Manager, Food Safety Inspector

**Regulatory Bodies (Tanzania):** Tanzania Food and Drugs Authority (TFDA) — now TMDA (Tanzania Medicines and Medical Devices Authority), NEMC (for environmental compliance in food processing), TBS (Tanzania Bureau of Standards — standards for food products), Municipal Health Authority (for restaurants and food handlers)

**Certifications & Standards:** TFDA Product Registration, Halal Certification (BAKWATA, JUMIKI, SHIMAS), TZS (Tanzania Standard), ISO 22000 (Food Safety Management), HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points), Nutritional labeling requirements, Batch number and traceability requirements

**Local Tanzanian Brands & Entities:** Azam Industries (Flour, Biscuits, Juices, Water, Ice Cream), Bakhresa Group (Azam brand), Tanzania Breweries Ltd (TBL — Safari Lager, Kilimanjaro), Serengeti Breweries, Sayona Drinks, Jibu (water), Bidco (cooking oil), Mo Fries, KFC Tanzania, Chicken Inn, Java House Tanzania, Steers, Barcelos, Café Kosmopolitan, Dar Fresh Bakery, Supermarkets (Shoprite, Carrefour, Game, Nakumatt)

**Key Terms:** Expiry Date (Tarehe ya Kumalizika), Best Before Date, Batch Number (Nambari ya Kundi), Lot Number, FIFO (First In First Out — stock rotation), Cold Chain, Shelf Life, Foreign Body Contamination, Food Poisoning (Sumu ya Chakula), Cross-Contamination, Allergen, Preservative, Additive, E-numbers, Nutritional Facts Panel, Serving Size, GDA (Guideline Daily Amount), Country of Origin

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. "Child was hospitalized after eating this product" — immediate product safety emergency and TFDA reportable incident
2. "Multiple people from the same group/family/event got food poisoning" — mass food poisoning outbreak requiring public health authority alert
3. "Foreign body found in food product — glass, metal, needle, blade" — product recall trigger and consumer safety emergency
4. "Botulism suspected — bloated or bulging canned food consumed" — life-threatening contamination requiring emergency notification
5. "Product found with fake/reapplied expiry date sticker (relabeled)" — consumer fraud and TFDA criminal violation
6. "Counterfeit version of a known brand suspected — packaging looks almost identical but different quality" — brand fraud and public safety risk
7. "Allergen not declared on label — consumer with known allergy suffered anaphylaxis" — legal and medical emergency
8. "Infant formula suspected of contamination — baby became ill" — highest priority escalation for child safety
9. "Product recalled by manufacturer — currently still on supermarket shelves" — urgent TFDA and retailer notification required
10. "Restaurant suspected of serving alcohol in non-alcoholic drink to an unknowing customer" — deception and potential harm
11. "Food handler tested positive for communicable disease (typhoid, cholera) and is still working" — public health authority alert
12. "Water source at food processing facility found to be contaminated" — production shutdown and health authority notification
13. "Undeclared controlled substance found in food or beverage product" — TFDA and police criminal matter
14. "Consumer collapsed after eating — possible toxic contamination" — emergency services and immediate product withdrawal
15. "Product batch believed to be the source of a regional outbreak of gastrointestinal illness" — multi-agency escalation

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Food Product vs. Pharmacy/Supplement:** Health supplements, vitamins, and meal replacement shakes occupy a gray area between food and pharmaceutical classification. If TFDA has classified the product as a medicine, classify under Pharmacy. If it is sold as a food supplement without medical claims, classify under Food & Consumable.
- **Restaurant vs. Supermarket/Retail:** Complaints about food bought in a supermarket and consumed at home (expiry, foreign body, taste) belong under Food & Consumable Products — manufacturer/brand focus. Complaints about the shopping experience, supermarket staff, or retail pricing belong under Retail. Complaints about food prepared and served at a restaurant belong under Food Service/Restaurant.
- **Catering vs. Restaurant:** Catering complaints (event food, institutional catering, school meals) share most signals with restaurant feedback. Classify together unless the feedback explicitly concerns a formal government contract (in which case Government/Procurement signals may appear).
- **Food Delivery App vs. Restaurant:** If the complaint is about the delivery service (lateness, wrong order, app malfunction), it may overlap with Logistics or Technology. If the complaint is about the food quality itself (cold, undercooked, wrong item), classify under Food & Consumable/Restaurant.
- **Price complaint vs. economic commentary:** "The price of cooking oil has gone up" as a general observation differs from "I was overcharged at checkout compared to the shelf price." The first is an economic observation; the second is a specific service complaint requiring action.
