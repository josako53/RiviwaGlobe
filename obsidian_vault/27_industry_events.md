## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Events / Entertainment & Hospitality

### Event Planning & Management Improvements
- Event companies should provide a detailed written contract covering all agreed items before taking deposits
- A dedicated event coordinator should be present on-site from setup to close
- Backup sound systems and generators should be mandatory for all outdoor events
- Wedding planners should provide a sample of materials before the event for client approval
- Caterers should always prepare 10% extra food quantity as a buffer
- Event companies should have a clear refund policy for cancellations stated upfront
- Provide clients with a detailed schedule of the event day including supplier arrival times
- Venue staff should undergo customer service training before any major event

### Hotel & Accommodation Improvements
- Hotels should send a confirmation message 24 hours before check-in with all details
- A digital room inspection form should be completed with the guest at check-in
- Hotels in tourist areas should have multilingual staff for English, French, and Chinese visitors
- Introduce a loyalty points program for returning hotel guests
- Resort websites should have live photo tours so guests know exactly what to expect
- Hotel should proactively inform guests of any facilities that are temporarily unavailable
- Provide a 24-hour front desk response guarantee for guest complaints
- Consider a late checkout option for guests who have late afternoon flights
- Game lodge websites should show realistic wildlife sighting probability by season

### Restaurant & Food Service Improvements
- Restaurants at tourist venues should display allergen information on menus
- Buffet stations should be continuously monitored and replenished throughout the event
- Catering companies should conduct a tasting session with the client two weeks before the event
- Introduce a QR code menu at restaurants to reduce physical contact and speed up ordering
- Offer Tanzanian local cuisine as an authentic option alongside international menus
- Train catering staff in food safety and hygiene as a compulsory requirement
- Consider pre-plated meals for large weddings to ensure consistent portion control

### Tourism & Safari Improvements
- Tour operators should provide clients with a clear code of conduct and cancellation policy
- TANAPA should update park fee payment systems to accept mobile money (M-Pesa, Airtel Money)
- Game drive vehicles should carry first aid kits and emergency communication devices
- Tour guides should pass a certified English proficiency test before licensing
- Introduce live tracking of safari vehicles so families can monitor their group
- Zanzibar beach operators should enforce life jacket wearing rules on all boat tours
- Lodge operators should publish a realistic description of facilities especially on remote properties
- Local tourism boards should create a verified star rating system for tour operators

### Conference & Exhibition Improvements
- DITF should open online pre-registration for exhibitors to confirm booth sizes digitally
- Conference venues should test all AV equipment 48 hours before events, not on the day
- Provide a quiet room for delegates to have private calls during multi-day conferences
- Exhibition booth assignments should be published two weeks in advance with a map
- Consider hybrid event options with streaming for international participants who cannot travel
- Hold a post-event debrief with event sponsors and exhibitors to collect structured feedback

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

- Food poisoning outbreak affecting multiple event guests — potential public health emergency requiring immediate medical response
- Structural failure (tent collapse, stage collapse) causing injury to guests or staff
- Fire breaking out at an event venue, hotel, or lodge with guests present
- Guest going missing during a safari, wilderness trek, or boat trip — search and rescue required
- Sexual assault or serious physical assault of a guest at a hotel, event, or tourist facility
- Safari vehicle breakdown in a remote park area with no mobile phone signal and medical emergency on board
- Child separated from family during a large public event or park visit
- Event payment fraud — company took full payment from multiple clients for the same date and disappeared
- Allergic reaction leading to anaphylaxis after food served at an event, with the caterer not disclosing allergens
- Guest drowning or near-drowning incident at a hotel pool, beach resort, or on a boat tour
- Illegal wildlife trade activity observed at or near a tour operator's lodge or camp
- Mass theft from hotel rooms during a single event — organized criminal activity suspected

## Disambiguation Notes

- Events complaints about **food served at a catered function** may resemble **Food / Restaurant** feedback — classify as Events if the primary complaint is about the catering service delivery (late, wrong quantity, wrong menu), and as Food / FMCG if it is purely about food product quality or safety in a standalone restaurant context.
- **Hotel accommodation** complaints overlap with **Real Estate / Property** feedback when the issue involves structural maintenance, noise, or construction — classify as Events/Hospitality if the customer is a paying guest (transactional stay), and Real Estate if it involves a long-term residential arrangement.
- **Tour operator and safari** feedback shares characteristics with **Transport** complaints when the primary issue is vehicle failure or driver conduct — classify as Events/Tourism if the safari experience as a whole is the subject, and Transport if the complaint is specifically about a vehicle hire company or public transport.
- Feedback mentioning **DITF, trade fairs, or conferences** belongs in Events even when it references product categories (agriculture, electronics, food) — the event management and venue experience is the industry anchor.
- **Wedding hall and venue booking** disputes may involve contract law and resemble **Legal / Consumer Protection** complaints — classify as Events and flag for escalation if a contractual breach or fraud is suspected, rather than reclassifying the industry.
