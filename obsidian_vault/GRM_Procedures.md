# GRM Procedures — Grievance Redress Mechanism

## Overview

The Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) is a structured process that allows Project Affected Persons (PAPs) and other stakeholders to submit, track, and resolve complaints, suggestions, and concerns related to World Bank-financed infrastructure projects in Tanzania.

## Submission Channels

PAPs may submit grievances through any of the following channels:

### SMS
Send a text message to the designated GRM hotline number. A reference number (e.g. GRV-2025-0042) will be sent back by SMS. Keep this number to track your grievance.

### WhatsApp
Send a message to the GRM WhatsApp number. Rivai (the AI assistant) will guide you through the submission process in Swahili or English. You can also send voice notes and photos.

### Mobile App (Riviwa App)
Download the Riviwa mobile application. Log in or register as a PAP. Submit a grievance from the home screen. Attach photos and your GPS location automatically.

### Web Portal
Visit the Riviwa web portal at app.riviwa.com. Anonymous submissions are allowed — you do not need to create an account. Fill in the grievance form and submit.

### In-Person (Walk-in)
Visit a GRM focal point office at your Ward, LGA PIU, or project site office. A GRM officer will record your grievance on your behalf. You will receive a written receipt with your reference number.

### Phone Call (IVR)
Call the GRM hotline. An automated voice system (IVR) will guide you through the process in Swahili. Your voice recording is transcribed and submitted as a grievance.

## GRM Hierarchy and Escalation Levels

Grievances are handled at progressively higher levels if they cannot be resolved at a lower level.

### Level 1 — Ward GRM Committee
- First point of contact for most community-level grievances
- Must acknowledge within 4 hours (Critical) or 48 hours (standard)
- Target resolution: 7 days
- Composition: Ward Executive Officer, GRM focal person, community representatives

### Level 2 — LGA Project Implementation Unit (PIU)
- Handles grievances unresolved at Ward level
- Handles land acquisition, compensation, and resettlement disputes
- Target resolution: 14 days from escalation

### Level 3 — Project Coordination Unit (PCU)
- Handles grievances unresolved at LGA PIU level
- Coordinates with TANROADS or TARURA depending on project type
- Target resolution: 21 days from escalation

### Level 4 — TARURA / WBCU / TANROADS
- Tanzania Rural and Urban Roads Agency (TARURA) for rural roads
- World Bank Country Unit (WBCU) liaison for national highway projects (TANROADS)
- Handles systemic or cross-cutting grievances
- Target resolution: 30 days from escalation

### Level 5 — TANROADS
- Tanzania National Roads Agency
- For national highway projects
- Handles complex technical or financial grievances

### Level 6 — World Bank
- Grievances that cannot be resolved domestically
- PAPs may contact the World Bank Inspection Panel
- Last resort — only after exhausting all lower levels

## SLA Targets by Priority

### Critical Priority
- Acknowledgment: within 4 hours
- Resolution target: within 72 hours (3 days)
- Examples: safety hazards, road completely blocked, risk to life

### High Priority
- Acknowledgment: within 8 hours
- Resolution target: within 168 hours (7 days)
- Examples: significant property damage, loss of livelihood, water supply disruption

### Medium Priority
- Acknowledgment: within 24 hours
- Resolution target: within 336 hours (14 days)
- Examples: construction noise, dust, temporary access restrictions

### Low Priority
- Acknowledgment: within 48 hours
- Resolution target: within 720 hours (30 days)
- Examples: general feedback, minor inconveniences, requests for information

## Grievance Categories

The following standard categories are used to classify grievances:

- **compensation** — disputes about compensation amounts or eligibility for affected assets
- **resettlement** — issues related to relocation of households or businesses
- **land_acquisition** — disputes about land takings, boundaries, or ownership
- **construction_impact** — damage caused by construction (roads, buildings, crops, utilities)
- **traffic** — road closures, detours, accidents caused by construction vehicles
- **worker_rights** — labor disputes, working conditions, non-payment of wages
- **safety_hazard** — dangerous construction practices, exposed trenches, unmarked hazards
- **environmental** — dust, noise, water contamination, waste disposal issues
- **community_relations** — disputes between contractor workers and community members
- **service_disruption** — interruption to water, electricity, or other utilities
- **information_request** — requests for project information or documents
- **suggestion** — constructive suggestions for project improvement
- **applause** — positive feedback and commendations for good work

## When to Escalate

A grievance must be escalated to the next level when:

1. The responsible level fails to acknowledge within the SLA acknowledgment window
2. The responsible level fails to resolve within the SLA resolution window
3. The grievant (PAP) formally appeals the resolution decision
4. The grievance involves allegations of fraud, corruption, or sexual harassment
5. The grievance has reached arbitration, mediation, or court proceedings
6. Multiple similar grievances suggest a systemic problem requiring higher-level intervention

## Grievance Lifecycle Statuses

- **submitted** — grievance received, not yet reviewed
- **acknowledged** — GRM officer has confirmed receipt and assigned a handler
- **in_review** — grievance is actively being investigated
- **escalated** — grievance elevated to a higher level in the hierarchy
- **resolved** — a resolution has been proposed and communicated to the grievant
- **closed** — grievant accepted the resolution, case is closed
- **dismissed** — grievance found to be outside scope or without merit (reason must be provided)

## Anonymity

PAPs may submit grievances anonymously. Anonymous grievances receive all standard protections — no retaliation is permitted. For compensation or resettlement grievances, the PAP must eventually identify themselves to receive any financial settlement.

## GRM Officer Responsibilities

GRM focal persons at Ward and LGA level must:
- Acknowledge all new grievances within SLA
- Assign grievances to the correct category and priority
- Record all actions taken in the Riviwa platform
- Escalate if SLA is approaching expiry without resolution
- Communicate resolution decisions to grievants in their language
- Never dismiss a grievance without a written, documented reason
