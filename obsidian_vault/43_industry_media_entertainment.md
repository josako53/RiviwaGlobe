## Suggestion / Advice Signals — Media / Entertainment & Creative Industries

### Content & Programming Improvements
- The national broadcaster TBC should produce more locally made documentaries about Tanzania's history
- Radio stations should dedicate at least 60% of their music airtime to Tanzanian artists
- TV stations should broadcast more agricultural and rural development content for farmers
- News programs should consistently fact-check stories before airing them
- The station should offer a live viewer Q&A segment at least once a week
- More programs in local languages such as Sukuma, Chagga, and Ha should be developed
- TBC should expand its digital streaming presence so diaspora can watch online
- Children's programming should be produced in Swahili by local content creators
- Introduce accessibility features — sign language interpreters for main news bulletins
- Documentary slots should feature investigative journalism on public interest issues

### Artiste & Creative Industry Support
- The government should increase COSOTA's enforcement capacity to protect artiste royalties
- Record labels should be required to register all contracts with TLS or a cultural authority
- A public fund should support emerging Tanzanian filmmakers to compete internationally
- Music education should be incorporated in secondary school curriculums
- Streaming platforms operating in Tanzania should be required to remit royalties locally
- A Tanzania Music Week should be officially gazetted to promote the industry
- A rating system for Bongo Movies should be introduced so audiences can make informed choices
- Local content production companies should receive tax incentives to invest in quality production
- Artiste welfare fund should be created to support musicians and performers in times of illness or emergency
- An accessible copyright registration portal should be launched by COSOTA so artistes can self-register online

### Regulation & Digital Media
- TCRA should establish clear guidelines for social media influencers and content creators
- Online news platforms should be required to follow the same editorial standards as print media
- Introduce a digital advertising standards board that reviews misleading online ads
- A clear takedown request process should exist for copyright violations on local platforms
- The government should pass a comprehensive digital content law covering streaming and OTT platforms
- Fake news penalties should be enforced consistently and publicly disclosed

### Advertising Industry Suggestions
- All advertising agencies should publish their standard rate cards for transparency
- Influencer marketing contracts should include content approval rights for the brand
- The advertising council should be revived and given regulatory power over digital ads
- A certification program for digital marketing professionals should be introduced in Tanzania

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. Broadcast of content that directly incites violence against a specific ethnic, religious, or political group — hate speech on air
2. Live broadcast of a suicide, graphic death, or violent crime without warning or cut-off — content causing severe psychological harm
3. A journalist has been arrested, physically threatened, or had equipment seized by security forces while on assignment
4. Publication or broadcast of a minor's identity in connection with a sexual offense — violation of child protection law
5. A media house is under political pressure to suppress a story in the public interest — press freedom violation
6. Verified deepfake video or fabricated audio of a public official being broadcast as genuine news
7. Sexual harassment allegation against a senior editorial figure at a media house involving a subordinate employee
8. Radio or TV broadcast advocating for voter suppression, election fraud, or violence during an election period
9. An artiste's master recordings are being held hostage by a record label and personal safety is being threatened
10. Content creator received credible death threats after publishing a public interest investigation
11. Illegal wiretapping or surveillance of a journalist by a government body — confirmed privacy violation
12. A media company is knowingly publishing CSAM (child sexual abuse material) in any format
13. TCRA enforcement action (shutdown/suspension) taken against a broadcaster without due process or notice

## Disambiguation Notes

- **Media vs. Telecommunications:** Complaints about TV signal, cable subscription, satellite dish installation, and set-top box service quality cross both industries. Classify as media/entertainment when the complaint targets content, programming, or the broadcaster; classify as telecommunications when the complaint targets signal infrastructure, subscription billing, or the ISP/network provider (e.g., TTCL, Azam Internet).
- **Media vs. Government Services:** Complaints involving state media (TBC) that relate to broadcast quality, editorial decisions, or content standards belong to media/entertainment; if the complaint is about procurement, public funding misuse, or government censorship as a policy matter, classify under government services.
- **Music/Artiste Complaints vs. Events/Entertainment Venues:** Complaints about a specific live performance quality or event organization belong to the events industry; complaints about royalties, copyright, recordings, and distribution agreements belong to media/entertainment.
- **Advertising Agency vs. Marketing Consultancy:** Complaints about creative content, campaign execution, or media placement belong to media/entertainment (advertising); complaints about business strategy, market research, or PR consulting without a creative production component belong to consultancy/business services.
- **Online Content Creators vs. E-commerce/Retail:** A content creator selling merchandise alongside their media work presents a disambiguation challenge — classify the complaint by its primary subject: if about content/copyright/platform, classify as media; if about product delivery, returns, or quality of goods sold, classify as retail/e-commerce.
