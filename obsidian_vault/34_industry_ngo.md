## Complaint / Grievance Signals — NGO / Non-Governmental Organization

### Beneficiary Exclusion & Selection
- I was excluded from the food distribution without any explanation or reason given
- The selection process for beneficiaries was unfair — certain households were favored
- My name was removed from the beneficiary list but I still meet all the criteria
- Community leaders are selecting only their relatives and political allies for assistance
- The targeting process was not transparent — nobody explained how people were chosen
- I live in the same village but I received nothing while my neighbors got assistance
- Disabled members of our community were overlooked during registration
- Women-headed households were not prioritized despite the program saying they would be
- Elderly people in our ward were not included and nobody conducted home visits to register them
- We were told only 50 households would benefit but 200 families need help — no clear selection criteria

### Aid Quality & Distribution
- The food rations distributed were insufficient for the size of our family
- The seeds provided for the farming program were of very poor quality — germination rate was terrible
- I received maize flour that was already going bad — it had a strange smell and colour
- The mosquito nets distributed have holes in them and are not functional
- Blankets given during the distribution were dirty and clearly used
- Cooking oil distributed smelled rancid and is not safe for consumption
- The sanitation kits provided were missing several essential items
- Medical supplies donated to our health post are expired and cannot be used
- The solar lamps provided stopped working within one month of distribution
- School materials given to children are of very poor quality compared to what the project reports claim

### Program Implementation & Project Management
- The project was supposed to last 18 months but was stopped after 6 months without explanation
- Activities promised during the community consultation were never implemented
- The training was conducted only once and had no follow-up or support afterwards
- Field officers visit only occasionally — the project targets say monthly community visits
- The project midterm review was conducted without including community members
- We have never been shown or explained the project budget — how are funds being used?
- The NGO made promises during the proposal phase that have not been kept
- Monitoring visits are only done when donors come — regular implementation is not supervised
- The exit strategy was not communicated — the project ended abruptly and left us with no support
- Gender-disaggregated data was collected but women were not included in decision-making

### Staff & Volunteer Conduct
- The field officer is rude to beneficiaries during home visits
- A staff member demanded money from a beneficiary in exchange for including them in the list
- Volunteers are not properly trained — they give incorrect information about the program
- The project coordinator is rarely available and does not respond to community complaints
- A staff member had a romantic relationship with a beneficiary which created conflict within the community
- Driver of the NGO vehicle was reckless and caused an accident on a community road
- Staff from the capital rarely interact with affected communities — they just collect data and leave
- A community health worker was distributing medicines without proper training or supervision
- An NGO staff member verbally abused a community elder during a feedback session
- The gender officer is male and beneficiaries feel uncomfortable reporting GBV to him

### Accountability & Transparency
- The organization never shared the results of the community survey with us
- We submitted feedback three months ago but have never received a response
- There is no complaint and feedback mechanism visible or accessible in our community
- We do not know who to contact to report problems with the project
- The information boards in the village are outdated — data from two years ago is still displayed
- Financial reports are submitted to donors but community members are never told how money was used
- The organization's local office closed without informing the community about the remaining project activities
- We were told the project had ended but donor reports show it is still being funded
- Community suggestions raised during quarterly meetings are never incorporated into project activities
- The NGO conducted a needs assessment but never shared findings or followed up with the community

### Donor Compliance & Reporting
- The NGO is claiming outputs in reports that the community has not seen on the ground
- The number of beneficiaries in the donor report is much higher than what was actually served
- Training attendance sheets were signed by people who did not attend the trainings
- The M&E officer visits once a year and counts outputs that don't match reality
- Environmental impact assessment promised in the proposal was never conducted
- Gender mainstreaming requirements in the donor agreement are not being implemented

### Registration & Legal Issues (Tanzania NGO Act)
- This organization has been operating in our district for two years with no visible registration certificate
- The NGO's annual report was not submitted to ANGOZA as required by law
- Foreign staff members are working without valid work permits
- The organization is using a different name in the community from what is on their NGO certificate
- A fundraising event was organized without the required permit from the relevant authority

## Suggestion / Advice Signals — NGO / Non-Governmental Organization

### Beneficiary Engagement & Accountability
- You should establish a formal feedback and complaints mechanism in every community you work in
- Consider posting project information in Kiswahili on village notice boards for broader community access
- Involve community members in the beneficiary selection process and document the criteria publicly
- Share project reports with the community — not just with donors — so everyone can hold you accountable
- Quarterly community forums would allow beneficiaries to raise concerns before they become bigger problems
- Consider a suggestion box system in each village or a toll-free hotline for anonymous complaints
- Beneficiary satisfaction surveys should be conducted and results shared openly

### Program Design & Delivery
- Livelihood programs should incorporate follow-up support for at least 12 months after training
- Training materials should be translated into local languages (Swahili and community dialect where needed)
- Target the most vulnerable — elderly, disabled, widowed women — and ensure home visits to register them
- Work with village governments to validate beneficiary lists before distribution
- Coordinate with government health and education departments to avoid duplication of effort
- The project exit strategy should be discussed with the community from the start — not at the end
- Include youth representatives in project steering committees — they are often excluded

### Staff Development & Conduct
- Mandatory code of conduct training for all staff, volunteers, and partners should be conducted annually
- A clear safeguarding policy against sexual exploitation and abuse (PSEA) should be posted in all offices
- Field officers should be required to speak the local language of the community they serve
- Provide staff with proper field equipment — motorcycles, first aid kits, communication devices

### Transparency & Reporting
- Please publish simplified project reports in Swahili for community distribution
- An independent community audit of project outputs would increase trust significantly
- Real-time project dashboards visible to community leaders would improve transparency
- Share budgets in simplified form so communities can understand how resources are allocated

### Technology & Innovation
- A mobile data collection system would reduce data entry errors and improve M&E quality
- SMS-based feedback channels would allow beneficiaries in remote areas to report issues
- Digital beneficiary ID cards would prevent duplicate registration across different programs

## Inquiry / Question Signals — NGO / Non-Governmental Organization

### Eligibility & Registration
- How does a household qualify to receive assistance from this project?
- When will registration of new beneficiaries begin?
- I was not included in the registration — is there a way to appeal?
- What criteria are used to determine who receives the cash transfer?
- Can a household be registered if they are not members of a community group?
- Is there a waiting list for households that did not qualify this cycle?
- I am a widow with young children — does that make me eligible for priority support?
- What documentation do I need to bring to register for the food distribution?
- We have a refugee family in our village — are they eligible for this program?

### Program Activities & Schedule
- When is the next distribution in our village?
- What is included in the nutrition kit we are supposed to receive?
- Will the training in January be held at the village office or at the sub-district level?
- How many cycles of support will this project provide?
- Is the cash transfer a one-time payment or will it continue monthly?
- When will the community health workers visit again?
- What are the graduation criteria for exiting the livelihood support program?
- Has this project been approved for an extension beyond December?

### Complaints & Accountability
- How do I report a problem with a staff member or volunteer from this organization?
- Is there a complaints box or hotline I can use to raise a concern anonymously?
- Who is the supervisor of the field officer working in our area?
- How long does it take for the organization to respond to a formal complaint?
- Is there an independent body I can contact if my complaint to the NGO is not resolved?
- Can I see the project proposal and the activities that were promised for our community?
- How do I get a copy of the beneficiary selection criteria used by this organization?

### Funding & Duration
- How long will this project continue in our community?
- Who is the donor funding this program?
- What will happen to the project when funding ends?
- Are there plans to hand over the program to the government or community?
- How much budget has been allocated for our district specifically?
- Is the organization registered with ANGOZA and can I verify that?

### Volunteer & Employment
- How can I volunteer with this organization?
- Are there paid positions available in this project for community members?
- What skills or qualifications are required to work as a field officer?
- Does the organization provide training for community volunteers?
- Are volunteers covered by insurance if they are injured during field activities?

- I am grateful for the school feeding program — my children now attend school every day
- The clean water project has eliminated the long journey our women made daily to the river
- The vocational training provided real skills that young people are using to earn a living
- Since the nutrition program started, we have seen much fewer cases of malnourished children
- The health awareness sessions genuinely improved our understanding of disease prevention

### Staff & Volunteer Recognition

- Your volunteers are committed and professional — they treat all community members with dignity
- The project coordinator listened carefully to our concerns and took action within one week
- The trainer was very patient and made sure everyone understood before moving to the next topic
- Your staff member was the first person to respond when our community faced the flooding emergency
- The gender officer successfully created a safe space for women to speak about their concerns

### Transparency & Accountability
- This is the first NGO that actually came back and shared the results of the survey with us
- We were given a clear explanation of who was selected and why — very fair and transparent process
- The complaint we raised last month was handled seriously and resolved — we are impressed
- Information about the project is clearly posted in the village — everyone knows what to expect

### Organizational Conduct

## Industry-Specific Escalation Triggers

1. Sexual exploitation or abuse (SEA) allegation against a staff member or volunteer targeting a beneficiary
2. Child safeguarding incident — abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a child beneficiary by a project actor
3. Beneficiary death linked to inadequate or negligent humanitarian response (food, medical supply failure)
4. Confirmed fraud in beneficiary lists — ghost beneficiaries or systemic exclusion driven by corruption
5. Expired medical supplies or contaminated food distributed to beneficiaries causing illness
6. Beneficiary forced to provide sexual favors or money in exchange for aid (transactional aid)
7. Gender-based violence incident occurring during a project activity or distribution event
8. NGO staff member confirmed working without legal work permit — immigration and legal violation
9. Organization operating without valid NGO Act registration or after certificate has been revoked
10. Whistleblower (staff or community member) reporting donor fund diversion with documented evidence
11. Community member physically harmed by NGO vehicle or field activity equipment
12. Refugee or displaced person denied access to protection services based on discriminatory grounds

## Disambiguation Notes

- **NGO vs. Government Services**: If feedback involves a government health clinic, school, or social welfare office directly, route to the relevant government service KB. NGO KB covers programs implemented by civil society on behalf of donors or communities, even when operating within government facilities.
- **NGO vs. Finance/Banking**: Cash transfer programs operated by NGOs are NGO context. The mobile money platform used to deliver the transfer (M-Pesa, NALA) is Finance/Banking KB (23) if the complaint is about the payment mechanism itself.
- **NGO vs. Healthcare**: An NGO running a health program is NGO context for program-level complaints (targeting, staff conduct, distribution). If the complaint is specifically about clinical medical negligence, cross-reference Healthcare KB (16).
- **NGO vs. Education**: NGO-run school feeding or scholarship programs are NGO context. Complaints about the school itself, teachers, or curriculum belong in Education KB (26).
- **Community vs. Beneficiary language**: Feedback using terms like "beneficiary," "distribution," "targeting," "logframe," "M&E," "UNHCR," "food ration," or "cash transfer" is strong NGO sector signal regardless of whether the submitter identifies themselves as a beneficiary or community member.
